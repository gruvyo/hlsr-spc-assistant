# Volunteer Handbook: Marketing & Publicity

**Quick answer:** Almost everything that carries the Show's name or logo needs prior approval. Committee logos, printed materials, merchandise, advertising, tickets used for promotion, and committee websites and social media all run through the Show's Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division (or the right department). Committees may only use private Facebook Groups, never public pages or other platforms. Send all media requests to Marisa Saenz at 713-819-4773 or msaenz@rodeohouston.com. Never put confidential Rodeo or volunteer information into an AI tool.

## What this is

The Marketing & Publicity section of the 2026 Volunteer Handbook. It sets the rules for how committees and individual volunteers may use the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo's trademarks (the "Marks"), how to handle media and advertising, what is allowed online and on social media, and how volunteers should use AI tools. The aim is a consistent visual identity and protection of the Show's brand and reputation.

## Protecting the Marks

The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo trademark properties (the "Marks") represent the Show's traditions, its volunteers, and its mission. To keep a strong visual identity, the Marks must be used the same way every time. They must use the proper colors, be in their proper proportions, and appear in an appropriate context. Unauthorized use is not allowed, and the Show will assert all legal rights, if necessary, to protect against infringement.

### Definition

Registered trademark, trademark, and copyright properties include all rights, title, and interest in the names, logos, slogans, trademarks, colors, emblems, mascots, and other proprietary rights of the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo. This includes:

- The official names "Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo," "Houston Livestock Show," "RODEOHOUSTON," "Rodeo Uncorked!" and "Go Tejano Day."
- The official "Bowlegged H," "RODEOHOUSTON," and "Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo" logos.
- All committee and subcommittee logos.

These properties, and any part of them, are the sole and exclusive property of the Show. Any unauthorized use infringes those exclusive ownership rights.

### General guidelines

- When you use the trademarked name more than once in one document, place the trademark symbol on the first use of the registered trademark or trademarked name only (for example, Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, then RODEOHOUSTON later in the same document).
- No organization, vendor, commercial exhibitor, donor, sponsor, or other entity may use the Show's name or logo, or associate with the Show in any promotional material, advertising, on-site booths, activities, and so on, without prior written approval from the Show's Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division.
- The official name "Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo," the official "Bowlegged H," or the official "RODEOHOUSTON" logos must appear on all items representing the organization. This includes, but is not limited to, committee logos, apparel, signs, invitations, flyers, merchandise, or any other identification.

## Committee use of Marks

Committee logos are the visual brand of the Show's most valuable resource, its workforce of more than 36,000 volunteers. Show officials take great pride in each committee, and these guidelines protect each committee's image. All committee logos should include the committee's full name, including the word "Committee."

- The name "Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo," or the "Bowlegged H" or "RODEOHOUSTON" Marks, must be used together with the Mark of any designated division, committee, or subcommittee that is promoting a project, event, or production of that division, committee, or subcommittee.
- Committee logos may not be used with any other logo without the written consent of the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division. They may not be trademarked, copyrighted, or owned by anyone other than the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo.
- Merchandise and other items meant for distribution to non-members of the committee should not carry the committee logo. The committee logo is reserved for volunteers only.

### Logo design (new and existing logos)

- All logos must include the words "Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo" or the "Bowlegged H" mark, to brand the committee as part of the Show. Show staff will help committees design and update logos.
- All new and revised committee logos must be designed by the Show's Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations department. A committee that wants a new logo or wants to update its current one should reach out to the Marketing department through its staff coordinator.
- New, approved logos must stay in place for a minimum of 3 years.
- Logos for subcommittees that include the words "Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo" or the "Bowlegged H" mark must be approved by the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division before each use. Approval lasts a minimum of 3 years. Committees or staff coordinators should track approvals and must give the Marketing department all logo files.
- When a committee logo changes, existing committee members cannot be required to buy vests, shirts, jackets, and so on with the new logo. New, incoming members should be required to buy identifying merchandise with the new logo. The old committee logo(s) may not be used on anything going forward.
- For help designing or redesigning a committee logo, contact the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division.

### Printed materials

- Use of the Marks by committees in printed materials must be approved in advance by the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division. This includes, but is not limited to, invitations, flyers, signs, programs, agendas, brochures, catalogs, and promotional items.
- Once a Mark's use has been approved, it can be reproduced or reprinted during the approved Show year as long as the approved scope does not change. Each printed item must be approved on an annual basis.

### Merchandise and licensed vendors

- Use of the Marks by committees to produce merchandise must be approved in advance by the Marketing department. This includes, but is not limited to, jackets, caps, shirts, badges, pins, buckles, and promotional and incentive gifts.
- Any company or individual that produces, manufactures, sells, or otherwise distributes merchandise bearing any Marks must be an approved Show licensed vendor with an executed trademark licensing agreement with the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo. To become an approved licensed vendor, a vendor should contact the Marketing Team. This applies even to vendors producing one-time and specialty gift items.
- A list of licensed vendors is on the membership portal volunteer account under "General Committee Announcements."

### Sponsors

Only official Show sponsors may use Show Marks together with the official Show sponsor's logo, emblem, name, or symbols for promotions and advertising, and then only with written permission from the Show's Sponsorship, Business and Corporate Development Department.

### Donors

- Donors for Show events and activities do NOT have logo rights. Their company logos cannot be used, and they cannot be acknowledged together with Show Marks by logo.
- Donors of Show events and activities, including committee events, may be acknowledged by name only (not logos). Acknowledgment may appear in printed materials such as event programs and on donor boards. The Show Marks may be used for name-only recognition. Acknowledgment is subject to sponsorship exclusivity provisions.
- Every instance of sponsor and donor acknowledgment, when used together with the Marks, must be approved in advance by the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division.

## Media and advertising

### Media as a promotional partner

- Each instance of cooperative promotion with a media outlet must be coordinated with the Show's Communications Department. The media must follow the Show's sign, logo, and presentation policies and procedures.
- The Show has no exclusive media sponsorships. It accepts and encourages coverage and promotional support from all media, even though some Show events may be sponsored by specific media outlets.
- The Show will not let any media outlet become the exclusive promotional partner of a committee fundraising event or off-site activity.
- Any media outlet that wants to become a promotional partner for a Show-wide fundraising event must work directly with an official Show sponsor. This applies to print, television, radio, and outdoor advertising, as well as commercial websites, mobile applications, and so on.

### Media relations

The Show's Communications Department keeps year-round relationships with media contacts. Send all media requests to Marisa Saenz, 713-819-4773, msaenz@rodeohouston.com, or the general media email, media@rodeohouston.com.

Media requests that should be forwarded include:

- Any media inquiries.
- If you are asked to speak to media on behalf of the Show.
- If you are asked to give an official statement or quote. In that case, say "let me find someone who can answer that question" and forward the request to Marisa Saenz.
- To get approval on media distribution lists and media advisories or press releases.
- To secure media personalities to emcee prominent committee events.

Follow Show policy: only designated Show officials are approved to speak for the Show.

### In a crisis situation

- In an emergency or crisis, the Show will deploy its safety and/or crisis communications plan.
- Only share information from the Show's official social media outlets and/or approved language from crisis communication plan notifications.

### Advertising (free or purchased)

- Committee volunteers may not solicit advertising or editorial placement without prior approval from the brand department. All advertising and editorial, whether print, web, broadcast, billboard, or any other, must be approved in advance and placed by the Show's Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division.
- Committee volunteers may not represent themselves as, or act as, agents or representatives of the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo to solicit advertising rates, complimentary advertising, editorial placement, and so on. There may be no implication of an offering of tickets, tables, and so on as trade for advertising or editorial placement.
- Scripts for public service announcements and/or advertising copy must be submitted to the Show's Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division for approval. This approval happens in a timely manner.
- Advertisements cannot be sold or included in programs or other committee publications as a benefit for a contribution, as a fundraiser, or for any other purpose. The only Show publication that includes advertising is the RODEOHOUSTON Souvenir Program.
- An ad is any acknowledgment that includes an address, phone numbers, email addresses, website references and/or URL, promotional tag lines, and/or products and services.

### Tickets used for promotional purposes

These guidelines cover all Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, RODEOHOUSTON, and committee event tickets.

- In most cases, only official Show sponsors will get permission to use Show tickets for promotional purposes.
- Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo tickets may not be used for advertising, promotion, or any other commercial purpose (including, but not limited to, contests, sweepstakes, and giveaways) without the prior written consent of the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division.
- Written permission will NOT be given to competitors of the Show's category-exclusive sponsors. (See the Donor and Sponsor Programs section.)

### Ticket use in committee fundraisers

- Committees that want to use Show tickets with fundraising events should request approval to use tickets for promotion in their request for event approval.
- Ticket donors used in approved events may be recognized, as long as the recognition follows the policies and procedures in this handbook.
- Ticket donors may not be recognized in brochures, posters, or any form of advertising without the prior written consent of the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division.

## Online and social media guidelines

### Committee and individual member websites

- Committees and members conducting committee business (including, but not limited to, selling advertising, tickets, or auction animals) may have websites for internal committee functions that are not currently available through the Show's member and volunteer services. Selling advertising space on the site, or running advertisements for services or items, is not permitted.
- Internal committee websites should be accessible by login only, to protect member information and privacy, and MUST NOT be accessible to the public.
- These internal websites cannot serve as informational websites for the Show or the committee, because that information is updated regularly at rodeohouston.com.
- Any website created for committee use should use generic login information that can be passed to the responsible committee representative as leadership changes. No one person may own the website.
- All internal websites must comply with applicable law. All internal websites must be disclosed to the digital media and IT departments with prior approval from both parties.

### Social media for committee use

- Committees are only permitted to have private Facebook Groups for their social media communication. Only current committee members should be in the Facebook Group.
- Public committee pages or accounts are NOT allowed. Instagram, X, TikTok, or any other social media platform accounts, private or public, are not permitted under any Rodeo affiliation or committee name. Any unapproved accounts on these platforms will be requested for immediate deletion.
- Any Facebook Group created for committee use should use Show-accessible login information that is updated with each leadership or account admin change. No one person may own the social media account.

All committee social media accounts must follow these rules:

- The committee's chairman, or a specific designee, must approve group members and followers.
- The committee's staff coordinator and the Show's social media coordinator must have administrator access to the committee's social media group. Send the link and login information to your staff coordinator and allow membership upon request.
- Content should be relevant to the committee, the committee's mission with the Show, Show information, committee events, and so on.
- Group administrators may remove any content deemed inappropriate. To help prevent irrelevant content in a closed Facebook Group, administrators may set the group so all posts must be approved before they post.
- To avoid inappropriate content, members should refrain from posting:
  - Personal requests and posts that are not relevant to the committee.
  - Posts with a revenue-generating purpose for personal gain.
  - Promotion of other charity events.
  - Conversation that is political in nature.
- Excessive alcohol shown in photographs, videos, graphics, memes, links, and so on should not be shared.

### Social media guidelines for volunteers

As a representative of the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo (HLSR), your online presence helps uphold the organization's values and reputation. If you post content where you are visibly affiliated with HLSR (such as wearing an HLSR vest, displaying a gold badge, verbally identifying yourself as a volunteer, or tagging HLSR or an HLSR committee), follow these guidelines:

- Do not use profanity, abusive or derogatory language, or exhibit inappropriate behavior.
- Do not use controlled substances or appear under the influence of alcohol or other controlled substances.
- Do not engage in sexual harassment or other misconduct.
- Keep in mind that gold badges should not be used solely to access the grounds for personal social media content and account growth.

HLSR reserves the right to request removal of any social media content it deems inappropriate or misaligned with the organization's values and standards. Failing to follow these guidelines may result in disciplinary action, up to and including removal from your committee.

### Photography and videography guidelines for volunteers

To protect the safety, security, and integrity of the Show, these rules apply to all volunteer photography and videography:

- Volunteers are not permitted on the dirt inside Reliant Stadium to take photographs or videos.
- Camera lenses must be no longer than 3 inches. Professional cameras and lenses longer than that are prohibited.
- Access to credentialed areas (including behind-the-scenes locations, restricted zones, or performance areas) for photography and videography requires prior approval from HLSR staff.
- Any photography or videography that volunteers capture intending to publish externally, specifically media submissions for organizational promotions, must be reviewed and approved by HLSR before use.
- Personal photography and videography for private use (for example, personal social media or keepsakes) is allowed in public, non-restricted areas, as long as it complies with all HLSR guidelines and does not disrupt guests, participants, or operations.

## Show social media policy

Social media tools are a powerful form of communication that can significantly affect organizational, professional, and individual reputations. Forms of social media include, but are not limited to, YouTube, Facebook, Snapchat, LinkedIn, Instagram, Pinterest, X, TikTok, Tumblr, Flickr, Reddit, and WhatsApp, as well as personal websites and blogs.

Show representatives must take care with social media, because the lines between personal and professional content, lawful and unlawful, and public and private content are often blurred. Whether participating on behalf of the Show or personally, representatives should follow the same standards of behavior online as they would in person. The Internet is not anonymous, and information published on it will very likely be around for a long time, so all content should be carefully considered before posting. Violations of this policy may result in corrective action and in removal from volunteer status.

### Take responsibility and be transparent

- You are responsible for anything you write or post online.
- Use good judgment when you post and think about the consequences. Assume your writings will spread.

### Show policies extend to social media activities

- Make sure communications and postings do not violate any policy in the Volunteer Handbook. The Show's policies prohibiting sexual harassment and other misconduct apply equally to social media.
- Do not express, communicate, or link to comments that are vulgar, obscene, threatening, pornographic, harassing, or defamatory, or that violate the Show's policies against retaliation, discrimination, hostility, or harassment based on age, race, religion, sex, ethnicity, nationality, disability, or other protected class, status, or characteristic.

### Testimonials and endorsements

The Marketing, Public Relations, and Presentations Division is responsible for marketing the Show. The Federal Trade Commission has detailed regulations (16 CFR Part 255.5) about what must and must not be disclosed in testimonials and endorsements, including those made on social media, and the Show prohibits any social media posts about the Show that do not comply with the FTC's mandate.

Testimonials or endorsements about the Show should be avoided, unless posted to the Show's official social media platforms by the Marketing, Public Relations, and Presentations Division for promotional and engagement purposes. If a Show representative uses social media for any testimonial or endorsement of the Show, the individual must clearly and conspicuously disclose his or her volunteer relationship to readers. The suggested disclaimer is: "The postings on this site are my own and do not represent the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo's positions, strategies or opinions."

### Protect intellectual property and privacy rights

- The Show's success depends on its confidential or proprietary information and intellectual rights staying protected. Representatives may not post any information on social media that would reveal confidential business plans, initiatives, or trade secrets, or that would compromise the Show's intellectual property rights in any way.
- In line with state and federal law, representatives may not post or share confidential personal information about volunteers, employees, sponsors, or vendors. This includes, but is not limited to, social security numbers, tax ID numbers, addresses, birth dates, protected health information, credit card numbers, and driver's license numbers.
- All representatives are expected to follow copyright laws. They may not use any Show, sponsor, or vendor trademarks or logos without the owner's express written permission. The only exception is if the image is shared from the Show's official social media platforms and clearly stated as such (for example, via @RODEOHOUSTON, or "sharing" an image, video, or post on Facebook).
- Social media is not for everyone, and many people choose not to socialize with peers online. Representatives are not required to connect with any volunteer or Show employee, or to "friend" them on social media. Representatives may not threaten other volunteers or Show employees to coerce them into social media activities.

### Be wary

- People who communicate online may not be who they claim to be. They may be associated with competitors, the media, regulators, or others seeking "insider information" about the Show.
- Everything written online can be traced to its author, and information is often repeated and linked to other sources. An incidental comment can go viral at any time. There are also countless viruses and spyware on the internet with clever ways to infect computers. Be careful, and report any unusual behavior on company computers to the staff or managing coordinator and/or officer-in-charge.
- For security, all employees, representatives, and official accounts must enable and maintain two-factor authentication (2FA) for social media accounts and websites associated with the organization. 2FA is an extra layer of security that requires two forms of identification or verification to access an account. It usually combines something the user knows (like a password) with something the user has (such as a unique code from an authentication app or sent via SMS).
- Be cautious when sharing personal information on social media. If you or someone else enters a contest or giveaway, always verify that the promotion is run through an official RODEOHOUSTON account.

If a social media group or website is compromised, contact your staff coordinator and the social media coordinator immediately.

### Obligation to report violations

If you become aware of a violation of this policy, immediately report it to an appropriate staff coordinator, management coordinator, or the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division. If a volunteer feels harassed, discriminated against, or retaliated against for reporting a violation, that person should immediately report it to the same personnel.

### No expectation of privacy

Volunteers should have no expectation of privacy when using the Internet, including social networking sites, during working time or when using Show equipment, in accordance with the Show's Computer, Email and Internet Usage Policy.

### Criminal and civil liability

Volunteers who post information may make themselves liable for defamation, violation of privacy rights, trade secrets, other civil damages, or criminal penalties. It is difficult to anticipate every way a seemingly innocent publication could become a serious legal matter. Even when unauthorized, postings by volunteers may create potential criminal or civil liability for the Show. If that is the case, the Show reserves the right to demand the posting be removed.

### Non-interference with applicable laws

This policy is not intended to restrict or interfere with any volunteer's federal or state labor law rights, including rights under the National Labor Relations Act or any whistleblower protections under federal or state law.

### Questions about this policy

Contact the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division with any questions about this policy.

## Policy for artificial intelligence usage

HLSR recognizes the potential benefits of AI applications, such as Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Transcend, or other similar tools that are or may become available, to improve efficiency and productivity. It also acknowledges the potential challenges and risks of AI and the importance of using it responsibly. This policy guides volunteers to use AI ethically and in compliance with current applicable laws, regulations, and HLSR policies.

### Guidelines for AI-generated content

Never share proprietary or confidential information about the Rodeo or any individual in an AI tool. Information may enter the public domain now or in the future. AI tools are not legal entities and are not bound by the same confidentiality agreements or legal protections.

- AI-generated ads and graphics must be reviewed and approved by the HLSR Marketing Department before sharing.
- Comply with HLSR's confidentiality, privacy, and data protection requirements.
- Do not give AI any sensitive committee or volunteer-specific information.
- Do not give AI any sensitive or proprietary HLSR information such as financials, safety statistics, or any non-publicly available information.
- Protect HLSR's intellectual property and respect others' intellectual property.
- Follow copyright laws when using AI. It is prohibited to use AI to generate content that infringes on others' intellectual property rights, including but not limited to copyrighted material.
- AI does not replace a person's creativity, critical thinking, or judgment.
- Fact-check AI-generated content to confirm all information is accurate and current.
- Independently review and verify sources, check statistics, and validate any claims before finalizing.
- Make sure the privacy of HLSR volunteers, clients, or any other third parties is maintained when using AI tools, in line with applicable legislation and HLSR's policies.
- Use AI ethically and in compliance with current applicable laws, regulations, and company policies. Volunteers must not use AI to generate discriminatory, offensive, or inappropriate content.

## FAQ

**Can our committee start an Instagram or TikTok account?**
No. Committees are only allowed to use private Facebook Groups. Public committee pages, and Instagram, X, TikTok, or any other platform accounts (private or public), are not permitted under any Rodeo affiliation or committee name. Unapproved accounts will be requested for immediate deletion.

**A reporter contacted me for a quote about the Show. What do I do?**
Only designated Show officials may speak for the Show. Say "let me find someone who can answer that question" and forward the request to Marisa Saenz at 713-819-4773 or msaenz@rodeohouston.com. The general media email is media@rodeohouston.com.

**We want a new committee logo. How do we get one?**
All new and revised committee logos must be designed by the Show's Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations department. Reach out to the Marketing department through your staff coordinator. The logo must include "Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo" or the "Bowlegged H" mark, and once approved it must stay in place for at least 3 years.

**Can we sell ads in our committee program to raise money?**
No. Ads cannot be sold or included in programs or other committee publications as a benefit for a contribution, as a fundraiser, or for any other purpose. The only Show publication that includes advertising is the RODEOHOUSTON Souvenir Program.

**Can I take photos on the field at the stadium for our committee's social media?**
No. Volunteers are not permitted on the dirt inside Reliant Stadium to take photos or video. Camera lenses must be no longer than 3 inches, credentialed areas need prior staff approval, and anything intended for external publication must be reviewed and approved by HLSR first.

**Is it OK to use ChatGPT or Copilot for committee work?**
Yes, with limits. Never put proprietary or confidential Rodeo or individual information into an AI tool, including financials, safety statistics, or any non-public information. AI-generated ads and graphics must be approved by the Marketing Department before sharing, and you must fact-check and verify anything AI produces before finalizing it.

**Can a donor's company logo appear next to the Show's logo on our event program?**
No. Donors do not have logo rights. Donors may be acknowledged by name only, in printed materials like event programs and on donor boards, and every acknowledgment used with the Marks must be approved in advance by the Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Division. Only official Show sponsors may use their logo with Show Marks.
