# How the Souvenir Program Committee works: roles, process, and customs

**Quick answer:** The Souvenir Program Committee (SPC) is one of HLSR's original committees. It raises money for the Show's educational scholarship fund by selling souvenir program ads and collecting donations. Members work their own contacts rather than scheduled shifts, organize into numbered teams under captains, and earn a badge each season by paying dues, signing an indemnity form, and hitting a sales threshold. The committee has its own leadership, sub-committees, and a calendar of traditions like the Kick-Off Crawfish Boil and Rodeo Prom.

## What this is

This doc is synthesized from The Heartbeat, the committee's newsletter, across its 2014 to 2024 run. It pulls together the durable facts about how the committee is structured and run, the things that have held steady year to year. Every fact below cites the issue it came from. Where a detail changed over the years, the most recent version is given and the change is noted. Anything tied to a single season's schedule or news has been left out, since this is meant to stay current.

## History and mission

The SPC was one of HLSR's original four committees, created a few years after the start of the Houston Fat Stock Show and Livestock Exposition, which makes it one of the oldest committees in the Show (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014; Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024). It originally had a separate men's committee and women's committee, which merged into a single committee in the 1970s (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024; Vol. 7.1, Jun 2020).

The committee's mission is to solicit ads and donations for the HLSR Souvenir Program, with all the money going to the Show's educational scholarship fund after the program is produced (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.6, Jan 2024; Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024). Those scholarship dollars help 4-H and FFA students across Texas attend college and pursue agricultural careers (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.1, Jul 2019). The program book itself is sold at the Rodeo by participating area high school FFA and 4-H groups, who keep all the proceeds from their own program sales for the nights they are out there (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.8, May 2017; Vol. 5.1, Jun 2018).

The newsletter is named The Heartbeat after HLSR's longstanding description as the Heartbeat of the Southwest (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014).

The committee has a member capacity cap of 500 (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024; Vol. 7.1, Jun 2020).

## Leadership and structure

The committee is led by a management team: an Officer in Charge, a Committee Chairman, and Vice Chairmen split by function (Sales, Recruitment, Communications, Administration, Food & Banquet, Retention, Awards, Program Sales, and Life Time) (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.3, Aug 2016). The Officer in Charge is an HLSR leadership role that turns over periodically and serves a three-year term (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.1, Jun 2018; Vol. 2.1, Jul 2015). The Chairman serves a multi-year term as well (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019).

Below the management team, members are organized into numbered teams, each led by a Team Captain and one or more Assistant Captains (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.3, Aug 2016; Vol. 3.2, Aug 2016). Lifetime committee members form their own dedicated team (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.3, Aug 2016). The committee sells its program advertising through these team structures (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.6, Oct 2018).

Each committee also has a staff coordinator, the HLSR employee who produces the Souvenir Program for the committee (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019).

The committee runs at least one sub-committee devoted to physically distributing the souvenir program to the youth of Texas on NRG grounds during the Rodeo season (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024; Vol. 7.1, Jun 2020). The Program Sales subcommittee coordinates the FFA and 4-H schools and supervises onsite program sales during the Show days, with members acting as SPC representatives and supervisors of the FFA/4-H teams (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.9, May 2019).

## Joining and recruiting

Joining is a two-step process. A person first becomes an official member of HLSR, selecting the Souvenir Program Committee and paying Rodeo membership dues, then, after committee approval off the wait list, goes back online and pays the separate committee dues (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.2, Aug 2014; Vol. 4.1, Jul 2017). New members are called rookies and attend a Rookie Orientation as part of joining (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.1, Jun 2016).

To recruit, the committee directs prospective members to its website, rodeospc.com, which acts as a brochure on who the committee is and what it does, and invites them to Rookie Orientation meetings to get their questions answered before committing (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.2, Jul 2023). Members track their recruits in the SPC dashboard at rodeospc.com, which adds each recruit to a recruit-and-rookie email list for rookie events, trainings, and meetings (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.1, May 2023). Members also upload prospective recruits' contact information into the dashboard so the Recruiting team can follow up (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.2, Jul 2020).

## Dues and badging

Members pay two separate dues each season: Show dues paid to HLSR and Committee dues paid to the SPC (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.3, Aug 2019). Show dues are $50 a year or $500 for a Lifetime Membership, and committee dues are $70 a year (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.4, Sep 2023; Vol. 9.1, Jun 2022). Committee dues cover the year's meetings and activities, including food and drinks for meetings and the Rodeo Prom celebration (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.3, Aug 2020; Vol. 8.1, Jun 2021). A member who has not paid by the second meeting is removed from the roster (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.2, Jul 2018).

To "badge" each season, a member must pay both Show and Committee dues, verify their legal name with the Membership Office, sign the indemnity form, and reach their sales threshold (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.4, Sep 2020; Vol. 3.2, Aug 2016). The sales threshold is selling or collecting $1,000 for rookies and a total of $2,000 for veterans (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.4, Sep 2020). Both ad sales and solicited donations count toward the badge requirement, so a member short on ad sales can still reach the threshold through donations (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.8, Nov 2018; Vol. 3.7, May 2017).

Skipping the signed indemnity form is the most commonly cited cause of a delayed badge, called out year after year in the newsletter (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.5, Oct 2023; Vol. 9.2, Sep 2022; Vol. 8.3, Nov 2021). The indemnity agreement also carries notice that HLSR runs sex offender background checks on all committee volunteers (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.2, Aug 2016; Vol. 2.4, Sep 2015). Legal-name verification is done by submitting a driver's license to the Membership Office by email, fax, or in person, and the office shreds or deletes the image after registering the name (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.2, Aug 2016; Vol. 2.4, Sep 2015).

Badging early rather than procrastinating is how a member earns a gold badge for the upcoming Show season (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.3, Aug 2019; Vol. 5.4, Aug 2018). Gold badge release also requires that all dues are paid, all Show requirements are met, and the member has updated their contact information on the Rodeo website (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.2, Jul 2018). Released badges are picked up at the membership window inside NRG Center on the second floor, not at committee events (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.3, Jan 2023).

## How members raise money

SPC members raise funds by soliciting program ads and collecting donations on their own time, rather than working scheduled shifts (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.6, Dec 2020). Ad contracts are due at the final meeting in November, but members can keep turning in donations until the Rodeo to earn their badge (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.7, Oct 2018). The final general meeting of the season always produces the largest sales turn-in of the year (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.7, Nov 2017).

Donations have their own timing rules. Donations received after the committee's annual donation deadline count toward the following year's Rodeo season rather than the current one (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.5, Oct 2020), and donations turned in after the program's content has gone to the publisher may not get recognition in the printed program (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.7, Nov 2017).

## The protected-client custom

Before prospecting a new advertiser, members check whether the company is already a Souvenir Program advertiser by reviewing the prior year's program index and the committee's "Protected" List, and they can also use the dashboard's client search at rodeospc.com (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.4, Sep 2020; Vol. 6.1, Jul 2019). When a member finds they are soliciting an existing advertiser or donor, the practice is to thank them, encourage renewal with their protected SPC member, gracefully exit, and notify their Captain or Assistant Captain and the protected member (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.1, Jul 2019).

## Recurring traditions

The committee opens each year with an annual Kick-Off Crawfish Boil, a recruiting party geared toward attracting rookies where members can bring a guest or potential new member and also pick up their badges (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.1, Jun 2016; Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014; Vol. 7.7, Apr 2021).

The committee runs a season-long calendar of general meetings, each with its own dress-up theme (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.3, Aug 2017). At meetings, new members sign in at the administration table by the entry doors and pick up a name tag (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.3, Jul 2018). Members who keep their red raffle ticket from every meeting and hold the complete set are entered into a special prize raffle at Rodeo Prom (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.4, Sep 2014; Vol. 1.3, Sep 2014).

Rodeo Prom is the committee's end-of-year Awards Banquet, where the year's final ad-sales numbers and committee awards are shared (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.3, Jan 2023; Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019). It has been held in late January or February in different years (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.7, Nov 2017; Vol. 3.5, Oct 2016; Vol. 1.6, Nov 2014). The committee names a Rookie of the Year at the banquet each year (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.6, Oct 2017).

The committee also runs an annual Karaoke Contest, which began with its inaugural event in 2014 (The Heartbeat Vol. 2.6, Oct 2015), and partners with the Trailblazer Committee's Rodeo ROPES program on an annual book drive hosted over committee meetings (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.4, Sep 2019).

## Apparel and social media

Committee vests are optional and are ordered online through Houston Uniform & Apparel Company using the committee login, rather than provided by the committee (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.6, Oct 2017; Vol. 4.3, Aug 2017).

The committee's social media identity is the Instagram handle @souvenir_program_committee and the Facebook group "Souvenir Program Committee - Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo," with the hashtags #hustleforthebuckle and #thatSPClife (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.4, Sep 2023; Vol. 7.7, Apr 2021). The Facebook group is where members ask questions, share tips, and post sales successes (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.5, Oct 2020). Content on the committee's pages must be relevant to the committee and the Show. The governance rule is a warning on a first post removal and removal from the page on a second (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.3, Jul 2018).

## FAQ

**What does the Souvenir Program Committee do?**
It solicits ads and donations for the HLSR Souvenir Program. After the program is produced, all the money goes to the Show's educational scholarship fund (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.6, Jan 2024; Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024).

**Who leads the committee?**
An Officer in Charge, a Committee Chairman, and Vice Chairmen split by function, with members organized into numbered teams under captains and assistant captains (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.3, Aug 2016).

**How do I earn my badge?**
Pay your Show and Committee dues, verify your legal name with the Membership Office, sign the indemnity form, and reach your sales threshold, which is $1,000 for rookies and a total of $2,000 for veterans (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.4, Sep 2020). Badge early to earn a gold badge for the Show season (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.4, Aug 2018).

**Why is my badge delayed?**
The most common reason cited across the newsletters is skipping the signed indemnity form (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.2, Sep 2022). You also have to verify your legal name with the Membership Office before a badge will be released (The Heartbeat Vol. 8.2, Sep 2021).

**What are the committee's main traditions?**
The Kick-Off Crawfish Boil opens the season, themed general meetings run through it, and Rodeo Prom, the end-of-year Awards Banquet, closes it (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.1, Jun 2016; Vol. 4.3, Aug 2017; Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019).

**Someone already has my prospect. What do I do?**
Thank them, encourage them to renew with their protected SPC member, exit gracefully, and notify your Captain or Assistant Captain and the protected member (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.1, Jul 2019).
