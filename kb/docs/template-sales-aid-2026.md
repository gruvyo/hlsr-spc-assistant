# Sales aid with sample ads (2026)

**Quick answer:** This is a ready-to-personalize sales tool. It opens with a cover letter (with blanks for the deadline, your name, email, and cell) that explains the Souvenir Program and the two ways to contribute, then shows a real sample ad at every size and price so a prospect can picture exactly what they'd get. Fill in your blanks, then show it or leave it behind when you call on a prospect.

## What this is

A Word document you personalize and use as a leave-behind or presentation piece with a prospect. It has two parts. First a one-page cover letter, written as if signed by you (a Committee Volunteer), that thanks the prospect for considering an ad or donation, explains what the Souvenir Program Committee does, lays out the two ways to contribute, and explains where the money goes. Second, a gallery of sample ads, each one a real example labeled with its size and price, running from the largest 2-page color spread down to a quarter-page black-and-white.

The blanks in the letter are yours to fill: the contribution deadline, your printed name and signature line, your email, and your cell. There's also a callout box for the deadline (space reservations and copy due date).

## What the committee does

The Souvenir Program Committee is responsible for selling advertising, obtaining donations from companies and individuals, and assisting the FFA and 4-H members who sell the programs.

## The two ways to contribute

The letter gives a prospect two options:

1. Make a donation to the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo Educational Fund.
2. Purchase advertising in the RODEOHOUSTON Souvenir Program.

The deadline for advertising and donations is a blank you fill in.

## Where the money goes

The letter explains how the contribution works, in three points:

1. **Provide educational opportunities to Texas youth.** Donations made to the Show's Educational Fund through the Souvenir Program Committee go toward scholarships, grants, and other educational programs throughout Texas.
2. **Benefit the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo.** Purchasing an ad in the souvenir program supports the mission of the Show.
3. **Fund FFA chapters and 4-H clubs.** The programs are sold each night by a different FFA chapter and 4-H clubs. All proceeds raised from program sales go to that working organization. Many FFA chapters and 4-H clubs are able to fund their annual budget in one night of selling.

The letter closes with a line about being honored that the prospect made the choice to consider the opportunity to make a difference, signed by the Committee Volunteer.

## How to use it

Personalize the cover letter first. Fill in the deadline, your name, email, and cell, and the deadline callout box. Then walk a prospect through the sample ads so they can see what each size and price actually looks like in print, and pick the tier that fits. The document pairs well with the presentation sales tip and the ad guidelines (which cover artwork specs and how to submit the finished art).

## FAQ

**How much does an ad cost?**
By size: 2-page color is $2K, Star color is $1K, full-page color is $1K (or $2K placed next to the Star ad), half-page color is $750, full-page black and white is $700, half-page B&W is $450, and quarter-page black and white is $300.

**What's the difference between a full-page color ad at $1,000 and at $2,000?**
The placement. A full-page color ad is $1K on its own. If you place it adjacent to the Star ad (a premium spot), it's $2K.

**What do I fill in before I use this?**
The contribution deadline, your printed name and signature, your email, your cell, and the deadline callout box (space reservations and copy due date). Everything else is already written.

**Can a prospect donate instead of buying an ad?**
Yes. The two ways to contribute are a donation to the HLSR Educational Fund or purchasing an ad in the Souvenir Program. Donations fund scholarships, grants, and educational programs for Texas youth.

**Where does the money go?**
Three places. Donations support scholarships, grants, and education programs across Texas. Ad purchases support the mission of the Show. And the FFA chapters and 4-H clubs that sell the programs each night keep all the proceeds from program sales, often enough to fund a chapter's whole annual budget in one night.

**Are the sample ads real?**
Yes. Each one is a real finished ad (Gulf Coast Brain & Spine, VistaCOM, Tri County Storage, and others) included so a prospect can see exactly what each size and price looks like in print.
