# SPC training videos: recording plan and creator template

**Quick answer:** This is the playbook for making SPC training videos. Part one gives the recording standards (keep each video under three minutes, one task per video, open with the point in the first five seconds, use the Bluetooth lavalier mic, record with the Mac recorder at Command-Shift-5) and a blank planning block you copy for each new video. Part two is the initial plan, about thirty videos across nine categories, each already filled out so it pastes straight into the admin upload form.

## What this is

A two-part planning document for the SPC training video library. Part one is the recording standards plus a blank planning template to copy for any new video. Part two is the predetermined recording plan for the initial library, with every video already filled out. The template fields match the fields on the admin upload form, so a completed plan becomes a copy-and-paste at upload time.

## Recording standards

Keep these consistent so the library feels like one library, even with several people recording.

- **Length:** keep every video under three minutes. One task per video.
- **Open with the point:** in the first five seconds, say what the viewer will learn.
- **Audio:** use the Bluetooth lavalier microphone as first choice. Test a full three-minute recording first to check for dropouts. The conference microphone is the reliable fallback. Do not use AirPods, since their microphone mode drops audio quality.
- **Recording tool:** use the Mac built-in recorder (Command-Shift-5) or QuickTime. It captures any app and handles the microphone natively. The Chrome screenshot extension is not needed.
- **Resolution:** record at native screen resolution and export at 1080p. Let YouTube handle the rest.
- **Legibility:** zoom the browser to 110 to 125 percent and record a tight region around the dashboard, so buttons stay readable on a phone. Small UI text is the number one quality killer.
- **Mobile:** most videos are desktop-only. Record a mobile companion only where the phone experience genuinely differs (navigation, anything that collapses, and the highest-traffic member tasks like entering a donation or using the shareable link). For mobile capture, use the iPhone screen recording, AirDrop the file to the Mac, and voice it over there.
- **Naming:** lead the title with the verb where it makes sense (Submitting an Ad Contract, Processing New Members).

## Blank planning template

Copy this block for each new video and fill it in before recording. The completed block is what you paste into the admin upload form.

- **Video title:** (lead with the verb where it fits)
- **Category:** (pick one of the nine defined categories)
- **Roles who can see it:** (pick from the dashboard role keys. Tag shared tasks to all relevant roles.)
- **One-sentence description:** (this becomes the description field on the page)
- **Open with:** (the five-second statement of what the viewer will learn)
- **This video covers:** (three to six bullets, no more)
- **This video does NOT cover:** (one line. Point to the related video instead. This field keeps videos short.)
- **Common mistakes and edge cases to mention:** (optional. Where the real value is for process-heavy videos.)

## The nine categories

Nine categories, roughly thirty starting videos. Several categories begin with few or zero videos, which is fine. The library grows as questions come in. Tags use the dashboard's existing role keys.

1. **Onboarding.** Identity and orientation content for every member, regardless of tenure. Owned by VC Recruitment. This tab sorts first so new members land in the right place.
2. **Member Tasks.** The doing-the-work library every member needs, regardless of tenure. Evergreen task references, not graduate-out-of onboarding content.
3. **VC Shared Tasks.** Tasks every Vice Chairman should be able to do. Tag these videos to all VC roles so no single person becomes a bottleneck.
4. **VC Administration.** Administration-specific workflows. The report-loading video here is tagged to all VCs, not just Administration.
5. **VC Communications.** Internal communications, namely newsletter, social media, and committee email. This is Joe's world. The content collection workflow defines how other VCs feed communications.
6. **VC Sales.** Sales-specific workflows. This is Carol's world. Includes the sales report load and the renewal confirmation policy.
7. **VC Recruitment.** Recruitment brings people in and trains them well enough to hand off to captains. Recruitment also owns the Onboarding tab content.
8. **Coordinators / External Outreach.** Placeholder. Coordinators are former VCs who do outreach to other committees, the external half of communications (four coordinators). No videos defined yet. The category can exist empty and will not render as a tab until it has content.
9. **Team Captains.** The most-used tab, given how many captains there are. Likely to grow as captains ask questions. Every repeated question is a future video.

## Initial recording plan: Onboarding

**Committee History.** Roles: All members. Description: where the Souvenir Program Committee came from and why it exists. Covers when and how the committee was formed, its place within the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, a few milestones worth knowing, and why the souvenir program matters to the organization. Does not cover anything about the current-year sales process.

**Committee Structure.** Roles: All members. Description: how the committee is organized, from Chairman down to new members. Covers the Chairman role, the Vice Chairmen (Administration, Sales, Communications, Recruitment), Coordinators and Team Captains, and how members fit into a team. Does not cover how to perform any specific task.

**What We Do and How We Function.** Roles: All members. Description: the committee's mission and how a year actually runs. Covers the committee mission, the annual cycle at a glance, how the program gets sold and produced, and meeting cadence and expectations. Does not cover badge, parking, or seating details.

**How Membership Works.** Roles: All members. Description: how to be on the committee and what is expected of you. Covers how you join and stay on the committee, dues and badge basics, and the member commitment and expectations. Does not cover the Vice Chairman side of processing a new member (covered in Processing New Members). Note: keep this title distinct from the VC Administration video Processing New Members. This one is for the member, that one is the admin workflow.

**Badges and Benefits.** Roles: All members. Description: what your badge gets you, including parking, seating, and corral clubs. Covers where and how to pick up your badge, parking, how many seats your badge includes and how seating works, and what the corral clubs are. Does not cover how to advance on the committee.

**Multiple Committees and Advancement.** Roles: All members. Description: how many committees you can serve on and how to move up. Covers how many committees you can be on, how to request another committee, and how to move up on this committee. Does not cover Vice Chairman specific duties.

**Dashboard Tour.** Roles: All members. Description: a quick orientation to the dashboard so you know where everything lives. Covers logging in, the main dashboard layout, where the key widgets are, and where to find your training videos. Does not cover task-specific actions like entering a donation (those live with each task video).

## Initial recording plan: Member Tasks

**Submitting an Ad Contract.** Roles: All members. Description: how to submit an ad contract from start to finish. Covers where to start in the dashboard, filling out the contract, and submitting and confirming it went through. Does not cover contract corrections or renewals (separate topics).

**Getting an Entertainer Ad.** Roles: All members. Description: the process for securing an entertainer ad. Covers what an entertainer ad is, the steps to get one, and what to confirm before submitting. Does not cover standard ad contracts (covered separately).

**Entering a Donation.** Roles: All members. Description: how to enter a donation in the dashboard. Covers where to enter a donation, the fields that matter, and confirming the donation recorded. Does not cover ad contracts. Note: strong candidate for a mobile companion video, since members enter donations from their phones in the field.

**Your Shareable Link and Social Sharing.** Roles: All members. Description: how your shareable link works and whether you can post it on social media. Covers where to find your shareable link, how it tracks back to you, and whether and how you can post it on social media. Does not cover building your prospect list (separate video). Note: mobile companion worth considering, this is a phone-first task.

**Building Your Prospect List.** Roles: All members. Description: how to build the list of people you will sell ads to or collect donations from. Covers where to keep your prospects, how to source good prospects, and keeping the list organized. Does not cover who you are not allowed to approach (covered in Protected Donors).

**Protected Donors and the Hands-Off List.** Roles: All members. Description: who you are not allowed to approach, and what to do if you are unsure. Covers who the protected and hands-off donors are, why they are protected, and what to do if you are unsure before approaching someone. Does not cover general prospecting (covered in Building Your Prospect List). Note: recommended or required viewing. A mistake here creates a real problem with a major donor.

**Recruiting Friends to the Committee.** Roles: All members. Description: how to bring friends onto the committee. Covers how to invite someone, what to tell them about the commitment, and where to send them to get started. Does not cover the recruitment VC intake process.

**Selling Incentives.** Roles: All members. Description: what incentives you have to sell and how to use them. Covers what incentives are available, how each one works, and how to position them when selling. Does not cover renewal confirmation rules (covered in VC Sales).

## Initial recording plan: VC Shared Tasks

## Initial recording plan: VC Administration

**Processing Team Change Requests.** Roles: VC Administration. Description: the administration side of handling a team change request. Covers where requests arrive, how to process and approve them, and what the member sees afterward. Does not cover the captain submission side or the sales review side. Note: one of three angles on team change requests (captain submits, administration processes, sales reviews). Use consistent terminology across all three and mention where the request goes next.

**Processing New Members.** Roles: VC Administration. Description: the admin workflow to bring an approved new member onto the roster and set them up. Covers taking an approved new member into the roster, getting them set up in the dashboard, and confirming they are ready. Does not cover the new-member-facing onboarding content (that lives in the Onboarding tab).

**Loading the Roster Report.** Roles: All VC roles (tag broadly). Description: download the full roster report from rodeohouston.com and load it into the data parser. Covers downloading the full roster report as a CSV from rodeohouston.com, loading it into the data parser, and confirming the load succeeded. Does not cover the sales report load (different source, separate video). Common mistakes and edge cases: malformed CSV, duplicate handling, and the common parser errors. This load runs every 3 to 4 days, so whoever covers it will hit every edge case within a couple of months. Show what to do when each one happens. Note: likely the single highest-value video in the library. Tag it to all VC roles so anyone can run the load when the Administration VC is out.

**Awards Management and Selection Process.** Roles: VC Administration. Description: how awards are managed and how the selection process works. Covers where awards data lives, the selection process, and key dates or steps to not miss. Does not cover general roster management.

Covers how other VCs submit content for inclusion, how to gather and format submissions, and the path from submission to published. ## FAQ

**How long should a training video be, and what should it cover?**
Under three minutes, one task per video. Open in the first five seconds by saying what the viewer will learn. Three to six bullets in the covers list, no more. If it runs long, split it.

**What equipment do I record with?**
Use the Bluetooth lavalier microphone first (test a full three-minute take first to check for dropouts), with the conference microphone as the fallback. Do not use AirPods. For screen capture use the Mac built-in recorder (Command-Shift-5) or QuickTime. Record at native screen resolution and export at 1080p.

**How do I keep the dashboard readable on a phone?**
Zoom the browser to 110 to 125 percent and record a tight region around the dashboard so buttons stay readable. Small UI text is the number one quality killer.

**When do I make a mobile companion video?**
Most videos are desktop-only. Add a mobile version only where the phone experience genuinely differs, like navigation, anything that collapses, and the highest-traffic member tasks (entering a donation, using the shareable link). Capture with the iPhone screen recording, AirDrop the file to the Mac, and voice it over there.

**What are the nine categories?**
Onboarding, Member Tasks, VC Shared Tasks, VC Administration, VC Communications, VC Sales, VC Recruitment, Coordinators / External Outreach (placeholder, no videos yet), and Team Captains. A category with no videos will not render as a tab until it has content.

**Which video should I record first if I want the most impact?**
Loading the Roster Report is called out as likely the single highest-value video. It runs every 3 to 4 days, so tag it to all VC roles so anyone can run the load when the Administration VC is out. The Renewal Confirmation Process and Protected Donors videos are strong required-viewing candidates because a mistake there causes real problems.

**Why is there a "This video does NOT cover" field?**
It keeps videos short. Instead of expanding scope, you point the viewer to the related video. That one line is what stops a three-minute video from becoming ten.
