# Notable records and recognition (from The Heartbeat archive)

**Quick answer:** Over the years The Heartbeat has recorded a handful of standout numbers and honors. The biggest single-event attendance record is George Strait's March 17, 2013 concert, at 80,020 paid. On the committee side, the SPC names a Rookie of the Year each season and shares its committee awards at the year-end Rodeo Prom.

## What this is

A synthesis of the notable records, milestones, and recognitions mentioned across ten years of The Heartbeat, the Souvenir Program Committee's newsletter (2014 to 2024). Every fact below cites the specific issue it came from. The newsletter archive is on the SPC Dashboard. Nothing here is invented, and the figures are as the newsletters reported them at the time.

## Attendance and participation records

- George Strait's March 17, 2013 concert-only performance set an all-time paid-attendance record for the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, with 80,020 people. It was also a record for any event held in the stadium. (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.2, Aug 2014)
- When the Show moved from the Sam Houston Coliseum to the Astrodome in 1966, opening attendance was 25,340. (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.3, Sep 2014)
- The first Rodeo, in 1932, lasted 5 days and brought in 2,000 visitors. (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017)
- The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo brings together roughly 32,000 volunteers. (The Heartbeat Vol. 2.6, Oct 2015)
- The Show's BBQ contest, conceived by Bill Bailey, grew to host 250 cooking teams and nearly 250,000 guests. (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017)

## People recognized and HLSR firsts

- Patricia "Pat" Mann Phillips was the first woman elected to the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo's Executive Committee. (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.1, Jun 2018)
- Charley Pride performed at RODEOHOUSTON 36 times across 14 years and is a member of the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo's Star Trail of Fame. (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.4, Sep 2016)
- Bill Bailey, born Milton Odom Stanley, became the "Voice of the Rodeo" announcer and originated the idea of incorporating a BBQ contest into the Show. (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017)

## SPC committee recognition

- The Souvenir Program Committee names a Rookie of the Year each year. (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.6, Oct 2017)
- The year's committee awards, along with the final ad-sales numbers, are shared at Rodeo Prom, the committee's end-of-year banquet. (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.3, Jan 2023)

## Milestones in HLSR history

- The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo began in 1932, conceived during the Great Depression by a group of cattlemen at the Texas State Hotel who planned to sell cattle to promote the Texas Gulf Coast cattle industry. The event was originally called the Houston Fat Stock Show and was held at the Democratic Convention Hall until 1937. (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017)
- The Saltgrass Trail Ride is the oldest trail ride that participates in the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo. It made its first ride into Houston in 1952, when four men left Brenham, Texas on horseback. (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.3, Sep 2014)
- The 2022 Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo marked the 90th anniversary of the organization, as noted in the spring 2021 newsletter. (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.7, Apr 2021)

## FAQ

**What's the all-time attendance record for the Rodeo?**
George Strait's March 17, 2013 concert-only performance, at 80,020 paid. It was also a record for any event held in the stadium. (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.2, Aug 2014)

**Who was the first woman on the HLSR Executive Committee?**
Patricia "Pat" Mann Phillips. (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.1, Jun 2018)

**Does the committee give a Rookie of the Year award?**
Yes. The SPC names a Rookie of the Year each season, and committee awards are shared at the year-end Rodeo Prom. The Vol. 4.6 newsletter (Oct 2017) lists honorees from 2012 through 2017.

**When did the Rodeo start, and what was it first called?**
It began in 1932 as the Houston Fat Stock Show. (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017)
