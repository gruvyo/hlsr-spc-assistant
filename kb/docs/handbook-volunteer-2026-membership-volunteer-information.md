# Volunteer Handbook: Membership & Volunteer Information

**Quick answer:** This is the big middle section of the 2026 Volunteer Handbook (pages 17 to 63). It is the rulebook for being an HLSR member and serving on a committee. It covers how to become a member (Annual $50, Junior Annual $25, Life one-time $500), how committee appointment and dues work, the lifetime committeeman policy, how rosters and hierarchy are managed, the four leadership roles, how gold and silver badges and parking are issued and what they get you, how to schedule and run committee meetings, the rules for invitations and correspondence, special requests like business cards and sign shop, the Corral Club, and all the legal policies (harassment, hazing, workplace violence, drug and alcohol, whistleblower).

## What this is

The "Membership & Volunteer Information" section of the 2026 Volunteer Handbook and Directory, pages 17 to 63. Most of it applies to every committee, the Souvenir Program Committee (SPC) included. A few parts call out specific committees by name (Corral Club, Area Go Texan, School Art, Jr. Rodeo, Graphics and Signage).

## Eligibility for membership

Membership is open to any individual interested in supporting the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo mission.

Members who submit a check with insufficient funds for committee dues or annual memberships are deemed ineligible to serve on a committee if the returned amount is not paid in full within 30 days of notice from Accounting. If a gold badge and/or parking permits for the upcoming Show have already been provided, those items will be confiscated.

Members who submit a check with insufficient funds for other obligations are deemed ineligible to serve on a committee if the returned amount is not paid in full within 90 days of notice from Accounting.

## Types of membership

- Annual (May 1 to April 30). Available to any individual 21 years of age and older. Cost: $50. Expires yearly on April 30. Renew through the member's online account.
- Junior Annual Membership (May 1 to April 30). Available to any individual under the age of 21. Cost: $25. Expires yearly on April 30. Renew through the member's online account.
- Life. Available to any individual for a one-time $500 payment. No subsequent Show membership purchases are required for the rest of the member's life.

## Membership benefits

Annual membership benefits:

- Official Rodeo car decal
- Subscription to the quarterly "Bowlegged H" Magazine
- Opportunity to volunteer on a committee (optional)

Life benefits (Annual Member benefits plus):

- Life Member pin
- Opportunity to purchase a life member plaque for $75
- Life Membership Card. Grants admission to the Livestock Shows and Horse Shows, Commercial Exhibits, Carnival, and the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest. Not included: Reliant Stadium or Rodeo Uncorked! events.

Not included in Annual or Life benefits: parking, gold or silver badges, and/or a guaranteed spot on a volunteer committee.

To get a Rodeo membership, complete a membership application online at rodeohouston.com, or complete a Membership Application form from the Membership Department or a Membership Committee member. Failure to provide all required information may prohibit processing. A completed membership application, indemnity agreement, member handbook acknowledgement, and membership payment must be received by the Membership Department.

## Membership cards

A membership card and any other benefit items are sent to new members by the Membership Department. Member cards are valid until the expiration date printed on the physical card. At expiration, the Membership Office sends a new card to the member's address on file. Cards are non-transferable and are for the exclusive use of the individual to whom they are issued.

## Suspension, expulsion, and miscellaneous membership rules

Any member, life or annual, may be suspended or expelled by the chairman of the board and the president when it is determined the member is a detriment to the welfare of the Show. Suspension or expulsion takes effect after the member has been notified of the action in writing by the Show.

Memberships are non-transferable, non-refundable, and issued on an individual basis only. There are no provisions for company, corporate, or family memberships.

If an annual member has already paid the current annual membership and wants to convert to a life membership within the same May 1 to April 30 year, they can do so by purchasing a $450 life member conversion from their online account or the Membership Office. Payment is due by the end of that same Show year.

## Eligibility for committee members

To serve on a committee, a person:

- Must be a member of, and in good standing with, the Show.
- Must be at least 21 years of age (exception: the Jr. Rodeo Committee).
- Must pass a routine criminal background check from a third-party provider.
- Must not have a delinquent account with the Show as of the designated payment deadline of the appointment year.
- Must have membership established, with payment made, prior to actual appointment. An appointment letter will not be sent to non-members.
- Must complete the Indemnity Agreement and member handbook acknowledgement on an annual basis.
- Must complete all required training.

## Appointment policy and steps

The committee chairman and the officer-in-charge are responsible for selecting committee members. All committee volunteers serve at the pleasure of the president, and all appointments are made under the president's authority. No one other than the appropriate chairman or officer-in-charge should notify a potential member of a specific committee appointment.

Appointment letters are emailed to the email address on file as a member is added to the roster. Final appointment is contingent on an executed indemnity form on file, member handbook acknowledged, current Show membership, and verification of eligibility.

An individual may not serve on a committee if he/she, a spouse, or a related business has a delinquent account with the Show as of the designated payment deadline of the appointment year. Committee dues must be paid prior to the first scheduled committee meeting or before beginning work on the committee, whichever comes first.

A member may serve on multiple committees but may earn only one badge and one year of committee service credit per Show. Service credit is recorded only if the volunteer meets the committee's badging requirements. Volunteers can verify service credits on the membership status page of their online account.

Each committee has a maximum number of members. Any deviation above the maximum must be approved by the president. Each committee should be large enough to handle its responsibilities but not so large as to have idle members. Each member must be willing to accept assignments and handle a share of responsibilities.

Appointment steps: after chairmen are appointed by the president, each chairman or official designee reviews the current roster to determine necessary changes. All roster changes must be approved by the staff coordinator. Volunteers removed from a roster must be indicated on the roster change form with a notation of why (for example resigned, did not meet requirements, leadership recommendation). All roster changes, including leadership recommendation removals, must be approved by the officer-in-charge, management coordinator, and other management staff assigned by the president. Appointment and removal notices are automatically emailed to the email on file.

Committee appointments are tentative until all membership dues are paid, background checks are completed, required training is completed, and a valid Indemnity Agreement Form and member handbook are signed and on file. The completed indemnity form and handbook acknowledgement must be returned, and dues paid, before the volunteer badge and parking are released.

Committee appointments remain in effect for multiple years. Any appointment becomes null and void when committee or Show leadership removes a volunteer from a roster.

Chairmen, or approved designees, are encouraged to respond to each person on the volunteer request list whether or not there are openings. Send a courtesy email if the committee is not recruiting.

## Committee application and the volunteer request list

The committee volunteer request list is part of the Show's recruitment process. It includes individuals who have asked to be contacted about volunteering on a specific committee by placing themselves on the request list through their online account. A simple referral back to the Membership Department for placement on another committee's request list is appropriate when the requested committee already has a full roster for the year.

Procedures:

- A "Volunteer Request Download" is available through Reports on both chairman and designee online accounts.
- The chairman assigns a committee volunteer to contact people on the request list about the timeline and process.
- For committees with limited space, a questionnaire is recommended to help pick the best candidates for interviews. Questionnaires must be approved by the Show through the staff coordinator.
- Once interviews are done or spots are filled, those remaining on the list must be told the roster is full for the year and encouraged to seek a position on another committee.
- The chairman or designee should remove the committee from the volunteer request list when recruitment is complete.
- Volunteer request lists are purged May 1 each year.
- A request history download is available from the staff coordinator.

Note: potential volunteers must be a life or current-year annual Show member before they can be added to the volunteer request list.

## Ineligibility and removal

Factors that may lead to removal or non-reappointment include:

- Failure of a committee member to perform at the expected level (see note below).
- HLSR's right to remove or not permit any person from volunteering.
- A consistent negative attitude or behavior not conducive to the committee's goals.
- Involvement in incidents that put others, or the Show, at risk.
- Involvement in incidents that negatively reflect on the Show.
- Violation of Show or committee policies.
- Failure to complete required training.
- Failure to complete the required background check by the designated deadline.

Failure to perform at the expected level includes, but is not limited to, not completing the badging requirements set by committee leadership. A one-year grace period may be approved by the chairman, but any member not badging for two consecutive years should be removed from the roster.

Removal is recommended by the chairman and approved by the staff coordinator, management coordinator, and other management staff assigned by the president. If a chairman is unsure or the removal is likely to be controversial, the chairman should consult the officer-in-charge and the management coordinator before submitting the roster change.

## Committee dues and service requirements

Committee dues are non-refundable.

- Committeeman. All volunteers serving on a committee must fulfill their committee service requirements and pay committee dues by the deadline designated for each committee they serve on. Chairmen may use discretion for volunteers facing financial difficulties on a case-by-case basis.
- Lifetime Committeeman. Required to pay committee dues for each committee in whose activities they participate, including attending meetings and social events.
- Director. Must be a lifetime member of the Show. Voting members of the board must actively serve on at least one committee and pay dues for all committees they volunteer on. Directors must fulfill each committee's badging requirements.
- Lifetime Director. Lifetime directors who were committee members on June 24, 2004, or future lifetime directors who were members at the time of elevation, do not count against the committee maximum. Lifetime directors added to a committee after June 24, 2004 do count against the total. All lifetime directors must pay dues for all committees they volunteer on and participate in.
- Vice President and Officers-in-Charge. Currently serving vice presidents (VPs) are exempt from dues for committees where they serve as officer-in-charge (OIC). To receive service credit on a committee where they are not the OIC, VPs must pay dues for that committee.
- Lifetime Vice President. Lifetime vice presidents (LVPs) do not count toward the committee maximum. LVPs must pay dues only to the LVP's committee. However, LVPs who serve and participate in another committee's activities must pay dues for each such committee to receive service credit. LVPs who do not pay dues and are not active on a committee where they formerly served as OIC are removed from that roster. The committee may include former OICs and LVPs as guests at activities and social events. Executive Committee members are also LVPs, so the same LVP guidelines apply to them.

## Delinquent account and insufficient funds check

A committee member may be placed on the Show's ineligible list for not paying the balance on a Show account, or for having more than one insufficient funds check within a year. An ineligible member cannot earn a badge and is not granted volunteer privileges.

When a committee member and/or their spouse becomes ineligible, the Accounting Department notifies the staff coordinator, who informs the chairman, who notifies the volunteer. An automatic notice is also sent from noreply@hlsr.com with reinstatement instructions.

To become eligible again, the volunteer must first clear the Show account. Then compose a letter to the Show president requesting reinstatement, including the circumstance for missing the payment deadline. Email the letter to the committee chairman processing the request. If approved, the volunteer is notified by email. Reinstatement requests should be submitted no later than Jan. 15 each Show year. Reinstatement is not guaranteed and can take up to two weeks.

## Lifetime committeeman policy

Volunteers who accept lifetime committeeman status can no longer receive other badge titles such as chairman, vice chairman, or captain (exception: past committee chairman title). They are also not eligible for recommendation to the board of directors.

Individuals who should not apply: committee leaders (those holding or wanting a leadership position), director applicants (must be active on at least one committee), and directors (must remain active to be a board representative).

Newly appointed lifetime committeemen without committee service credit for the previous three Show years are automatically removed from that roster. Those with the three required years remain on the roster and serve at the discretion of the chairman. Lifetime committeemen remaining on a roster must pay committee dues.

A lifetime committeeman keeps the appointment as long as they remain a current member in good standing. If at the end of a Show year a lifetime committeeman is no longer a member or in good standing, they forgo the status. Lifetime committeemen receive a gold badge and complimentary parking at a designated off-site location, including shuttle service. Their gold badges convey the same privileges as those of active committeemen and must comply with the Show's badge policy.

Application procedures: the application period runs April 1 to May 31 each year. Applicants must be at least 60 years of age with a minimum of 20 years of documented committee service. Age is determined as of August 1 each year. Submit an official application electronically through the volunteer's online account (on the lifetime committeeman page), or get a paper application from the Membership Office. The president appoints a review committee of two lifetime vice presidents and one current vice president, which submits recommendations to the president no later than July 15 each year. Appointment to the title, and the notification, is the sole responsibility of the president. Appointees do not re-apply each year, but status is reviewed annually. The president may suspend a lifetime committeeman for good cause and must notify them in writing by Sept. 1 if suspended. Specific dates may be revised to better facilitate the policy.

## Committee size and roster management

Each committee has a maximum number of members. Any deviation above the maximum must be approved by the president. Lifetime committeemen and lifetime vice presidents do not count toward the maximum.

Lifetime committeemen must have badged on a committee for the three years preceding their lifetime committeeman application to continue participation. Volunteers who accept lifetime committeeman status may not join a new committee. Lifetime directors who were members on June 24, 2004 (or future lifetime directors who were members at elevation) do not count against the maximum. Lifetime directors added after June 24, 2004 do count.

The chairman and designees the chairman selects are given access to online features. HLSR Privacy Policy prohibits using volunteer personal contact information from committee rosters for any outside solicitations (No Solicitations).

To assign designees (chairman access only):

1. Log into your online account.
2. Select the Committee Permissions Tab.
3. To add a member, select their role from the drop-down box, enter their member number, then click the add button.
4. Click the save button.

The designee has access to all responsibilities as the chairman except the permission table. To remove a member, click the delete button next to that person's name.

## Hierarchy change request

Hierarchy changes are used when the structure (titles) or size of a committee is to be altered. Submit through the online system:

- Select the Hierarchy Request tab.
- Review current maximums in the left-hand column. Enter the new maximums in the right-hand column.
- Enter a brief reason in the comment field.
- Hit the submit button.

The chairman should consult the staff coordinator, management coordinator, and officer-in-charge before submitting. The approval path is officer-in-charge, then management coordinator, then chief volunteer officer, then president. Each person in the chain receives an email with a link to the request form.

## Submitting a roster change

Roster changes are submitted through the Show's online system. The chairman and designees have access.

Online submission:

- Click Menu, and under the Chairman Tools section of the side menu, click Roster Request.
- In the Request Type box, select Addition or Title Change, Removal, or Removal-Leadership Recommendation.
- If Addition is selected, a second box offers a drop-down of the committee's volunteer request list. New members must be selected from this list.
- Click the Add button and a selection box appears for subcommittee and title assignments.
- Informative red text error messages may appear if the change cannot be processed. Click Save to continue later, or Submit when all adjustments are recorded.
- If a title change or remove action is selected, a second box appears to enter the member number of the current member.
- Click the Add button and the detailed selection box appears.
- The Reason field is mandatory for all removals.
- All removals MUST be on a separate roster request. Removals cannot be on the same request as additions or title changes.
- Click Submit when all adjustments are recorded.
- When possible, chairmen are encouraged to submit as many names as possible on a single request (maximum of 50). This is faster than requests with one or two names.

Roster summary: after chairmen are appointed, each chairman or official designee reviews the current roster. All roster changes, including adds, must be approved by the staff coordinator. Removals must note why the person left. Appointment and removal notices are automatically emailed to the email on file.

## Leadership roles and responsibilities

More than 36,000 volunteers serve on 109 different committees. Each committee is overseen by four people: the officer-in-charge, the management coordinator, the committee chairman, and the staff coordinator.

Officer-in-Charge (OIC). Ensures the committee functions consistent with the Show's mission. Officers are elected by the board for one-year terms and may serve no more than three terms in the same office (the Executive Committee may extend term limits if a force majeure prevents the Show in a calendar year). Responsibilities include providing the big-picture view in committee decisions, communicating significant budget or operating issues to management, communicating staff support concerns to the president, communicating with management before reacting to rumors, grooming future leaders and officers, advocating for hierarchy change requests, communicating Show-wide policy changes, representing the Show at events, addressing emergency needs that may be out of budget, and overseeing strategy while letting the chairman manage the details.

Management Coordinator. A salaried Show staff member who works with the OIC and senior management to ensure the committee has the financial, personnel, and other resources to carry out its mission. Responsibilities include operating within the approved budget (and getting approval to exceed it), communicating budget or committee issues to the OIC, keeping the OIC informed on Show policies, integrating committee actions into Show functions, thinking creatively about new opportunities, finding efficiencies to save money, and coaching staff coordinators.

Committee Chairman. The most visible role and the one that most directly affects members. Appointed by the president with the chairman of the board's approval for a one-year term, no more than three terms in the same office (force majeure extension possible). Responsibilities include all roster selections and removals, hierarchy change recommendations to the president, internal committee communications (with written consent before sharing personal information such as illnesses, births, or deaths), timeline development with the staff coordinator by July 31, committee budget development with the staff coordinator on the Finance Division's timeline, informing the OIC of issues, advocating for the committee while respecting Show decisions, running effective meetings, confirming meeting dates with the staff coordinator before distributing the calendar, submitting the agenda and handouts to the staff coordinator at least three business days in advance, confirming room setup the morning of the meeting, being available throughout the meeting, running subcommittee meetings (generally without staff support), establishing schedules, maintaining an up-to-date committee handbook and timeline, determining badging criteria with OIC approval, recognizing volunteer contributions, monitoring quality, identifying improvements through a post-Show assessment, mediating volunteer issues, and releasing badges to eligible members.

Vice Chairman. Appointed by the chairman annually for no more than three successive years. Reports to the chairman or division chairman/vice chairman and should know all aspects of the committee to be a future candidate for division chairman/chairman. Not eligible for the past committee chairman title after their term.

Captain. Appointed by the chairman annually for no more than three successive years. Reports to the vice chairman or assistant club chairman (Corral Club). Oversees committee members and assistant captains (if applicable).

Assistant Captain. Appointed by the chairman annually for no more than three successive years. Reports to the captain and oversees committee members on their team.

Staff Coordinator. A salaried Show staff member responsible for all aspects of committee support and the conduit to all Show departments. Works closely with the chairman, produces the committee timeline and handbook, and develops the budget. Responsibilities include being the primary communications facilitator with leadership, setting the timeline with the chairman by July 31, keeping handbooks updated and posted, developing the budget on the Finance Division's process, coordinating information flow to other departments and committees (contracts to Legal, donor forms to Marketing, sign requests to the Graphics and Signage Committee), managing onsite meetings, submitting online meeting requests to the Operations Division, assembling handouts at least three business days in advance, checking room setup early on meeting day, attending subcommittee meetings as needed, securing supplies, and facilitating purchase requests and reimbursements.

Additional committee positions (committee-specific):

- General Chairman, Corral Club. The Corral Club's committees are separated into three divisions under the General Corral Club Committee. Division I (At Large, Beverage Services, Auctions and Receptions, Equipment and Support) supports the other divisions. Division II handles clubs outside Reliant Stadium (Committeemen's Club, The Hideout, Main Club, Stockman's Club). Division III, the largest, includes the clubs within Reliant Stadium.
- Club Chairman, Corral Club. Direct responsibility for the day-to-day operation of a club, including personnel, inventory and ordering, and hosting. Eligible for the past committee chairman title after their term.
- Assistant Club Chairman, Corral Club. Reports to the club chairman, runs day-to-day operations in the club chairman's absence. May be assigned to one team or move between teams. Not eligible for the past committee chairman title.
- Division Chairman. Appointed annually for no more than three successive years. Reports to the chairman, oversees a specific division. May alternate through divisions over a three-year term. Restricted to 300-plus member committees. Not eligible for the past committee chairman title.
- Division Vice Chairman. Appointed annually for no more than three successive years. Reports to the division chairman. Restricted to 500-plus member committees. Not eligible for the past committee chairman title.
- District Chairman, Area Go Texan. Appointed by the co-chairmen, annually for no more than three successive years. Each district is six or seven counties led by a district chairman and two assistant district chairmen. Approved for Area Go Texan only (due to the committee's geographic spread). Not eligible for the past committee chairman title.
- Assistant District Chairman, Area Go Texan. Same appointment terms. Approved for Area Go Texan only. Not eligible for the past committee chairman title.
- County Chairman, Area Go Texan. Appointed by the co-chairmen, reports to the assistant district chairman, annually for no more than three successive years. Approved for Area Go Texan only. Not eligible for the past committee chairman title.
- District Coordinator, School Art. Appointed by the chairman. Must have served at least two years and be recommended by leadership. Serves as contact for assigned school districts, knows all rules and judging guidelines, trains assigned coordinators, and oversees the district shows. Approved for School Art only.
- Coordinator. Appointed annually for no more than three successive years. Reports to the chairman or vice chairman. Assists with administrative tasks (roster management, recruiting, training, event coordination).
- Judge, School Art. Appointed by the chairman on recommendation of the vice chairman of judges, steering judges, or other judges. Judges work in assigned teams (typically three to four judges per steering judge). A judge cannot currently, or for the previous three years, be employed by a participating school district or private school. Approved for School Art only.
- Ambassador. To be phased out. Provides guidance to the existing chairman, should have served in leadership previously. Appointed annually for no more than three successive years.
- Past Committee Chairman. A former chairman available as a resource to the current chairman. A counting title assigned by the Membership Office after a chairman or club chairman completes service. Not separately appointed.
- Lifetime Committeeman. A non-counting title changed on current rosters by the Membership Office once approved by the Lifetime Committeeman Review Committee and the president.

## Fundamental communication and issue escalation

The chairman and staff coordinator are expected to build a strong working relationship and manage the committee's responsibilities together. If issues arise, the chairman communicates to the OIC and the staff coordinator communicates to the management coordinator, so the OIC and management coordinator can resolve it. If they cannot, the issue goes up to the next highest management person, ultimately the president and, if needed, the chairman of the board.

Issue escalation order: first the Chairman and Staff Coordinator. Then the Officer-in-Charge and Management Coordinator. Then the Chief, then General Counsel and/or Chief of HR/Volunteers. As a final step, unresolved issues may go to the Chairman of the Board (COB) and CEO. The president maintains an open-door policy.

## Volunteer gold badge policy

Committee badges are non-transferable and for the exclusive use of the individual they are issued to. Loaning, giving, or selling badges is strictly prohibited and is grounds for forfeiture of the badge and/or parking permit, and may result in removal from committee service.

Committee badges shall not be issued to or worn by individuals under 21, with one exception: Gold Jr. Rodeo Committee badges go only to members of the Jr. Rodeo Committee, who range in age from 17 to 20.

A committeeman receives only one gold badge for committee participation, regardless of how many committees they serve on.

Anyone found duplicating, modifying, knowingly purchasing, or attempting to reproduce badges or parking permits may be prosecuted to the fullest extent of the law and/or permanently expelled.

Badge allotments for leadership:

- Directors receive a gold director badge plus one additional gold guest badge for a spouse or guest.
- Lifetime vice presidents receive a gold LVP badge, one gold badge for their spouse or designee (name engraved), and five gold and three silver guest badges.
- Executive Committee members and current officers (including lifetime executive committee members) each receive a personalized gold badge, plus five gold and five silver badges for guests or PR use, plus a personalized badge for their spouse or designee.
- Lifetime committeemen receive a gold lifetime committeeman badge each year if they remain a member in good standing.

Additional gold badges are issued only to committee volunteers, staff, and individuals approved by the Executive Committee or Show management, through the president's office, for daily duties, PR, and promotion of the Show. No one receives more than one gold badge except as stated here.

## Committee identification badges

The gold badge is the only Show badge produced with a gold finish. Other metal badges or pins for the Show, committee, or group must be non-gold (silver, colored enamel, and so on). Badges or pins produced before Sept. 2, 1993 are exempt. Anything designed or redesigned after that date must comply.

Committees planning to make a badge or pin must get prior written approval. The chairman submits a written request to the management coordinator that includes a sketch or artwork and a full description with specifications. Management staff evaluates it and notifies the chairman of the decision.

## Show badge and parking benefits

Badges and parking permits are non-transferable and for exclusive use. Loaning, giving, or selling them is prohibited and grounds for forfeiture and possible removal. Duplicating or reproducing them without written permission may lead to prosecution or permanent expulsion.

Badge distribution. Committee badges are distributed from the Membership Office on the second floor of Reliant Center, or at pre-approved committee meetings. Board of directors' badges are available in November at the Membership Office windows (ex-officio board members' badges are available in January). Other pickup start dates are committee-specific. Check with the Membership Office or committee leadership.

Valid photo identification must be presented at pickup, and individuals must sign a receipt for the badge and parking and acknowledge the benefits. Before a badge can be released: Show dues must be paid, a current indemnity agreement must be on file, the member handbook must be acknowledged, and legal name verification must have taken place. Committee dues payment may be mandatory before badge pickup (check with leadership). Any special committee requirements must be fulfilled, and the chairman or staff coordinator must authorize the badge. A completed Conflict of Interest questionnaire must be on file for board member badges.

A volunteer may pick up another volunteer's badge only with a completed pickup authorization form (online account entry or the official form from the Membership Office). The designated person must provide photo ID, which is photocopied and placed on file for that Show.

Volunteers should pick up badges and parking passes before the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest begins. A ticket or credential is required to access the Membership Office during that event and the Show.

Gold badge privileges:

- Gold committeeman badges shall not be issued to or worn by individuals under 21. Gold guest badges have no minimum age. Gold JRC badges go only to Jr. Rodeo Committee members (ages 17 to 20).
- Allows the badge holder and one guest admission to three things. First, the Reliant Stadium main concourse for each rodeo/concert performance, March 2 to March 21, 2027 (seating is not guaranteed, gold badge holders and their guest must leave seats when the ticket holder arrives, and refusal is grounds for forfeiture and possible removal). Second, the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest, Feb. 26 to Feb. 28, 2027. Third, the livestock show, carnival, horse show, and commercial exhibits areas.
- Excludes Rodeo Uncorked! events and any other invitation-only or ticket-only events.
- Does not provide parking.
- Does not qualify the holder for special discounts from commercial exhibit vendors.

Silver badge privileges:

- No minimum age requirement.
- Allows the badge holder and one guest admission to: the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest, livestock show, carnival, horse show, and commercial exhibits areas.
- Excludes admittance into Reliant Stadium for rodeo/concert performances, Rodeo Uncorked! events, and any other invitation-only or ticket-only events.
- Does not provide parking.
- Does not qualify the holder for special discounts from commercial exhibit vendors.

The Show may modify badge access to meet health and safety guidelines. Neither badge provides parking, admission to restricted areas, or seating in handicap areas, even if unoccupied. Admission to the Show is subject to the terms and conditions at rodeohouston.com.

Lost or stolen badges and parking permits. May be replaced by submitting a request through a committee designee. The volunteer contacts their committee's designee, who submits an official replacement request through Chairman Tools. The request must be approved by the current chairman, officer-in-charge, staff coordinator, management coordinator, membership office, and Show president. The Show is not obligated to replace lost or stolen items. If a replacement is granted, a fee of $50 is charged per item, picked up at the Membership Office.

## Parking

## Committee meetings and events

Scheduling meetings. The chairman works with the OIC and staff coordinator to set the calendar for the upcoming year immediately after each Show. Meetings are only scheduled for the current Show year. Do not submit meeting/event requests (including Show wrap-up meetings) until the current Show has concluded.

Showtime meeting requests. Can be submitted starting August 26 and no earlier. All Showtime requests must be in by Thanksgiving. Confirmations cannot be sent until most or all Showtime requests are in. Mission-driven events (judging contests, auctions) get the highest scheduling priority. Committee parties are last to be confirmed and are scheduled by submission date (first come, first served).

Reliant Park meetings. Reliant Center is a non-smoking facility, including meeting rooms, offices, restrooms, and stairwells. Schedule meetings at least three weeks in advance and plan all Reliant Park meetings through the staff coordinator. Do not directly contact Aramark or Legends Global. Turnaround for a room confirmation is a minimum of three weeks from when the staff coordinator submits the online request (longer for Showtime). Submit three specific date options per request (specific dates, not date ranges). Dates are tentative until 90 days prior to the event, and meeting space is tentative until 30 days prior. Legends Global events can move a meeting up to 90 days prior, so any confirmation before 90 days is tentative. Before 90 days, the date is held but the location may still move. Schedule only necessary meetings with clear objectives, such as an orientation/initial planning meeting, a progress meeting, and a final wrap-up meeting. Room dimensions and layouts are at nrgpark.com/nrg-center. Include time for decorating and setup, registration, the meeting, and hospitality and music on the request. A room can be set up early, but only 30 minutes prior to start is guaranteed. January and February are busiest, and not all events can be accommodated. Blackout dates cannot be booked (the staff coordinator has the list). The staff coordinator posts the meetings to the committee home page and ensures setup. If the location, date, or time changes, notify the staff coordinator. Setup crews leave at 3 p.m., so last-minute changes must be done before then.

Off-site meetings. Must be scheduled and confirmed with the staff coordinator and placed on the Show's calendar. The schedule is tentative until the staff coordinator confirms all dates, times, and locations.

Meeting agenda. Call the meeting to order (always start on time). Introduce the officer-in-charge, who introduces special guests in this order: chairman of the board, chairman of the board elect, then the president. To save time, acknowledge these as a group rather than by name: Executive Committee members, other officers, lifetime vice presidents, directors, lifetime directors, management, and staff. Do not interrupt to introduce late arrivals. Be mindful of speaking dignitaries' limited time. Briefly state the reason for the meeting, keep it moving, talk to the group as a whole, retain control without stifling discussion, invite constructive criticism, report on or call for prior reports, ask for unfinished business, discuss new business, make special announcements, then adjourn.

Correspondence. Any printed, emailed, or distributed materials (applications, forms, letters, newsletters, flyers, invitations) must be sent to the staff coordinator for approval. The staff coordinator coordinates final approval with the appropriate departments.

Letterhead. The Show provides standard committee volunteer letterhead for official correspondence, available from the staff coordinator. Committees may not develop their own letterhead. To identify itself, a committee may place its name under "Committee Volunteer" at the top of the letterhead.

## Invitation guidelines

Invitations should cover:

- Who: Identify the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo (in writing or logo form), the committee and/or individuals hosting, and who is invited. Clarify if a guest is allowed.
- What: Describe the event type and reason, and give the name and phone number of a contact for more information.
- When: Provide date and time (example: Saturday, Jan. 4, 2025 7 p.m.).
- Where: Provide the location, including facility name, address with ZIP (unless Reliant Stadium, Arena, or Center), a room number if available, and a phone number. Consider directions or a map. The ZIP lets invitees find a map online or via GPS.
- Parking: If at Reliant Park, indicate specific lots. If elsewhere, indicate whether valet is available and any cost.
- Attire: Indicate the appropriate or preferred category. Black-tie (men in tuxedos, women in evening gowns). Black-tie optional (men may wear tuxedos, women may wear evening gowns or cocktail dresses, dark suits acceptable for men). Cocktail attire (men in suits, women in cocktail-length dresses or dressy pants and jacket/top). Business attire (men in suits and ties, women in business suits/pantsuits or business-appropriate dresses). Business casual (men in slacks and a collared shirt, women in a nice top with pants or skirt). Rodeo casual (jeans or other nice casual wear). You may jazz up the categories ("Western glitz cocktail attire," "haute rodeo casual") but the basic category reference must be included.
- R.S.V.P.: Provide a phone number/email and a deadline to reply.
- Alcohol: Strict Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission rules govern alcohol at events. If the invitation references alcohol or beverage service (cash bar, open bar), the staff coordinator obtains the appropriate approval.
- Money: If the correspondence discusses money, event proceeds, or charitable contributions, the staff coordinator obtains approval from the Financial Division. Examples needing review include "net proceeds benefit the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo" or "contributions may be tax deductible." The standard tax-deductibility wording is: "The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo is a 501(c)(3) charitable organization. The amount of the contribution that may be deductible for federal tax purposes is the excess value contributed by the donor over the value of goods or services received. Consult with your tax professional for more information." If the contributor receives goods or services, the value must be stated (the staff coordinator has the approved list of standard values and gets Financial Division approval). Do not state "Your contribution is tax deductible." If a letter or invitation states a specific use for money, the IRS requires the Show to use it that way. Example: if proceeds are stated to go to the HLSR Educational Fund, then 100 percent must go directly to the Educational Fund, not to committee dues, awards, or operating accounts.
- Colors and Logos: Show and/or committee logos must follow the brand guide in the member section of the website. Corporate or business logos may not be included.

All publicly distributed committee materials must be reviewed by the HLSR Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Department before distribution to check brand standards and writing style. The chairman or designee sends materials to the staff coordinator, who forwards them to the appropriate division.

Sponsors: the words "sponsor," "patron," and "contributor" are reserved for official Show sponsors, patrons, or contributors. "Donor" is the acceptable acknowledgment.

Distribution: invitations to the Executive Committee and officers must be distributed by the Executive Office staff. Allow sufficient mailing time.

## Other meetings and event guidelines

- Animals at events. Committee members cannot take live animals to events without written prior approval of their management and staff coordinator.
- Auctioning of alcohol. It is illegal to auction (silent or live) any alcoholic items for fundraising at any HLSR event (except the HLSR Champion Wine Auction). Strictly enforced by the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission, punishable by confiscation, fine, and/or arrest.
- Bar services. Bar service after a meeting must be scheduled like the agenda (for example, 30 minutes before and after the meeting). Bars should not be open during a meeting. The bar at general committee meetings can be open one hour max. Anything longer requires OIC approval and should be limited to fundraisers, socials, or parties. No bar service without a bartender present. Bartenders will not serve any member showing signs of intoxication and take instruction only from Show management staff and/or the OIC (or, in the OIC's absence, the chairman). Bartenders cannot pour double or triple shots. A high-ranking committee member (chairman or vice chairman) should be present while the bar is open. Bar service may or may not be provided by the Show at off-site events (contact the staff coordinator).
- Casino nights. Allowed as part of an event, but only for fun. No prizes or winnings of any sort. The invitation must state the event is for fun only.
- Catering. Caterers must provide all utensils, since Reliant Center has no on-site kitchen. Cooking outdoors at Reliant Park must be approved by Legends Global (tell the staff coordinator about propane/gas and the cooking vehicle size).
- Decoration. Nothing can be mounted to walls with tape under any circumstances. Balloons, confetti/glitter, and open flames are not allowed at any time. Setup crews leave at 3 p.m.
- Entertainment contracts. Bands cannot be scheduled if they might disrupt other meetings. The chairman or vice chairman gets a Committee Entertainment Contract Request Form from the staff coordinator, completes it, and returns it at least 30 days before the event. The staff coordinator generates a contract using the HLSR template, which is sent to the entertainer for signature. A W-9 must be submitted by every entertainer with their signed contract. All committee entertainment must be on HLSR contracts (the Show does not sign artists' contracts). The staff coordinator and chairman handle all payment requests (send invoices to the management coordinator to code for payment). Entertainment must fit the approved budget. Entertainers may not be paid in cash or by personal check (a Show check is required), and committee members who pay entertainers will not be reimbursed. The entertainer will not be paid if a contract is not fully executed before the performance.
- Leave of absence. A chairman may, but is not required to, offer a leave of absence policy (noted in the committee handbook). It is for eligible members otherwise in good standing whose health or personal circumstances prevent normal participation. The member must have completed at least 3 years of service (the chairman may make exceptions). Covered reasons include illness or injury, birth or placement of a child and bonding, care of a family member with a serious health condition, bereavement, military leave, and any other reason approved by the chairman and officer. A member must give written notice (email) to the chairman as soon as possible, and email intent to return as soon as practical or by a date the chairman sets. Anyone using leave must have paid show dues, will not receive a gold badge or earn a year of service, but their spot is held for one year or until May 1 (whichever is first). The chairman decides whether to remove the member and gives direction in the handbook, including whether committee dues are paid or refunded. If granted leave after the Show starts, the member returns the gold badge to the chairman as soon as possible and all privileges are suspended until the next Show year. Misusing a gold badge while on leave leads to immediate removal at the chairman's discretion.
- Lifetime committeemen leave of absence. A volunteer nearing LTC eligibility who needs leave may (or their chairman may) contact Membership for a special-consideration LTC application. The LTC Review Committee evaluates these case-by-case each June. Current LTCs must stay current on Show and/or committee dues to remain on the LTC and any active roster. The Membership office automatically removes any LTC who does not meet this on May 1. For LTC members, not receiving a year of service does not affect remaining on the LTC. The chairman may submit a Committee Dues Waiver request to Membership if a leave is needed.
- Raffles. HLSR committees or representatives may not hold raffles of any kind at Reliant Park or off-site.
- Sale of firearms in auctions. Firearms may be sold in silent and live auctions at committee and Show fundraisers under these conditions. No handguns. No fully or semi-automatic rifles. Firearms must be 100 percent donated. If displayed, trigger locks must be installed and no ammunition present. Firearms must not change possession at the event. Buyers must agree to a background check (a bill of sale is not sufficient). New firearms: the buyer receives a gift card or certificate to claim the firearm at a licensed dealer, where the background check is done. Used firearms: arrangements are made for the buyer to claim the firearm at a licensed dealer, where the background check is done.
- Speakers and solicitation. Unapproved vendors may not display or sell products or services at committee meetings (at Show offices or off-site). Official Show licensees may, upon invitation by the chairman, attend meetings to sell or take orders for committee-marked merchandise as specified by the chairman. Candidates for political office, their representatives, and supporters may not speak at committee meetings or functions. Election or political pamphlets, signs, flyers, and other materials cannot be distributed at HLSR committee functions or related events at Reliant Park or off-site, or through the Show's online notification system. Non-Show solicitations or literature are not allowed during committee meetings or events (other than pre-approved Show business).

## Legal guidelines

Equal Opportunity. The Show is committed to treating all individuals with respect and dignity, free of discrimination and harassment. It prohibits harassment or discrimination based on gender, race, color, creed, religion, national origin, ancestry, age, mental and/or physical disability, medical condition, sexual orientation, veteran or marital status, or other protected reasons. Harassment is unlawful regardless of the gender of those involved and can apply to conduct at any time and place, not just during official committee service.

Sexual Harassment. Defined as unwanted sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature when submission is a term of service, submission or rejection is a basis for service decisions, or the conduct unreasonably interferes with performance or creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment. A partial list of offensive behavior includes unwanted advances or propositions, offering service benefits for sexual favors, threatening reprisals after a negative response, derogatory comments, epithets, slurs, explicit jokes, or comments about a person's body or dress, verbal abuse of a sexual nature or suggestive or obscene letters, notes, emails, texts, or invitations, and physical conduct such as touching, assault, or blocking movements. The Show investigates reports to establish facts and prevent recurrence. Officers, committee chairmen, vice chairmen, coordinators, assistant captains, and captains must take an online sexual harassment prevention course every two years (Rodeo staff take it annually). Failing to score 80 or better on the end-of-training test means retaking the course. Those required to take it will not receive a badge until they pass.

Other Types of Harassment. Prohibited harassment on the basis of race, color, national origin, ancestry, religion, disability, age, or any protected basis includes verbal conduct (threats, epithets, slurs), visual conduct (derogatory posters, photos, cartoons, gestures), physical conduct (assault, unwanted touching, blocking movement), and retaliation for reporting harassment.

Initiation and Hazing. The Show prohibits any committee initiation or hazing. Hazing is any activity that humiliates, degrades, or risks emotional or physical harm, regardless of willingness to participate. Initiations and hazing, on or off Show grounds, will not be tolerated.

Complaint Procedures. Anyone who feels subjected to or witnesses discrimination or harassment should file a written complaint with their immediate supervisor, committee chairman, the officer assigned to the committee, the general counsel, and/or the Show's Human Resources Department as soon as possible. Officers and chairmen must refer reports of improper behavior to the chief volunteer officer immediately. The complaint should include all relevant details (names of those involved, witnesses, direct quotations, and any documentary evidence). The Show conducts a discreet, thorough, objective investigation and informs the complainant of the result. Corrective action may include termination of employment or removal from committee and HLSR membership. Federal law prohibits retaliation against a good-faith complainant. The Show urges prompt reporting. The complaint procedure does not preclude individuals from telling the offender directly that the behavior is unwelcome.

Liability for Harassment. Anyone found to have engaged in prohibited harassment or discrimination, and any supervisor or committee chairman who knew but took no action, is subject to discipline up to discharge or removal from committee membership. The Show does not consider such conduct within the scope of service, and reserves the right not to defend or pay damages for conduct in violation of this policy.

Additional Enforcement Information. The reporting procedure does not limit an individual's right to seek a remedy under state or federal law by reporting to the appropriate agency. For more information, contact Human Resources or the nearest office of the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC).

Non-Partisan and Non-Political. HLSR is a non-profit precluded from political activity and does not tolerate discriminatory practices. Clothing, merchandise, signage, decor, and public engagement must be non-political and non-discriminatory, appropriate for a public audience of all ages, races, and ethnicities. The Rodeo may remove any such signage, decor, merchandise, or displays and end any activity that detracts from the general character of the Rodeo.

Workplace Violence. Acts or threats of physical violence, including intimidation, harassment, and coercion, that involve or affect the Show, its employees, or volunteers will not be tolerated. This applies to all persons in the Show's operations. Violations may result in discipline up to termination or removal from committee service, and/or legal action. Examples include threats or acts of physical harm to an individual or their family, friends, associates, or property, intentional destruction of Show or personal property, stalking or harassing/threatening phone calls, and conduct resulting in a conviction relating to violence. Anyone who engages in a threat or violent action on Show property will be removed as quickly as safety permits and may be required to stay off the premises pending investigation. Employees and volunteers are prohibited from carrying weapons on Show premises (per NRG Park Policy) and in Show vehicles regardless of location. "Weapon" includes firearms (licensed or not) and any potentially dangerous object not customarily used in duties.

Drug and Alcohol Use. The manufacture, distribution, dispensing, possession, sale, purchase, or use of a controlled substance on Rodeo property is prohibited. Being under the influence of illegal drugs at Reliant Park or on Rodeo property at any time is prohibited. Unauthorized use of prescription drugs and illegal use or abuse of over-the-counter drugs is prohibited. Being under the influence of alcohol at Reliant Park or on Rodeo property during volunteer assigned shifts/specified work hours is prohibited. Violators are subject to discipline, including committee removal. Marijuana or cannabis in all forms is included as an illegal drug. Volunteers will not use, sell, purchase, manufacture, or store any illegal drug, alcohol, or drug paraphernalia (or assist another to do so) while on assigned shifts at Reliant Park or on Rodeo property (exception: alcohol stored by management and Rodeo officials for hospitality in entertaining guests), and will not report to a shift while impaired. Volunteers must comply with the policy, report immediately to their volunteer leader if they are impaired (including by a prescription drug), and report any reasonable suspicion (supported by actual evidence, not rumor) that another volunteer or employee has violated the policy. A volunteer is subject to immediate removal upon any arrest, indictment, or conviction for the use, possession, or distribution of any controlled substance.

Search. Inspections of a volunteer's personal property are part of the Rodeo's security measures. Volunteers are expected to cooperate, as a condition of continued participation, with special drug/alcohol searches of personal vehicles on Rodeo property, purses, clothing, briefcases, or other personal property when there is reasonable suspicion. Searches on Rodeo premises can be conducted at any time.

Coordination with Law Enforcement. HLSR will report information about possession, distribution, or use of illegal drugs to law enforcement and turn over any such substances found during a search. Searches are based on reasonable suspicion, and the Rodeo cooperates fully in prosecution.

Whistleblowers. The Show is committed to a workplace where people can raise good-faith concerns about its business practices, specifically suspected violations of law (including federal laws), truthful information in connection with an inquiry or investigation, and potential violations of Show policy. To report, contact the Show's president or the chairman of the Audit Committee, the general counsel, and/or Human Resources (contact info is on the Show's website). An anonymous message may be left, and reports are confidential. If a report involves an individual holding one of these offices, that person is not contacted and does not participate in the investigation. The Show prohibits retaliation against anyone who raises suspected violations, cooperates in inquiries, or identifies potential violations, and anyone who retaliates is subject to discipline up to termination or removal. A summary of all reports is presented to the Audit Committee of the board.

Sex Offenses. No one shall be employed or allowed to volunteer who has at any time been charged with, convicted of, or pled guilty or nolo contendere to any offense of sexual abuse, solicitation, or molestation, or any offense involving a minor, whether felony or misdemeanor, in any jurisdiction. It is grounds for immediate termination if such a conviction or plea occurs after the person became an employee or volunteer. A conviction with probation or deferred adjudication is considered a final conviction for this policy.

Criminal Charges and/or Convictions. If HLSR has information of a charge or conviction of a crime above a Class C misdemeanor, it may, in its sole discretion, remove the volunteer and internally mark them ineligible to serve on any committee. If the charge is cleared, the volunteer may reapply to join a committee by contacting the membership office at membership@rodeohouston.com.

Contracts. Contracts obligating the Show or any committee must be reviewed by the in-house general counsel and the applicable business unit (marketing, livestock, operations, and so on). Once reviewed and approved, they must be signed by the president or another authorized Show official. Committee members are not authorized signatories and may not sign contracts on the Show's behalf. All contracts must be submitted to the staff coordinator, who oversees approval and returns the contract to the chairman or mails it to the contracting party. At least 10 business days must be allowed for review. Executed contracts are kept on file in the Show's Legal Department, with a copy kept by the staff coordinator.

Show Records. "Records" include but are not limited to contracts, expense reports, and accounting and other financial records. Records must be a fair presentation of the activity, prepared per Show policy to reflect the nature and purpose of the activity, and accurate and true. A committee member authorized to make expenditures must ensure all records comply with the Show's accounting and purchasing policies. Certain records relating to serious incidents or disputes must be preserved by law. In a serious incident or emergency, or if litigation is threatened or a law enforcement investigation begins, all related records should be preserved and the matter reported to the Operations Department and the general counsel.

Show Communications Equipment Usage (No Expectation of Privacy). Members using Show equipment (phones, computers, Internet, mail) must use it ethically and lawfully and understand it reflects on the Show. All Internet data composed, transmitted, or received via Show systems is part of the Show's official records and subject to disclosure to law enforcement or third parties. The Show has the right to monitor and access data on its systems and equipment for legitimate business purposes, and members should have no expectation of privacy with respect to documents, voicemail, email, or any data on Show systems and equipment.

## FAQ

**How much does HLSR membership cost, and what are the types?**
Three kinds. Annual is $50 (ages 21 and up). Junior Annual is $25 (under 21). Both run May 1 to April 30 and renew through your online account. Life is a one-time $500 payment, good for the rest of your life. If you already paid an annual membership this year and want to convert to Life within the same May 1 to April 30 year, you can buy a $450 conversion from your online account or the Membership Office.

**What do I have to do to serve on a committee like the SPC?**
Be a member in good standing, be at least 21 (Jr. Rodeo Committee is the exception), pass a third-party criminal background check, have no delinquent account with the Show, have your membership paid before appointment, and complete the Indemnity Agreement, the member handbook acknowledgement, and all required training each year. Committee dues must be paid before your first committee meeting or before you start work, whichever comes first.

**What do my gold and silver badges actually get me into?**
A gold badge admits you and one guest to the Reliant Stadium main concourse for each rodeo/concert performance (March 2 to March 21, 2027, no guaranteed seating), the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest (Feb. 26 to 28, 2027), and the livestock show, carnival, horse show, and commercial exhibits. A silver badge admits you and one guest to everything except the Reliant Stadium performances. Neither badge includes parking, Rodeo Uncorked! events, or any invitation-only or ticket-only event, and neither gets you commercial exhibit vendor discounts.

**My badge or parking permit was lost or stolen. What now?**
Contact your committee's designee, who submits an official replacement request through Chairman Tools. It must be approved by the chairman, officer-in-charge, staff coordinator, management coordinator, membership office, and Show president. The Show is not obligated to replace it, but if a replacement is granted there is a $50 fee per item, picked up at the Membership Office.

**How do committee meetings get scheduled at Reliant Park?**
Through your committee's staff coordinator, never by contacting Aramark or Legends Global directly. Schedule at least three weeks ahead and give three specific date options (not date ranges). Confirmations take a minimum of three weeks. Dates are tentative until 90 days before the event and the space is tentative until 30 days before. Showtime requests can be submitted starting August 26 and must be in by Thanksgiving.

**Where do badges get picked up, and what has to be done first?**
At the Membership Office on the second floor of Reliant Center, or at pre-approved committee meetings. Before a badge is released you need Show dues paid, a current indemnity agreement on file, the member handbook acknowledged, and legal name verification done. Committee dues may also be required first (check with leadership). Bring valid photo ID and sign for the badge and parking.

**Can I order HLSR business cards or make my own committee letterhead?**
Business cards yes, letterhead no. Order business cards any time through the Business Cards tab in your online account menu (or manually at the Membership Office). Payment is due at order, shipping is included, and delivery via USPS takes 3 to 4 weeks. You cannot make your own letterhead. The Show provides standard committee volunteer letterhead through the staff coordinator, and a committee may put its name under "Committee Volunteer" at the top.
