# SPC history and milestones (from The Heartbeat archive)

**Quick answer:** The Souvenir Program Committee is one of HLSR's four original committees, formed a few years after the Houston Fat Stock Show and Livestock Exposition began. It started as separate men's and women's committees that merged into one in the 1970s, and today it caps at 500 members. Its mission is to raise money for the HLSR educational scholarship fund by selling souvenir program advertising and soliciting donations.

## What this is

A synthesis of the durable history and milestones the Souvenir Program Committee (SPC) has recorded in its newsletter, The Heartbeat, across issues from 2014 through 2024. The newsletter takes its name from HLSR's longstanding description as the "Heartbeat of the Southwest" (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014). Everything below is pulled from those issues, and every fact cites the issue it came from. Where the issues disagree across years, the doc gives the most recent version and notes the change.

## Where the committee came from

The SPC is one of the four original committees formed when HLSR began organizing working committees, which makes it one of the oldest committees in the Show (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014). It was created a few years after the start of the Houston Fat Stock Show and Livestock Exposition (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024).

It did not start as a single committee. There were originally a separate men's committee and a women's committee, and they merged into one committee in the 1970s (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024).

One issue gives a specific founding year of 1978 and describes the committee as raising funds for the souvenir program book that FFA and 4-H students sell outside the stadium (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.1, Jul 2019). The committee's more recent and more frequently repeated framing is the "one of the four original committees, created a few years after the Show began" description above, so treat that as the primary account and 1978 as a single data point.

Today the committee has a member capacity cap of 500 (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024, and Vol. 7.1, Jun 2020). By 2014 its recruiting had already grown membership to over 500 members (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.5, Oct 2014).

## What the committee is for

The mission centers on soliciting ads and donations for the HLSR Souvenir Program (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.6, Jan 2024). The committee raises money by selling advertising in the RODEOHOUSTON souvenir program and collecting donations to benefit the youth of Texas (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.2, Jul 2020). All proceeds the committee raises go to the educational scholarship fund after program production costs (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024, and Vol. 7.1, Jun 2020). The scholarship program helps 4-H and FFA students across Texas attend college and pursue agricultural careers (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.1, Jul 2019).

The committee directly supports the FFA and 4-H groups whose students sell the souvenir programs at the Rodeo (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.2, Sep 2022). It also keeps a sub-committee devoted to physically distributing the souvenir program on NRG grounds during the Rodeo season (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024, and Vol. 7.1, Jun 2020).

## The committee's standing traditions

A few recurring events have become fixtures of the SPC year.

The **Kick-Off Crawfish Boil** opens each season. It is a recurring season-opening tradition and a recruiting event geared toward attracting rookie members (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.1, Jun 2016, and Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014). It doubles as a place members can pick up their badges, and members are invited to bring a guest or potential new member (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.7, Apr 2021).

**Rodeo Prom** is the committee's end-of-year awards banquet, where the year's final ad-sales numbers and committee awards are shared (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.3, Jan 2023). The nickname shows up consistently across the years for the same event (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019, and Vol. 2.1, Jul 2015). It has been held in January and in February depending on the year (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.5, Oct 2016, names January, and Vol. 4.7, Nov 2017, names February).

The committee's annual **Karaoke Contest** began with its inaugural event in 2014, organized by Cynthia Vinson (The Heartbeat Vol. 2.6, Oct 2015).

The committee also names a **Rookie of the Year** each season. Past honorees include Rebecca Bertrand (2012), Terry Richardson (2013), Beth Garney (2014), Rachel Atherton (2015), Greg Turman (2016), and Lori Hill (2017) (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.6, Oct 2017).

## Leadership milestones

The committee is overseen by an Officer in Charge, an HLSR leadership role that turns over periodically. Jeff "Doc" Lewis served three years before being succeeded by Pat Mann Phillips (The Heartbeat Vol. 2.1, Jul 2015), and the Officer in Charge serves a three-year term (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.1, Jun 2018).

The SPC Chairman serves a multi-year term. Keith Powell served as Chairman from 2018 to 2020 (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019). The early newsletters also describe a committee chairman serving a three-year term (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014).

Patricia "Pat" Mann Phillips was the first woman elected to HLSR's Executive Committee (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.1, Jun 2018).

## Broader HLSR history The Heartbeat recounts

The newsletter regularly carried Show history alongside committee news. These are HLSR milestones, not SPC ones, but they show up in the archive.

The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo began in 1932, conceived during the Great Depression by a group of cattlemen at the Texas State Hotel who planned to sell cattle to promote the Texas Gulf Coast cattle industry (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017). The first Rodeo lasted 5 days and drew 2,000 visitors. It was originally called the Houston Fat Stock Show and was held at the Democratic Convention Hall until 1937 (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017).

Bill Bailey, born Milton Odom Stanley, became the "Voice of the Rodeo" announcer and originated the idea of adding a BBQ contest to the Show, which grew to host 250 cooking teams and nearly 250,000 guests (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.2, Jul 2017).

The Saltgrass Trail Ride is the oldest trail ride that participates in the Show. It made its first ride into Houston in 1952, when four men left Brenham, Texas on horseback (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.3, Sep 2014).

In 1966 the Show moved from the Sam Houston Coliseum to the Astrodome, with an opening attendance of 25,340 (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.3, Sep 2014).

George Strait's March 17, 2013 concert-only performance set an all-time paid attendance record for the Show with 80,020 people, also a record for any event held in the stadium (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.2, Aug 2014).

Charley Pride performed at RODEOHOUSTON 36 times across 14 years and is a member of HLSR's Star Trail of Fame (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.4, Sep 2016).

As of the mid-2010s, the Show brought together roughly 32,000 volunteers across all its committees (The Heartbeat Vol. 2.6, Oct 2015), and the 2022 Rodeo marked the organization's 90th anniversary (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.7, Apr 2021).

## FAQ

**How old is the Souvenir Program Committee?**
It is one of HLSR's four original committees, created a few years after the Houston Fat Stock Show and Livestock Exposition began (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024, and Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014). One issue puts its founding at 1978 (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.1, Jul 2019), but the recurring account is the "original four committees" one.

**Why were there once two committees?**
The SPC began as a separate men's committee and women's committee. They merged into a single committee in the 1970s (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024).

**How many members can the committee have?**
It caps at 500 (The Heartbeat Vol. 11.1, Sep 2024).

**Why is the newsletter called The Heartbeat?**
After HLSR's longstanding description as the "Heartbeat of the Southwest" (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014).

**What is Rodeo Prom?**
The committee's end-of-year awards banquet, where the final ad-sales numbers and committee awards are shared (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.3, Jan 2023).
