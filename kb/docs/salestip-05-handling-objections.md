# Sales Tip #5: Handling Objections

**Quick answer:** Once you ask for the order, most prospects won't say yes the first time. They'll ask a question or raise an objection, which is just a request for more information said with some emotion. Stay calm, paraphrase the objection as a question, get the prospect to agree to it, answer it, and ask for the order again. If they stall with "let me think about it," that means "tell me more," so run the qualifying questions and close again.

## What this is

The fifth and final tip in the Rookie sales tip series. It picks up right after the presentation (Tip #4), at the moment you've asked for the order, and walks through what to do when the prospect responds with a question, an objection, or a stall instead of an immediate yes.

## If the prospect says yes

If the prospect says yes, pull out your Advertising Order Form and start filling it out. Don't stop unless or until he or she stops you with a question or an objection.

It helps to have the Category Index Listing of companies available so you can classify the advertiser appropriately. (That listing is attached to the original tip, available for download from your HLSR website, and available for pick up at the next meeting on Tuesday, October 6.)

Remember that you can always call back for anything you need, except the signature. So be sure to get the signature on the forms before you leave the prospect.

## Why people don't say yes right away

In general people will not say yes on your first attempt. People generally want to seem smarter than that, so in all likelihood they will ask you a question. The key to success here is to answer the question and, once again, ask for the order.

## What an objection actually is

Some people will object. An objection is nothing more than a request for more information stated with some emotion.

Some people feel great pressure when asked to make a decision, and that can bring out an emotional response. The key is to remain calm and listen, then paraphrase the prospect's objection in the form of a question. Once the prospect has agreed to the question, answer the question and ask for the order.

A question or an objection is an indication of interest, which makes it your best friend in sales. Don't fear an objection. Welcome it as an opportunity to answer a question and make a sale.

## The fatal alternative close

If the prospect's questions involve whether they can afford the ad, flip to a half-page ad. Show the prospect the ad and ask whether he or she would like the half-page ad in color or black and white. If the prospect says color, go ahead and complete the Advertisers Form.

This either-or close is called the fatal alternative close. By answering either choice "a" or choice "b," the prospect is still buying.

## When the prospect says "let me think about it"

If your prospect says "let me think about it," what you're actually hearing is "tell me more."

Standard objections take these forms:

- **Delay:** "I want to think about it."
- **Transfer:** "I need to talk to my husband/wife/boss, etc."
- **Excuse:** "We really can't afford it now."
- **Negate:** "I don't think I'm interested right now."

Each one of these is a request for additional information. Before the prospect will listen to you, you have to listen to the prospect and prepare him or her for the additional information.

## The script for handling the stall

Here is how you do it. Start by taking the pressure off:

"Mr. Prospect, I thought you might say that. I certainly would not expect you to make a decision on the spot. Would it be okay if I asked just a few quick questions?"

This statement relieves the pressure the prospect might be feeling by letting him or her off the hook for a decision. The prospect will say yes, and then you can ask the qualifying questions:

- "Do you have a budget for advertising? Do you have a budget for charitable gifts?"

If yes to both:

- "If you were to contribute to the Rodeo, what budget item would it fall under?"
- "Do you feel that the HLSR is a worthwhile charity?"
- "Would you be interested in hearing about the other ways that you might support HLSR?"

Now you have prepared the prospect for additional information. Pull out your FAQ or Fact Card and review some of the information with the prospect. Then close again by asking the closing question:

"Would you rather make a contribution or purchase an ad?"

Once again, wait for the answer.

## The one thing to remember

Always ask for action when you are ready to stop talking. That's it. Fairly simple and easy.

If the prospect won't act, don't antagonize. Just thank the prospect for his or her time and move on to the next prospect. You'll be surprised how well these techniques can work to move the prospect to a yes.

## FAQ

**What is an objection?**
A request for more information, stated with some emotion. It's a sign the prospect is interested, so welcome it as a chance to answer a question and make the sale.

**How do I handle an objection?**
Stay calm and listen. Paraphrase the objection back as a question, get the prospect to agree to that question, answer it, then ask for the order again.

**What's the fatal alternative close?**
An either-or close. When the prospect worries about cost, show them a half-page ad and ask if they'd want it in color or black and white. Either answer means they're still buying.

**What do I do when someone says "let me think about it"?**
Treat it as "tell me more." Take the pressure off by saying you wouldn't expect a decision on the spot, ask permission to ask a few quick questions, run the budget and charity questions, review your FAQ/Fact Card, then close again with "Would you rather make a contribution or purchase an ad?"

**What's the one thing I can't call back for later?**
The signature. You can call back for anything else you need, but get the signature on the forms before you leave the prospect.

**What if the prospect still won't act?**
Don't antagonize them. Thank them for their time and move on to the next prospect.
