# Volunteer Handbook: Financial Operations & Responsibility

**Quick answer:** This is the Show's rule book for committee money. Every expenditure needs advance written approval from your committee chairman, officer-in-charge, management coordinator, and staff coordinator. Purchases over $5K need three arm's length bids and over $25K need a formal RFP. Logo items must come from licensed vendors. Invoices go to your staff coordinator at least five business days before the Thursday check run (check requests at least five days), and reimbursement requests are due within 60 days of the expense.

## What this is

The Financial Responsibility and Operations section of the 2026 Volunteer Handbook. It tells HLSR committee volunteers (including the Souvenir Program Committee) how to budget, spend, raise funds, pay invoices, get reimbursed, and keep accounts in good standing. Failing to follow Show policy on financial responsibility can negatively affect a member's eligibility to serve on a committee, so the rules below are not optional.

## Budgeting

Officers-in-charge and committee chairmen should work with their management coordinator and staff coordinator to budget for each fiscal year.

The Show's fiscal year begins Sept. 1 of the year before the annual event. For example, the 2027 fiscal year begins Sept. 1, 2026.

Staff coordinators give chairmen a budget worksheet in June of each year, one for each pertinent committee account. Proposed budgets are due to the staff coordinator by the assigned deadline. Budgets need to include committee dues accounts, committee operating accounts, committee fundraising accounts, and awards accounts (when applicable).

Budget variances should be documented when identified and approved in advance if possible. Work with your staff coordinator to request approval.

Note: failure to comply with Show policy on fiscal responsibility may negatively affect a member's eligibility to serve on a committee.

## Expenditures

Show volunteers may not make financial commitments for the organization without the advance approval of their chairman, officer-in-charge, and management coordinator. Increased revenues do not constitute approval of increased expenditures.

## Committee accounts (the four fund types)

Officers-in-charge and committee chairmen budget each fiscal year with their management and staff coordinators. There are four types of committee funds, identified by number series.

**Committee Operations (2000s)**

- Expenses paid by the Show to support the operation of the committee.
- Funds do not roll over to the next year. They flow to the bottom line.
- The officer-in-charge or committee chairman may request funds be transferred from a committee operating account to an awards account, but NOT the other way around.
- Operating funds should not be used to reimburse alcohol purchases made by volunteers.

**Awards Funds (4000s)**

- Committee funds solicited to underwrite awards, plus approved inter-fund transfers from committee operation funds.
- Expenses can only include additional awards and prizes (T-shirts, buckles, hats, etc.) to exhibitors and contestants only, not to committee members or judges.
- Rollover is limited to one year's expenses.

**Non-mission Fundraising (5000s)**

- Funds solicited for purposes outside the committee's goals.
- See the Fundraising Policy section for more.

**Dues Accounts (6000s)**

- Committee funds derived from dues or solicited from committee members for purposes other than awards.
- Expenses cannot exceed revenues.
- Contributions can ONLY come from committee members.
- NOT tax deductible.

## How committee funds are administered

The flow is Committee, then Staff Coordinator, then Membership.

- Committee: submit funds collected to the staff coordinator, with an authorized Committee Dues Reconciliation Form, within five days of collection.
- Staff Coordinator: verify the deposit amount and forward the funds and reconciliation form to Membership on the day received.

Requests for committee funds, and requests to purchase goods or services with committee funds, must be made in writing, jointly approved in writing by the committee chairman and officer-in-charge, and submitted to the staff coordinator.

Requests for committee funds must be made at least five days before checks are processed by the Accounting Department. Checks are processed every Thursday.

## Purchasing policy

The purpose is to buy quality products at the best overall value to the Show and to protect the Show's trademarks and logos.

**Purchases of $5,000 or less.** No formal bid process is required. On request, the purchasing department will help verify that prices are competitive.

**Purchases exceeding $5,000.** Require at least three arm's length bids.

**Purchases exceeding $25,000.** The staff coordinator submits a request for the purchasing department to solicit bids to the purchasing director, who prepares a formal request for proposal (RFP) and follows the same procedures as for purchases over $5,000. RFPs over $25,000 are posted on the Show's website for a minimum of 10 business days, inviting potential bidders to participate.

### Submitting requests for purchasing

These purchasing documents are on the membership portal under general committee announcements:

- Request for purchasing department to solicit bids form
- Bid Summary form
- List of Licensed Vendors (including the types of items they offer)

### Step 1: Soliciting bids

- Submit bids to several vendors to get at least three arm's length bids that satisfy the requested need.
- Bids can be solicited by the staff coordinator, committee members, or the purchasing department.
- The staff coordinator submits a Request for Purchasing Department to Solicit Bids form to the purchasing director if they want the purchasing department to solicit the bids.
- If enough qualifying bids are not received by the deadline, the purchasing department works with the requester to ensure the integrity of the process.

### Step 2: Bid summary creation and approval

- If bids are solicited by a staff coordinator or committeeman, the staff coordinator submits the bids to the purchasing department, which completes a bid summary and submits it for approvals.
- If the purchasing department solicits the bids, it completes the bid summary and reviews it with the staff coordinator and the appropriate approvers to recommend a vendor.
- If the lowest bid is not recommended, the bid summary should explain why.
- To protect the integrity of the process, DO NOT share bid information with others, especially not other vendors.
- All purchases over $5,000 must be reviewed and approved by the applicable division head and the purchasing department before anyone places an order.
- If a purchase is not approved by the division head, the purchasing department works with the staff coordinator and division head to resolve issues and resubmit.
- Once approved, the purchasing department sends the approved bid summary (scanned copy) to the staff coordinator. The person who solicited the bids is responsible for notifying both the winning and losing bidders of the outcome. The notice to the winning vendor also includes the staff coordinator's contact information so the transaction can proceed.
- NOTE: any business transaction involving a voting member of the board of directors (a company he or she owns or manages) must be approved by the Executive Committee before a purchase is made.

### Step 3: Making the purchase

Once the bid summary is approved, the staff coordinator is responsible for:

- Ordering the goods or services from the vendor, including details (deadlines, delivery instructions, menus, quantities, location, date, time, etc.).
- Accepting delivery and handling related issues (quantities received, breakage, returning unsatisfactory goods, etc.).
- Working with the Show's general counsel's office to obtain any necessary additional legal documents (service agreement, performance contract, etc.).

### Step 4: Submitting the invoice for payment

The staff coordinator should submit an invoice to the Accounting Department upon receipt.

### Exemptions from the bid process

The Show reserves the final decision on what is exempt. These include, but are not limited to:

- Certain Entertainment. All entertainment contracts for headline acts are negotiated and processed through the Entertainment and Concert Production Department.
- Sole Source. Specialized, unique, or proprietary items or services. This also includes venue rentals.
- Calamity. Purchases required to relieve the necessities of the Show or to preserve Show property.
- Health and Safety. Items needed to preserve or protect the public health or safety of committee members, staff, exhibitors, vendors, visitors, and guests.
- Operating Supplies. Items consumed during normal operations, such as office supplies, paper products, lumber, and shop supplies.

## Licensed vendors (items with logos)

All committee logos are the property of the Show and are treated the same as Show logos and trademarks. They cannot be altered or changed (for example, omitting certain verbiage off a committee logo).

Any item purchased by a committee with a Show logo, Show trademark, or committee logo must be bought from a Licensed Vendor who has a current executed trademark licensing agreement with the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, regardless of the purchase amount. Only items given to Show contestants or exhibitors by the Show are excluded (for example, RODEOHOUSTON saddles).

A current list of licensed vendors is on the member login page: https://secure.rodeohouston.com/Membership/Home

The staff coordinator is responsible for verifying that all designs, layouts, and use of Show logos or trademarks are approved by the HLSR Marketing Department before any items are ordered.

### Reminders

- Transactions of $2,500 or more involving a member of the board or their immediate family require prior approval of the Executive Committee. Immediate family includes spouses, siblings, children and grandchildren, parents and grandparents, and in-laws.
- All vendors asked to bid should get the same details and specifications on the goods or services at the same time.
- All bid responses should include the vendor's signature and submission date. At NO time should a bidder be given another bidder's pricing.
- Vendors are not allowed to change their prices after submitting a bid if the specifications have not changed. If specifications change, each bidding vendor is notified and given an opportunity to bid on the new specifications.

## Fundraising policy

A committee holding a fundraiser must follow all guidelines and provide all required information to the Show. To give a donor a potential tax deduction, the donor must be provided with the required information.

- Invitations, flyers, and notices for an event must include the ticket price, what is being provided at the event (dinner, appetizers, drinks, cash bar, etc.), and the standard IRS charitable language. When the invitation is reviewed for approval, this language will be provided and added to the invitation.
- Committees may engage Corral Club Inc. to provide alcohol for an event, following the guidelines set by the Hospitality Department. HLSR is not licensed to sell alcohol, so its committees are prohibited from generating revenue through the sale of alcohol at committee events.
- Payments for tickets to an event must go to the staff coordinator, not to a committee member. This protects the Show, its volunteers, and its donors.
- Auctions (live or silent). Items to be auctioned must be donated, not purchased. Show funds should not be used to purchase items to resell. (The printed handbook drops the word "not" here, which contradicts the same sentence's "must be donated, not purchased" rule. The intended meaning is that Show funds may not be used to buy resale items.) (See the prior handbook sections Sale of Firearms in Silent or Live Auctions and Meetings and Events.) The committee must give the Show the results of the auction, including donor name, address, amount paid, the item purchased, and the fair market value of the item (the FMV must be provided by the company or individual that donated the item).
- Consigned items cannot be included in auctions.

This information is mandatory so the Show can meet IRS requirements to provide the donor with a tax letter.

### Taxability of proceeds from committee fundraisers

Whether proceeds are taxable depends on more than just the organization's 501(c)(3) status. To stay compliant with state sales and use tax laws, each committee fundraiser is subject to the following:

1. Proceeds should be identified and recorded as one or more of: a) ticket proceeds, b) auction revenue, c) other donations or underwriters.
2. Ticket proceeds are evaluated to determine eligibility for a donation event. a) Ticket price is at least 200 percent of the supported fair market value of the event.
3. Ticket proceeds that aren't donations are then evaluated for exemption as an amusement event or deemed taxable as a food event: a) exempt amusement events will be tax-free, b) food events will charge 8.25 percent and include notification on the flyer or registration form.
4. Auction revenue is tracked by item, with new fields required in documentation: a) donor-identified source of fair market value, b) taxability of item, c) calculation of proceeds vs fair market value, d) assessment of sales tax on taxable items without sufficient documentation.

Complete the Committee Event Checklist for Taxability of Proceeds to determine taxability before the event. Complete the Item Sales Report to document auction results, if applicable. Submit completed forms to the staff coordinator.

### Committee dues

Committee dues are for the benefit of the committee, so they are not tax-deductible donations to the Show. Committees should not "fundraise" for their committee dues account. If a committee wants an event to increase funds in the dues account, only the committee member, their significant other or guest, and the committee's officer-in-charge can be invited. The invitation or flyer should state that items purchased at the event are not tax-deductible donations. Companies and individuals outside the committee may not be solicited for any donations, including cash or in-kind auction items. All auction items must be donated by committee members and are not tax-deductible donations. Committee dues cannot be used to purchase auction items. Consigned auction items are not allowed. Committee dues are non-refundable.

### Non-mission fundraising (5000 funds)

These rules apply to committees fundraising outside the committee's purpose. They do not apply to committees fundraising for 4000 awards funds or Metro Go Texan events.

Each committee decides whether to fundraise outside its mission, and if so, what for (scholarship, auction purchase, etc.).

- Funds cannot be donated to an outside organization.
- Participation MUST be voluntary. Committees cannot require members to donate.

Committees are limited to ONE fundraiser per year:

- A committee can have ONE event OR sell ONE merchandise item.
- A merchandise item can only be sold to that committee's members, not to the general public. The item needs to be of de minimis value per the IRS.
- Only the Show's Merchandise Committee is authorized to sell to the general public.
- People outside the committee can be invited to a fundraising event.

Committee buying group rules:

- Cannot bid on or participate in the Grand or Reserve Grand Champion lots.
- Sales committee members cannot get sales credit from purchase commitments from committee buying groups.
- The committee chairman has the final decision on which auction to participate in.
- Buying groups will not be invited to any buyer appreciation parties but may be eligible for invitation to the Scholarship Picnic, depending on purchase level.
- Donor benefits, if accepted by the committee buying group, must benefit the whole group or committee. They may not be given to or kept by individual committee members. Examples: a meat package accepted and used for a committee party over the summer, or a hide or buckle accepted and displayed in the committee tent or office during Show.
- Committee dues funds may not be transferred to buying group accounts or used to supplement buying group obligations unless solely to secure meat for committee events.

Committee buying groups seeking donations from people who are not Show members will be required to collect all information needed to set up a customer account if a donation is $75 or greater. All forms, flyers, and invitations must be approved by the Financial and Marketing, Public Relations and Presentations Divisions before use.

## Purchasing for approved events

All committee expenditures must be approved in writing in advance of the activity by the committee chairman, officer-in-charge, management coordinator, and staff coordinator.

The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo is generally exempt from paying sales tax for show operations, so in many cases sales tax can be avoided. HLSR's tax exemption generally cannot be used for purchases from committee dues accounts.

Tax exemption forms should be requested from the staff coordinator. The staff coordinator requests a signed form from Accounts Payable and will need the vendor name, vendor address, item being purchased, and the purpose of the item.

Not all expenses are tax exempt, and Accounting can help determine if a form can be issued. Tax exempt forms should be issued and signed by the Accounts Payable Supervisor, Controller, or CFO.

Submit the original invoices to the Show. Invoices must be addressed to the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo at either the Show's physical or mailing address. Invoices addressed to Show members, committees, employees, or to non-Show addresses cannot be paid by the Show. DO NOT use petty cash or event revenues to pay expenses.

## How to pay an invoice

Reminder: before purchasing goods or services, the committee member must secure approval from the committee chairman, officer-in-charge, management coordinator, and staff coordinator.

Invoices submitted for payment MUST contain:

- Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo
- The Show's physical address
- Attention to the committee's staff coordinator
- The committee name that purchased the items
- Contact information for the volunteer who was previously approved to make the purchase on the Show's behalf
- A list of each item or service purchased (quantity, price per item, extended price, etc.)
- The total invoice amount

Submit the invoice to the staff coordinator, who codes it and forwards it to the Accounting Department for payment. The Show cannot pay invoices addressed only to the committee or volunteer. Submit at least five business days before checks are processed by Accounting. Checks are generally processed every Thursday.

## How to request a check

If the vendor will not invoice the Show, a check can be obtained to pay the vendor at the point of purchase or service.

Reminder: before purchasing, get approval from the committee chairman, officer-in-charge, management coordinator, and staff coordinator.

- Complete a check request form, available from staff coordinators or the Accounting Department.
- Attach supporting documentation to the check request (the approval to purchase, the vendor quote, etc.).
- Submit the check request to the committee chairman and officer-in-charge for approval.
- Submit the approved check request and supporting documentation to the staff coordinator.
- The staff coordinator adds the proper coding and forwards it to the Accounting Department.
- Submit the check request to the staff coordinator at least five days before checks are processed. Checks are generally processed every Thursday.

## Collecting money at events

The only forms accepted by the Membership Department are the forms included in the back of the handbook and distributed by the Accounting Department. No other years' forms will be accepted. For dues and all other funds collected at committee events or by committee members, follow these procedures.

### Payments

If required, issue a receipt to the member using a pre-numbered receipt book. The receipt should include the member name, member number, amount paid, method of payment, and type of payment (for example, Horse Show Committee dues). Receipt books come from staff coordinators or the Accounting Department. If you make an error on a receipt, void it and keep all copies in the receipt book.

- For 6000 accounts, log the payment on the committee deposit reconciliation form. This form is available electronic or paper from staff coordinators or the Accounting Department. Make sure all information is included.
- For 5000 account payments, contact the staff coordinator or the Accounting Department for the necessary forms.

At the end of the event, the person collecting the money totals the form. If cash or check, verify the cash and checks collected against the form totals. Then sign the form and attach one set of receipts. Retain a copy of the deposit reconciliation form for the committee's records. For security reasons, DO NOT copy checks.

Submit the collected receipt book, deposit reconciliation form, and money to the staff coordinator as soon as possible, and no more than five business days after the payment is collected.

If a committee needs to take credit card payments at events, a credit card machine must be checked out from the staff coordinator (see the Wireless Credit Card Machine section below).

Keep all payments and documents in a SECURE location. This does NOT include cars, purses, or briefcases. If it is lost or stolen, the Show will require a police report.

### How to submit paperwork

If the event is onsite:

- During normal business hours, deliver the paperwork to the staff coordinator's office.
- During membership window hours, send members and volunteers to the membership window to make payments.
- Members who know their online account login can pay at the membership kiosk.
- After business hours, put the paperwork in a deposit bag or envelope marked with the committee's name and staff coordinator's name, and drop it in the night deposit box (located in the main hall of Reliant Center, just past the ticket window). Deposit bags are available from staff coordinators or the Accounting Department.

If the event is offsite:

- Use a pre-addressed, prepaid Federal Express envelope to send the paperwork to the Show offices. The envelopes are available from staff coordinators or the Accounting Operations Department.
- Drop it in the night deposit box after normal business hours or on weekends when Reliant Park is open.

### Wireless credit card machine procedure

To use the Show's credit card machines for an offsite event, a committee must reserve the machines in advance through the staff coordinator. Users must know and comply with the Show's Credit Card Payment Policy and Guidelines. The following items must be returned to the Accounting Department within three business days of the event date:

- The credit card machine or machines
- All forms or documents that contain credit card information (table, ticket, registration)

For security, do NOT copy any of the above documents, receipts, or reports. If the committee needs copies of the Totals Report or Settlement Report, copies will be at the Show's offices. Failure to comply will result in the committee's forfeiture of credit card machine privileges.

## Membership and committee dues

Committee members are encouraged to use the Show's online tools to pay membership and committee dues online. Other options are at the Show membership windows or by mail sent directly to the Show office. The membership kiosk at the top of the escalator in Reliant Center, from September to February, has computers for online payments. Membership Committee members supervising the kiosks cannot accept payments for membership renewals or committee dues. Committee dues are non-refundable.

## Expense reimbursements

All committee expenditures must be approved in writing in advance by the management coordinator, committee chairman, and officer-in-charge. The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo is generally exempt from paying sales tax, so sales tax will not be reimbursed in most cases unless the expense is reimbursed from a committee dues account. The tax-exempt number and exemption forms are available from staff coordinators or the Accounting Department. All reimbursement requests are due to the Accounting Department within 60 days of the date of expenditure.

Please note:

- Committee members are encouraged to use the Show's purchasing power by using licensed vendors and the Show's purchasing process instead of making individual out-of-pocket purchases.
- Order necessary office supplies from staff coordinators.
- Plan ahead. Time purchases and paperwork submission appropriately.
- Out-of-pocket expenses cannot be reimbursed through petty cash.
- The Show does not reimburse volunteers for mileage expense.
- The Show will not reimburse tips at restaurants greater than 20 percent of the total bill.
- Reimbursement requests are subject to all the financial policies in the Volunteer Handbook.
- If receipts and documents do not support the full reimbursement amount requested, the request will be returned for editing.

### To receive reimbursement

Complete the Committeeman's Expense Reimbursement Form, available in electronic or paper format from staff coordinators or the Accounting Department:

- Tape the original, itemized receipts to a letter-sized piece of paper and attach to the expense form. Original receipts are preferred. Legible scanned copies are acceptable.
- A credit card authorization slip is not an itemized receipt.
- If the documentation is an invoice, proof of payment should be provided (invoice marked as paid, credit card charge, etc.).
- If the receipt is lost or incomplete, some other proof of payment is required (a card or bank statement with sensitive and irrelevant information blacked out).
- Submit the completed expense form to the committee chairman and officer-in-charge for their signatures. It is the volunteer's responsibility to obtain both signatures, though they can ask the committee's coordinator to help facilitate approvals.
- The chairman's and officer's preferred method of approval should be discussed with their coordinators each year, so volunteers and coordinators know whether digital signatures and emailed approvals are accepted. Regardless of method, all volunteer expense reimbursements must be approved by both the chairman and the officer of that committee before payment is processed.
- Once approved and signed, submit the expense form to the staff coordinator at least five days before checks are processed. Checks are processed every Thursday.
- When circumstances prevent the steps above, a separate note acknowledging the departure from policy will be requested, along with approval from the chairman, officer, and president.

Committees wishing to purchase carnival tickets or merchandise for use at committee parties or as door prizes should email their staff coordinator to request these items. Do not buy these items out of pocket from the Show and then submit a reimbursement request. Requesting them lets the Show record the transfer correctly, and individuals will not have to spend their own money.

### Completing the expense form

At the top of the form, write the name of the person to be reimbursed, their address, and the committee name. Indicate whether the person wants the check mailed to the address listed or held at the Show offices for pick-up. Work with your staff coordinator for pick-up arrangements.

- Column 1: record the date the expense was incurred.
- Column 2: for purchases, provide the vendor name and the item purchased. For meals, list who was there and the business purpose.
- Column 3: provide pertinent, detailed information about the expense.
- Column 4: leave blank so the staff coordinator can add the proper account code.

## Managing Show accounts

Committee membership requires being a Show member in good standing. Dues must be paid, and there should be no delinquent accounts with the Show.

For questions about accounts with the Show, contact the Accounting Department at 832.667.1099. Annually, members with delinquent accounts are reported to the committee chairman, officer-in-charge, and Show management, and are subject to removal from committees and placement on the ineligible list.

### Payments to the Show

- Use the online system to make payments.
- When sending a payment, note the member number on the payment. The Show's accounting system is driven by member numbers rather than customer names, which helps eliminate duplicate accounts.
- Include the invoice number or reason for payment (send the remittance portion of the invoice or statement, or note it on the memo line of the check).

### Returned checks

- If a check written to the Show is returned for insufficient funds, contact the Accounting Department immediately to make payment arrangements. Payment for returned checks must be remitted directly to the Accounting Department.
- There is a $25 fee for all returned checks. This fee must be remitted directly to the Accounting Department, regardless of the purpose of the original check.
- Members with more than one insufficient-fund check in a year may be deemed ineligible to serve on a committee.

### Matching gift contributions

All matching gift forms and payments must be sent directly to the Accounting Department. Matching gifts may not be used to purchase items from the Show or to pay balances on a Show account unless specifically allowed by the company (company rules must be provided).

Procedures:

- Obtain the Matching Gift Form from the employer and complete the employee section.
- If the form requests contact information for the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, list Stannicia Reddick as the contact at the Show's mailing address. Do not list a committee member or any address other than the Show's address.
- All matching gift acknowledgments and payments must be sent directly to the Accounting Department.
- The matching gift must be acknowledged by an employee of the Show's Accounting Department. Committee members cannot sign on behalf of the Show.
- The Show will only complete forms for paid amounts, not for commitments to pay.

## Incentive awards and gifts

An IRS Form 1099 will be issued to anyone receiving an award, or several smaller awards, with a total value of $600 or more (for example, a committee incentive award). The recipient is responsible for paying the appropriate income tax on that item.

Chairmen or their designees are responsible for providing the Show with completed Gift and Award Acknowledgment forms for items more than $100. The Show accumulates these and issues a 1099 to those who received more than $600 in value.

To comply with IRS rules, a Gift and Award Acknowledgment form should be completed before an award or gift is delivered. All completed forms should be turned in to the staff coordinator.

## FAQ

**Who has to approve a committee purchase before I make it?**
Get advance written approval from your committee chairman, officer-in-charge, management coordinator, and staff coordinator before you spend anything. Increased revenue does not mean you can spend more.

**When do I need to get bids, and how many?**
A purchase of $5,000 or less needs no formal bid process. Anything over $5,000 needs at least three arm's length bids. Anything over $25,000 needs a formal RFP through the purchasing director, posted on the Show's website for at least 10 business days.

**We want to buy items with the committee or Show logo. Where can we buy them?**
Any item with a Show logo, Show trademark, or committee logo must come from a Licensed Vendor with a current trademark licensing agreement with HLSR, no matter the dollar amount. The current vendor list is at https://secure.rodeohouston.com/Membership/Home. Logos cannot be altered.

**How do I get reimbursed for something I paid for out of pocket, and what's the deadline?**
Complete the Committeeman's Expense Reimbursement Form, tape your original itemized receipts to a letter-sized sheet, get both the chairman and officer to sign, then submit to the staff coordinator at least five days before the Thursday check run. All reimbursement requests are due to Accounting within 60 days of the expense. The Show does not reimburse mileage, does not reimburse restaurant tips over 20 percent of the bill, and generally does not reimburse sales tax unless it's from a committee dues account.

**Is our committee fundraiser taxable, and how often can we fundraise?**
It depends on more than the 501(c)(3) status. Ticket, auction, and donation proceeds each get evaluated. Food events charge 8.25 percent sales tax and note it on the flyer or registration form. Amusement events can be tax-free. A committee is limited to one non-mission fundraiser per year (one event or one merchandise item sold only to members). Complete the Committee Event Checklist for Taxability of Proceeds before the event.

**How do I pay or get a check to a vendor?**
If the vendor invoices the Show, the invoice must be addressed to the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo at the Show's physical address, attention your staff coordinator, with the committee name, the approved purchaser's contact info, a line-item list, and the total. Hand it to your staff coordinator at least five business days before the Thursday check run. If the vendor won't invoice the Show, complete a check request form (from staff coordinators or Accounting), attach the approval and quote, get the chairman and officer to approve, and submit to the staff coordinator at least five days before the check run. Never pay with petty cash or event revenue.

**What happens if my check to the Show bounces?**
Contact the Accounting Department right away to arrange payment. There is a $25 fee for any returned check, paid directly to Accounting regardless of the original check's purpose. More than one insufficient-fund check in a year may make a member ineligible to serve on a committee.
