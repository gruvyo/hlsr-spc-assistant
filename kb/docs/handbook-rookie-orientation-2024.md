# 2024 Rookie Orientation slide deck

**Quick answer:** This is the welcome deck for first-year (Rookie) Souvenir Program Committee members for the 2024 season. It covers what the committee does, the two steps to join (Rodeo membership at $50, then committee dues at $70), how to earn your gold badge ($1,000 for rookies, $2,000 for veterans), ad rates and deadlines, sales tools, awards, and the committee's rules.

## What this is

The 2024 Rookie Orientation slide deck, used to onboard new members of the Souvenir Program Committee (SPC). It's written for rookies (anyone in their first year on the committee), but it's a useful refresher for veterans too. The deck mixes instruction slides with trivia questions and photos. This doc captures the substance of every content slide.

## What the committee does

The Souvenir Program Committee has four jobs:

1. We SELL ad space in the annual Rodeo Houston Souvenir Program.
2. We SOLICIT donations to support the Show's Educational Fund.
3. We DONATE the Souvenir Programs to roughly 70 different local FFA and 4-H clubs.
4. We SUPPORT the clubs in selling the programs onsite during the Rodeo season.

## Committee history (timeline)

A timeline slide lays out the milestones. The slide is a decorative graphic with the event boxes set above and below a horizontal year line, each box joined to its year by a connector stem. The year-to-event pairing below follows those connector stems:

- 1932 - HLSR began.
- 1938 - 1st souvenir program sold.
- 1952 - Advertising Committee created.
- 1957 - 1st scholarship granted.
- 1958 - Advertising Committee becomes the Ladies' Souvenir Program Committee.
- 1962 - Mens' Souvenir Program Committee created.
- 1988 - Ladies' and Mens' committees combine, forming the Souvenir Program Committee.
- 2020 - Rodeo shuts down mid-season due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- 2021 - 1st year without a Rodeo due to the global pandemic.
- 2022 - Rodeo Houston celebrates 90 years. A grand champion steer named "Vanilla Ice" breaks the record sale at $1 million.

## How to join (the two steps)

### Step 1: Become a member of HLSR

- Log onto www.RODEOHOUSTON.com and create your profile.
- Select Souvenir Program from the Committee drop-down.
- Under Sales Person, select the name of the Membership Committee member who is signing you up.
- Under Desired Committee, select SOUVENIR PROGRAM.
- Pay your Rodeo membership dues ASAP ($50).

### Step 2: Get accepted onto the committee

- Patience is a virtue.
- Receive the Rodeo welcome email.
- Pay your committee dues ($70) via www.RODEOHOUSTON.com.
- The deadline to pay dues is October 1, 2023.

Once both steps are done, you're official.

## How the committee is structured (teams)

The committee has a 500-person maximum, so it's split into 9 different internal sales teams, each with its own team captain.

- If you want to be on a certain team, tell one of the Vice Chairs of Sales.
- To switch teams, email your team captain and request the change. They'll make it happen through the proper channels.
- To find out which sales team you're on, check the Dashboard (www.rodeospc.com) or contact one of the Sales Team Vice Chairs.

### Distribution sub-committee

In 2017 the Distribution sub-committee was created for one purpose, to support the 4-H and FFA clubs' program sales each night. Programs are published and given to 4-H and FFA chapters to be sold outside of NRG Stadium, and all the money raised selling programs goes directly back to that chapter. Distribution has limited positions and is only open to veterans (anyone not in their first year on the committee). You can ask how to help out after your rookie year.

## How to earn your gold badge

You earn a badge by hitting a minimum in sales:

- Rookies: $1,000 minimum.
- Veterans: $2,000 minimum.

You can work toward your minimum with both ads and donations. It's cumulative.

## Selling: know your potential advertisers

Build a prospect list from your day-to-day life:

- Friends, businesses, and your daily interactions and appointments.
- Think veterinarian, nail salon, barber, dentist, local coffee shop, mechanic, and the like.
- The USPS brings prospects right to your doorstep. Those businesses are already advertising, and many can reuse their existing artwork.
- Cold calls and walk-ups work too. At a bar or restaurant, ask to speak to the manager about supporting Rodeo Houston.
- Get creative with your social media, email, and call list.

Note: the advertiser or donor does not need to be located in Houston.

## Ad rates (the contract)

### Special Pages

- The first 75 or so pages in the front of the Souvenir Program (easy to find, lots of traffic).
- The inside front and back covers are usually reserved for corporate sponsors.
- Just call your captain to confirm availability.

### Entertainer Pages

- Half-Page: $1,000 value.
- Full-Page: $2,000 value.
- Payment must be collected by September 1st.

## Turn-ins and deadlines

### Ad contract turn-in

- All ad contracts are turned in electronically through the Rodeo Houston website (the link is found on the Dashboard).
- All ad artwork is turned in via the WeTransfer app (download it now).

### Deadlines

- Ad deadline: November 17th, 2023. This gives the Rodeo enough time to publish the program so it's ready for distribution.
- Donation deadline: end of year 2023 (potentially January, to be determined).

## Legal stuff (taxes)

- Ads are NOT tax deductible. Tax advisors may recommend declaring them as a business expense, so tell your advertiser to speak with their tax advisor for guidance.
- Donations ARE tax deductible. A W-9 form is available to download.
- We accept any donation amount. People have badged with individual $20 donations, and they add up.
- Collect payment immediately if the donation is less than $10,000.

## Collecting payment for contracts

Keep an eye on the status of your contracts. Advertisers have the option to "bill upon production" of the book, and it's your responsibility to make sure payment is collected. Once a contract is entered, an invoice is automatically sent to the advertiser. Otherwise the Rodeo will start sending collections notifications to your advertiser, which can affect the release of your badge.

## Sales tools

### The Dashboard (www.rodeospc.com)

A custom private website designed by some of the committee's own members. You must have a Rodeo Member ID. Create your login and you're in.

### Don't recreate the wheel

- SPC Leadership are your best sales tools. There's power in numbers, so team up and get some friendly competition going within your team.
- Ad Frames ($30 each): use them as a thank-you or an incentive. Various sizes are available to match all ad sizes. Demonstrate a frame with a "mock ad."
- Letterhead: a PDF template is available online.
- Business Cards: order through the HLSR website (about $25 for 500). The deck shows a sample card with "rodeohouston.com" and "2022 Committee Volunteer," the member's name and committees, an email address, and a cell number.

## Overcoming objections

- "Don't have budget for it this year" - go over billing options.
- Competitors - acknowledge them.
- Lean on benefiting the Youth of Texas and supporting education.
- If an ad isn't right for them, suggest the ease of a donation instead.
- Don't take a "no" personally. Move on.

## Sales awards

- Rookie of the Year: the rookie with the highest sales gets a special award and recognition at Rodeo Prom.
- Awards eligibility: $2,500 or more in sales (ads and/or donations). Prizes include belt buckles, gift cards, and drawings for cowboy hats, buckles, and more.
- Recruiting: each recruit you bring in who badges increases your award level by 500 points. You must have badged yourself to be eligible.

## Handbook and guidelines

- Protected ads, donations, and sponsorships: no poaching anyone already represented by a committeeman. Check the 2023 Protected SPC Advertisers list before proceeding. If you run across someone who already has a relationship with another committeeman, politely thank them for their service and move along. Any ads you sell this year will be protected for you next year.
- Email and social media use and abuse: be professional. You represent not only the committee but Rodeo Houston as well.
- Your badge is your badge. Don't share it. You earned it.
- Volunteers caught sharing their badge could have it revoked and their volunteer status cancelled.
- The handbook is available on the Rodeo Houston website (after login). Please read it.

## FAQ

**How do I join the Souvenir Program Committee?**
Two steps. First, create your profile at www.RODEOHOUSTON.com, select Souvenir Program from the committee drop-down, pick your Membership Committee sales person, choose SOUVENIR PROGRAM as your desired committee, and pay the $50 Rodeo membership dues. Second, wait for the Rodeo welcome email and pay your $70 committee dues. The dues deadline was October 1, 2023.

**How much do dues cost, and when are they due?**
Rodeo membership dues are $50 and committee dues are $70. The deadline to pay committee dues for this season was October 1, 2023. Both are paid via www.RODEOHOUSTON.com.

**How much do I have to sell to earn my badge?**
Rookies need $1,000 minimum and veterans need $2,000 minimum. You can mix ads and donations, and it's cumulative.

**What are the ad rates and the deadlines?**
Entertainer Pages are $1,000 (half-page) and $2,000 (full-page) in value. Entertainer-page payment must be collected by September 1st. The ad deadline was November 17th, 2023, and the donation deadline was end of year 2023 (possibly January, to be determined). Ad contracts go in electronically through the Rodeo Houston website (link on the Dashboard), and artwork goes in via WeTransfer.

**Are ads or donations tax deductible?**
Ads are not tax deductible (an advertiser's tax advisor may treat them as a business expense). Donations are tax deductible, and a W-9 form is available to download. Any donation amount is accepted, and you should collect payment immediately for donations under $10,000.

**How do I find out which sales team I'm on, or switch teams?**
Check the Dashboard at www.rodeospc.com or contact a Sales Team Vice Chair. To switch, email your team captain and request the change.
