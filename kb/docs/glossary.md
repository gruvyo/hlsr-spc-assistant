# Committee glossary

**Quick answer:** This is a glossary of the terms a Souvenir Program Committee (SPC) volunteer is most likely to run into and ask about. Terms are listed alphabetically. Each definition is plain and faithful to the knowledgebase, and most cite the doc to read for more.

## What this is

A reference list of committee jargon and named events, drawn from across the SPC knowledgebase (the rookie and member handbooks, the Rookie sales tips, The Heartbeat newsletter syntheses, the donor benefit packages, and the dashboard portal pages). Use it to look up a single term. For full context on any item, follow the cited doc.

## Terms

**Advertiser vs donor.** Two different ways a committee member raises money, and both count toward your badge. An advertiser buys ad space in the Souvenir Program (a paid placement, not tax deductible). A donor gives money to the Show's Education Fund (a charitable gift that is tax deductible, with a W-9 available). When an ad is not the right fit for someone, members are encouraged to suggest the ease of a donation instead. (`handbook-rookie-orientation-2024`, `ref-donor-benefits-2026`)

**Awards Banquet.** The committee's end-of-year celebration, nicknamed Rodeo Prom, where the year's final ad-sales numbers and committee awards are shared. See Rodeo Prom. (`heartbeat-traditions-and-events`)

**Badge and badging.** Your badge is your credential for the Show season, and to "badge" means to earn it. To badge each season a member must pay both Show and Committee dues, verify their legal name with the Membership Office, sign the indemnity form, and reach their sales threshold (1,000 dollars for rookies, a total of 2,000 dollars for veterans). Your badge is yours alone, and sharing it can get it revoked. See also Gold badge. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`, `handbook-rookie-orientation-2024`)

**Badge Run.** The push to grow your sales so you reach your badge minimum and climb to an award level. The Rookie sales tip series kicks off the Badge Run. (`salestip-01-prospecting`)

**Committee (Comm) dues.** The dues paid to the SPC itself, separate from Show dues, at 70 dollars a year. They cover the year's meetings and activities, including food and drinks at meetings and the Rodeo Prom celebration. A member who has not paid by the second meeting is removed from the roster. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`)

**Corral Club.** The Show's member hospitality clubs (for example the NRG Center Committeeman's Club and NRG Arena's Stockman's Club), run under Responsible Alcohol Management rules. Guests must be accompanied by their Corral Club host and leave when the host leaves. Some donor benefit tiers include Corral Club drink cards. (`handbook-member-2026`, `ref-donor-benefits-2026`)

**Donor Gold Badge.** A donor benefit badge given at higher donation tiers (one at the 10,000 to 19,999 dollar level, two at the 20,000 dollar Scholarship Donor level), each with a fair market value of 288 dollars. Distinct from the gold badge a member earns by selling. (`ref-donor-benefits-2026`)

**Donor Silver Badge.** A donor benefit badge, with a fair market value of 75 dollars each. Donors at the 2,000 to 9,999 dollar level receive one per 2,000 dollars donated, with larger counts at higher tiers. (`ref-donor-benefits-2026`)

**Frame order.** An order for a printed and framed copy of an ad that ran in the Souvenir Program. Members use the frame order form (5x7 or 11x17 sizes) as a thank-you or sales incentive, often shown to a prospect as a "mock ad." (`form-frame-order-2026`, `handbook-rookie-orientation-2024`)

**Gold badge.** The badge a member earns for the upcoming Show season by badging early rather than procrastinating. Release also requires that all dues are paid, all Show requirements are met, and contact information is updated on the Rodeo website. Released badges are picked up at the membership window inside NRG Center on the second floor, not at committee events. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`)

**The Heartbeat.** The committee's newsletter, named after HLSR's longstanding description as the Heartbeat of the Southwest. Its archive (59 issues, 2014 to 2024) is on the dashboard. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`, `portal-newsletter-archive`)

**Indemnity (indemnity form).** A form every member must sign each season to badge. Skipping the signed indemnity form is the most commonly cited cause of a delayed badge. The agreement also carries notice that HLSR runs sex offender background checks on all committee volunteers. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`)

**Kick-Off Crawfish Boil.** The committee's season-opening event, a combined kick-off and recruiting party geared toward attracting rookies, where members can also pick up their badges and bring a guest or potential new member. (`heartbeat-traditions-and-events`)

**NRG Center.** The building at NRG Park that houses the Show's main offices and the membership window where released gold badges are picked up (second floor). On the dashboard, an event room shown as just a number (for example 604-605) is in NRG Center, and the NRG Center 2nd Floor Map helps locate it. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`, `portal-help-navigation`)

**Prospect.** A qualified suspect. You confirm the name is not already a Souvenir Program advertiser or on the Protected List, then gather their company details (name, title, company, address, phone, fax, website, nature of business). A prospect is a decision maker at an established company. See Suspect. (`salestip-02-converting-suspect-to-prospect`)

**Protected List.** The committee's list of advertisers and donors already represented by a committeeman, used so members do not poach each other's relationships. Check a name against last year's program index and the Protected List before prospecting. If someone is already taken, thank them, encourage them to renew with their protected member, and exit gracefully. Ads you sell are protected for you the next year. (`salestip-02-converting-suspect-to-prospect`, `heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`)

**Reserved space.** Ad placement reserved for a specific spot in the book. On the ad request form, a Reserved Page means placement as the ad ran the previous year, or placement to a specific page number or area if available (Reserved One Page and Reserved Two Pages are priced tiers). In sales terms, being the first and only advertiser in a category is "exclusive space." (`form-ad-request-2027`, `salestip-02-converting-suspect-to-prospect`)

**Rodeo ID.** A member's HLSR Rodeo Member ID. You need one to create your dashboard login, and the dashboard uses Rodeo ID as a search and tracking field for members and recruited prospects. (`handbook-rookie-orientation-2024`, `portal-dashboard-home`, `portal-my-prospects`)

**Rodeo Prom.** The committee's nickname for the year-end Awards Banquet, its season-closing celebration where the final ad-sales numbers and committee awards (including Rookie of the Year) are shared. It has been held in late January or February in different years, so confirm the date against the current season's calendar. (`heartbeat-traditions-and-events`)

**Rookie.** A member in their first year on the committee. Rookies attend a Rookie Orientation and have a 1,000 dollar sales minimum to badge. (`handbook-rookie-orientation-2024`, `heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`)

**Scholarship Donor.** The top published donor tier, 20,000 dollars and above. It carries a larger badge and hospitality package plus scholarship recognition, including a scholarship awarded in the donor's recognition name. Donations above 20,000 dollars get custom benefits arranged with HLSR Donor Relations. (`ref-donor-benefits-2026`)

**Show dues.** The HLSR membership dues a member pays to the Rodeo, separate from Committee dues. They are 50 dollars a year, or 500 dollars for a Lifetime Membership. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`, `handbook-member-2026`)

**Souvenir Program.** The annual RODEOHOUSTON program book the committee sells ads in. After it is produced, all the ad and donation money goes to the Show's educational scholarship fund. The book is sold at the Rodeo by participating area FFA and 4-H groups, who keep the proceeds from their own sales. (`heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`)

**Suspect.** A name on your starting prospect list, before you have qualified them. Build a list of 20 to 30 suspects by asking who you would call for everyday needs, without trying to guess who will buy. Tip #2 covers converting a suspect into a prospect. See Prospect. (`salestip-01-prospecting`, `salestip-02-converting-suspect-to-prospect`)

**Veteran.** A member who is not in their first year on the committee (anyone past their rookie year). Veterans have a 2,000 dollar total sales minimum to badge, and only veterans can join the Distribution sub-committee. (`handbook-rookie-orientation-2024`, `heartbeat-how-the-committee-works`)

**WCBBQ.** The World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest, a Show event. Some donor benefit tiers include parking passes for WCBBQ, and gold and silver badges allow entry for two into the contest. (`ref-donor-benefits-2026`, `handbook-volunteer-2026-donor-program`)

**Wine Garden.** The Champion Wine Garden, the Show's Rodeo Uncorked! wine event (presented by Frost), pouring wines from the RODEOHOUSTON International Wine Competition. The dashboard carries its menu and a layout map. (`menu-wine-garden-2026`, `map-wine-garden-2026`)
