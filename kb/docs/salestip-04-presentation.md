# Sales Tip #4: Presentation

**Quick answer:** Once you've qualified a prospect and they're curious to learn more, you make the presentation. Bring a three-ring binder of sample ads and forms (best if the samples are the prospect's own competitors), then walk through a short script about what the Souvenir Program does and what it costs. The secret is there's no sales pitch needed. People want to buy, they just need to be helped along. Ask if they'd like a full-page color ad, then stop talking and listen. If they say yes, pull out the order form and start filling it in.

## What this is

The fourth tip in the Rookie sales tip series (originally sent as e-mail #4, "Presentation"). It assumes you've already qualified the prospect, listened during the Introduction (covered in the prior tip, #3 Approaching a Prospect), and now have someone who's curious to learn more. This tip covers what to bring and what to say. The next tip (#5 Handling Objections) covers what to do if the prospect doesn't say yes.

## Prepare your sample binder

Before your visit, assemble a three-ring notebook with the following material inserted in clear sleeves.

**1. A sample of each of these pages from prior Souvenir Programs:**

- a. Full page color ad
- b. Two page color ad
- c. Half page color ad
- d. Half page Star (performer) ad
- e. Full page black and white ad
- f. Half page black and white ad
- g. Quarter page black and white ad
- h. The "Advertisers' Index" (both alphabetical and categorical) from last year's Souvenir Program, so you can show a list of the prospect's competitors who advertise with HLSR

Ideally, the ad samples and indices (items a through h) should be of the prospect's competitor(s), to show that their competitor(s) has or have the advertising advantage.

**2. Examples of Donation recognition, including scholarships.**

**3. Several Advertising Order Forms** (loose in the front so they can be completed easily).

**4. Several Donation Forms** (to be used if the advertiser would rather donate than advertise).

**5. The FAQ / Fact Card.** This was attached to the original e-mail, is available for download from the HLSR website, and could be picked up at the next meeting (the source named Tuesday, September 30).

**6. Anything else** that will help you to help the prospect become an advertiser.

## The big secret

Once you're thoroughly prepared, you're ready to "sell" an ad. The big secret is that no "sales pitch" is necessary. People actually want to buy. They just need to be helped along.

## What to say

The exact words may not fit your style, but the information is what matters. The suggested script is:

"The Souvenir Program has been published each year by the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo since it began in 1932. It is sold at the gates by 4H and FFA members whose organizations realize thousands of dollars of benefits. With over 25,000 Souvenir Programs sold each year, advertisers like you contribute significantly to the education of nearly 2,000 students today, and to the education of an additional 500 students every year. Not only do the 4H, FFA and nearly 2,000 college-age Texans benefit, but so do you.

This is one of the rare occasions where you can do well by doing good. You can help the kids and potentially help yourself and your business. The Souvenir Program reaches over 25,000 people. With a full page advertisement for only $1,000, you can reach our audience for less than 4 cents per person. That is less than half the cost of alternative commercial advertisement such as the Chronicle. So helping the kids might just help you as well.

Do you think you would be interested in a full-page color ad?"

At that point, show the prospect a full-page or 2-page color ad of their competitor.

## The close

Here's another secret to sales success. Once you've asked a question, stop talking and listen. Do not speak until after the prospect does.

If the prospect says yes, pull out your Advertising Order Form and start filling it out. Don't stop unless or until they stop you with a question or an objection.

If the prospect says no, "I don't think so," or anything other than yes, that's where handling objections comes in. The next tip covers it, and the source says it's really quite easy.

## FAQ

**What do I bring to a presentation?**
A three-ring binder with clear sleeves holding sample ad pages (full page color, two page color, half page color, half page Star, full page black and white, half page black and white, quarter page black and white), the Advertisers' Index from last year's program, donation and scholarship recognition examples, several Advertising Order Forms loose in the front, several Donation Forms, the FAQ / Fact Card, and anything else that helps. Ideally the samples and index are of the prospect's own competitors.

**Why use the prospect's competitors in my samples?**
It shows the prospect that their competitors already have the advertising advantage in the program, which makes the case for the prospect to advertise too.

**What's the key talking point on cost?**
A full page ad is only $1,000 and reaches over 25,000 people, which works out to less than 4 cents per person. The source says that's less than half the cost of alternative commercial advertising like the Chronicle.

**What should I say after I ask if they want an ad?**
Nothing. Stop talking and listen. Do not speak until after the prospect does. If they say yes, pull out the Advertising Order Form and start filling it out.

**What if the prospect doesn't say yes?**
That's handling objections, which is the next tip (Sales Tip #5). The source says it's really quite easy.

**Do I have to use the exact script?**
No. The words may not fit your style. What matters is the information you convey, not the exact wording.
