### Objective and coordination

The emergency procedure's objective is to provide information, procedures, and a plan of action to protect life and property in the event of an emergency during HLSR Showtime operations. The types of emergencies covered are severe weather, fire, bomb threat, and other accidents or disasters.

In the event of an emergency, the first individuals at the scene should immediately contact the Show's Medical and Safety Committee (832.667.1300) or the Show's Security Office (832.667.3636). The Show will then follow its Crisis Communications protocol for notifying the proper individuals.

For media relations, information to the news media or the public about emergency activities is released by Chris Boleman. Direct all media inquiries to Lisa Gagnon (832.667.1072). For inquiries about the welfare of attendees, employees, and volunteers, refer the questions to Mel Underhill (832.667.1078).

### Evacuation information

For the general public, in a disaster or similar emergency the complex is divided into four sections: Reliant Center, Reliant Stadium, Reliant Arena, and the Reliant Park grounds. The priority in evacuating any of these areas is the safety of all people involved.

For animals, it will be necessary to contain them in the exhibit space until the emergency can be further evaluated. Once the scope is determined, animal evacuation is directed by Security and Safety personnel.

Each building handles announcements its own way:

- **Reliant Center** is equipped with strobe lights, warning signals, and an automated or manual audio system. The strobe lights and warning signals precede the audio system, which gives evacuation or other verbal instructions. Announcements come over the PA from Show staff or volunteers per the Crisis Communications Plan. The Medical and Safety Committee, Houston Fire Department fire inspectors, and security assist Show management and Legends Global management as needed.
- **Reliant Stadium** has a sound system designed to cover persons in all areas. Announcements come over the PA from Rodeo staff or volunteers per the Crisis Communications Plan. The system provides evacuation or other verbal instructions as needed.
- **Reliant Arena** uses the house paging system or a supplemental sound system. Announcements come over the PA from Show staff or volunteers per the Crisis Communications Plan. The Medical and Safety Committee, HFD fire inspectors, and security assist Show management and Legends Global management as needed.

## Safety plans

### Severe weather

The Severe Weather Plan identifies actions to take in the different areas of Reliant Park when a severe weather event may occur during Rodeo hours. Flexibility is required because each event differs, and Security and Safety personnel may use discretion to ensure the safety of people.

Reliant Park staff monitors local weather reporting stations through two command posts, located in Reliant Stadium and Reliant Center. These command posts operate continuously during the entire Show, and security and emergency personnel are kept up to date on current conditions. In a severe weather event, the command post determines which safety measures, if any, are needed. Measures may include (but are not limited to) closing temporary venues such as tented structures, the carnival, and other outdoor venues, or a complete grounds evacuation.

After a severe weather event, Rodeo management, staff, and contractors inspect the grounds and facilities for damage. Necessary repairs are made before the general public is allowed back in and the Rodeo resumes operations. Once it is safe, an all-clear announcement is made for patrons, volunteers, staff, and exhibitors to return to their scheduled events. In a severe weather emergency, information and instructions are communicated to everyone.

### Lightning and thunderstorms

During lightning episodes, and when thunderstorms are overhead or near Reliant Park, stay inside the buildings while the storm is in the area. When you become aware of lightning or thunderstorms:

- Go to safe shelter immediately unless otherwise advised by emergency and security personnel.
- When outdoors, seek shelter in Reliant Stadium, Reliant Center, or Reliant Arena, whichever is most accessible.
- When indoors, remain in the building until cleared by Security and Safety personnel.

### Windstorms

When a windstorm is indicated, Reliant Park personnel assess the situation and confirm the appropriate course of action. Instructions are issued by Security and Safety personnel.

If sustained winds reach 25 miles per hour, scheduled events may be canceled and a voluntary evacuation announcement may be issued.

If sustained winds reach 35 miles per hour:

- All outdoor exhibits may be closed and outdoor scheduled events may be canceled.
- A mandatory evacuation may be issued for all people within the area.
- Attendees are advised to seek shelter immediately unless otherwise advised by emergency personnel.
- When outdoors, seek shelter in Reliant Stadium, Reliant Center, or Reliant Arena, whichever is most accessible.
- When indoors, remain in the building until the all-clear is given.

### Tornadoes

To stay safe during a tornado, put as many walls between yourself and the tornado as possible. That means interior bathrooms, hallways, and closets on the lowest floor are the best places to be. When tornadoes threaten, seek safe shelter immediately unless otherwise advised by Security and Safety personnel. When outdoors, seek shelter in Reliant Stadium, Reliant Center, or Reliant Arena, whichever is most accessible. When indoors, remain in the building until the all-clear is issued by Security and Safety personnel.

In Reliant Center, proceed to:

- Bathrooms on the first floor of the building, in the lobby or exhibit areas
- Office and storage rooms, as directed by Security and Safety personnel
- A first-floor stairwell foyer
- The exhibit hall, standing near a permanent structural wall if possible

In Reliant Arena, proceed to:

- Bathrooms on the first floor of the building
- Office and storage rooms, as directed by Security and Safety personnel
- A first-floor stairwell foyer
- The arena area, standing near a permanent structural wall or the arena wall if possible

After arriving at the safe shelter point, use your arms to protect your head and neck in a "drop and tuck" position, and stay away from windows.

If caught outside:

- Lie flat in a ditch or depression.
- Use arms to protect head and neck in a "drop and tuck" position.
- Use a jacket, cap, backpack, or similar item, if available, to protect your face and eyes.
- Avoid sheltering in small buildings, under isolated trees, under tents, or near fences and metal poles.

### Fire

Report a fire emergency to the Show's Medical and Safety Committee (832.667.1300). Be prepared to provide your name and contact number, the fire location, the type or cause of the fire if known, and whether emergency medical help is necessary. All fires, no matter the size, must be reported to the Medical and Safety Committee.

When a fire is small and contained, attempt to put it out with a fire extinguisher if it can be done without jeopardizing personal safety. Never allow the fire to come between you and the exit.

If the fire cannot be contained:

- Evacuate the area.
- Close doors and windows, if possible, to contain the fire.
- Avoid breaking windows where the fire is located, because oxygen feeds and worsens the fire.
- Do not attempt to save possessions or equipment at the risk of personal injury.

### Bomb threat

Telephone operators should familiarize themselves with these procedures and the checklist.

- The operator receiving the call should activate the voice recording option at the call station, attempt to keep the caller on the line as long as possible, and complete the procedures in the manual.
- Keep the procedures readily available so they can be obtained immediately. Complete as much of them as possible, paying particular attention to tone of voice, accent, and background noise.
- As soon as the caller hangs up, contact Show Security (832.667.3636), then Kyle Olsen (832.667.1183), Legends Global's manager on duty (832.667.1460), and Customer Service at Reliant Stadium if appropriate. Contact should be made immediately and in private.
- Do not discuss the call at the switchboard or with anyone else except those listed above. Following all procedures as outlined is extremely important.

Search Action is initiated by Show employees designated by Chris Boleman, Rodeo Security, and Legends Global Security immediately upon receiving a threat, but in as discreet a manner as possible to avoid alerting or unduly alarming the public. Any suspicious object that may be an explosive device must not be disturbed in any manner by anyone other than qualified bomb disposal personnel of the Houston Police Department or the FBI.

## Show policies and procedures

### Equipment procurement policy

Because of insurance requirements and costs and the potential liability of borrowing or renting equipment, all equipment requests for the Show must follow these procedures.

- All requests for equipment (forklifts, tractors, carts or UTVs, aerial equipment, and so on) are submitted electronically through the online Chairman Tools section of the membership website. Requests are initially made through the chairman of each committee as far in advance of the Show as possible, by the appointed deadlines. Through the online request process, each request follows an approval track that includes the appropriate officer-in-charge, staff coordinator, and management coordinator before going to the Operations Division.
- All equipment, whether on loan or rented from outside vendors, must be delivered to the Show's Buildings and Grounds Department at the HLSR warehouse for check-in and inspection by the Equipment Acquisition Committee or Asset Distribution Committee before it is distributed to the requestor.
- Following the policy should ensure HLSR gives adequate recognition to the firms and individuals making contributions. It should also result in financial savings to the Show and increase contacts with corporations that may want to support the Show this way.
- All users must hold the appropriate license or attend the proper safety training class. Safety training dates are announced, and registration is made available through online member accounts before each Show year.

**Equipment loaning:** the Show cannot loan any equipment (radios, tables, chairs, and so on) to a committee or individual hosting a non-Show-related committee function off-site.

### Conflict of interest policy

Executive Committee members, officers, voting directors, responsible employees, and volunteers (called designated individuals) stand in a joint relationship with HLSR and are expected to use their authority in good faith to serve the best interests of the Show. Any time their personal or other business interests might influence their judgment in matters involving the Show, a potential conflict of interest exists.

No officer or voting member of the board may enter any contract with the Show in which their interest and the organization's may conflict, unless the contract is approved by an affirmative vote of the Executive Committee.

An independent review should be made of any transaction or situation, whether proposed or already completed, involving the Show and another enterprise in which an Executive Committee member, officer, voting director, responsible employee, volunteer, or a member of their immediate family may have a direct or indirect interest (or a reasonable expectation of obtaining one), or where a course of action being considered may affect, directly or indirectly, the personal or other business interests of those individuals or their immediate family. Immediate family includes spouses, siblings, children and grandchildren, parents and grandparents, and in-laws.

When a potential duality of interest is discovered, a written description of the transaction or situation, with full disclosure of all relevant facts, must promptly go to the President for final approval by affirmative vote of the Executive Committee. Examples of situations that could result in a conflict of interest:

- Business transactions (sale of goods, services, or property), or a loan of personal property, between the Show and a designated individual, or an enterprise in which the individual or an immediate family member is a trustee, director, officer, key employee, or greater-than-35% owner.
- Transactions between a designated individual and a third party when that third party also deals with the Show or seeks to (routine business relationships conducted at arm's length are not included).
- Business activities of designated individuals that may directly or indirectly depend on the Show taking certain action.
- Disposition of a business opportunity the Show may be entitled to, but that a designated individual may wish to take advantage of personally.

Situations similar to these examples do not necessarily constitute conflicts, but they create the potential for one. The policy is based on the idea that full disclosure is the only effective means of assuring that a possible conflict does not become a reality.

In the routine conduct of business, some normal continuing relationships exist that could cause practical problems if literal compliance with the review procedure were required. For example, an attorney may serve as a director of the Show while his firm serves as counsel, or an insurance agent may serve as a director while he or his agency places insurance business with the Show. The policy does not intend for every routine transaction from such established relationships to be approved by the Executive Committee, but the existence of such relationships must be disclosed and understood by both parties. Any transaction that is not routine, or that involves terms the Show does not normally offer or receive with unaffiliated parties, must be submitted for review.

**Committee transactions:** individual members of a committee may not sell Show-related or committee-related goods or services valued at $5,000 or more unless that person or company is the best of three arms-length bids.

**Investment and contract transactions:** any designated individual involved in recommending the purchase or sale of an investment, or approving a contract, must disclose whether they or a family member owns, directly or indirectly, any interest in the enterprise that is the subject of the recommendation or contract, and whether they have owned any such interest within the past 12 months.

**Gifts:** employees and other designated individuals, including committee volunteers, who establish outside business relationships on behalf of the Show with suppliers, vendors, or contractors are prohibited from accepting gifts or favors that exceed a nominal value. Any gift or favor determined to be excessive or more than nominal must be approved by the president or his designee before acceptance. Failure to comply may result in termination or other action, and the item becomes the property of HLSR. At the end of each fiscal year (Aug. 31), designated individuals who received gifts should report all gifts received and the names of the people who gave them, in writing, to the President as a supplement to the annual conflict of interest questionnaire. Gifts received from committee dues or directly from the Show are governed by the Show's committee gift and award guidelines. Accepting tangible or intangible favors for approving contracts, granting special considerations, or delivering merchandise or supplies to the Show may result in removal from a volunteer position. Any attempt to bribe a designated individual should be reported in writing to the president promptly.

**Miscellaneous:** after this policy is adopted, and then annually (Aug. 31), or when an individual is first employed or becomes an officer, board member, or Executive Committee member, the president gives each a copy of the policy and a questionnaire to complete, return, and file for periodic review. A completed conflict of interest questionnaire is required annually for voting members of the board to be in good standing with the Show. Any questions about compliance should go to the president or his designee. The policy is strictly enforced and consistently applied as of the date of approval.

## FAQ

**What is the Show's main phone number and address?**
The main line is 832.667.1000. The address (both physical and mailing) is Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, Reliant Center, 3 NRG Park, Houston, Texas 77054.

**What are the office hours and what holidays are the offices closed?**
The Main Office, Membership Office, and Ticket Office are open Monday to Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. (subject to change). In 2026 the offices are closed for Juneteenth (Jun. 19), Independence Day (July 3), Labor Day (Sept. 7), Thanksgiving (Nov. 26 to 27), Christmas (Dec. 23 to 25), and New Year's Eve (Dec. 31).

**Who do I call in an emergency during the Show?**
Contact the Medical and Safety Committee / First Aid at 832.667.1300 or Show Security at 832.667.3636 right away. The first people on the scene should call one of those numbers, and the Show then follows its Crisis Communications protocol.

**What should I do if there is a tornado or severe weather?**
Seek safe shelter immediately unless told otherwise. Get inside Reliant Stadium, Reliant Center, or Reliant Arena, whichever is closest. For a tornado, get to an interior bathroom, hallway, or closet on the lowest floor, drop and tuck to protect your head and neck, and stay away from windows. Remain inside until Security and Safety personnel give the all-clear.

**How do I request equipment like a forklift or cart for the Show?**
Submit the request electronically through the online Chairman Tools section of the membership website, routed through your committee chairman as far in advance as possible. It follows an approval track (officer-in-charge, staff coordinator, management coordinator) to the Operations Division. You must hold the right license or attend the safety training class, and all equipment is delivered to the Buildings and Grounds Department at the HLSR warehouse for check-in first.

**Can I sell goods or services to my own committee?**
A committee member may not sell Show-related or committee-related goods or services valued at $5,000 or more unless that person or their company is the best of three arms-length bids. Conflicts of interest must be disclosed in writing to the President.

**Can I accept a gift from a Show vendor?**
No, not beyond a nominal value. Designated individuals, including committee volunteers, who set up business relationships on the Show's behalf cannot accept gifts or favors that exceed nominal value. Anything more must be approved by the president or his designee first, and gifts received must be reported in writing at fiscal year-end (Aug. 31).
