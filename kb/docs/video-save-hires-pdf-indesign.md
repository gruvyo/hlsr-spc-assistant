# How to Save a Hi-Res Print-Ready PDF in InDesign

**Quick answer:** Open your ad in Adobe InDesign, go to File, Adobe PDF Presets, and pick High Quality Print. Name the file, click Save, then on the Marks and Bleeds panel make sure no boxes are checked and click Export. Your new high-res PDF lands in the same folder as your InDesign file.

## What this is

A short how-to for exporting your ad from Adobe InDesign as a high-resolution, print-ready PDF (also called a High Quality Print file). This is the file the Souvenir Program needs. If you build your ad in Illustrator instead, see the Illustrator version. The steps below match what's shown in the video, so you can follow along without watching it.

## Before you start

Open your finished ad in Adobe InDesign. Have your contract number and the company name handy, since you'll use them to name the file.

## Step by step

1. **Open your InDesign file.** Start with your finished ad open on screen.

2. **Go to File, then Adobe PDF Presets.** Click the **File** menu at the top, scroll down to **Adobe PDF Presets**, and hover over it to open the submenu.

3. **Select [High Quality Print].** In the submenu, choose **[High Quality Print]**. This is the preset that makes a hi-res, print-ready file.

4. **Name the file and click Save.** A save window opens. In the **File name** box, type your file name. Use your contract number, then the company name, like `Contract#_Company_Name`. Leave **Save as type** set to **Adobe PDF (Print) (*.pdf)**. Click **Save**.

5. **The Export Adobe PDF window opens.** A new window appears. At the top you'll see **Adobe PDF Preset: [High Quality Print]** and **Compatibility: Acrobat 5 (PDF 1.4)**. Leave those as they are.

6. **Go to Marks and Bleeds.** In the list on the left side of the window (General, Compression, Marks and Bleeds, Output, and so on), click **Marks and Bleeds**.

7. **Make sure no boxes are checked.** On the Marks and Bleeds panel, leave every box unchecked. Do not check All Printer's Marks, Crop Marks, Bleed Marks, Registration Marks, Color Bars, or Page Information. Leave Use Document Bleed Settings unchecked and the Bleed values at 0. Leave Include Slug Area unchecked.

8. **Click Export.** Click the **Export** button at the bottom of the window.

9. **Close your file.** That's it. If InDesign asks whether you want to save changes before closing, answer as you like. Your new high-res PDF is now saved in the same folder as your original InDesign file, ready to submit.

## FAQ

**Which preset do I pick?**
High Quality Print. In InDesign it shows in brackets as [High Quality Print]. It's the same thing as a hi-res, print-ready PDF.

**Where do I find the preset?**
File menu, then Adobe PDF Presets, then [High Quality Print]. You don't use File, Export for this. Picking the preset starts the export for you.

**Should I turn on crop marks or bleed?**
No. On the Marks and Bleeds panel, leave every box unchecked. The Souvenir Program does not want printer's marks on the file, and the artwork specs say to delete all crop marks before exporting.

**What should I name the file?**
Use your contract number and the company name, like `Contract#_Company_Name`. Keep Save as type set to Adobe PDF (Print) (*.pdf).

**Where does the finished PDF end up?**
In the same folder as your original InDesign file. Look there for the new PDF after you click Export.

**My ad is in Illustrator, not InDesign. What do I do?**
Use the Illustrator how-to instead. See How to Save a Hi-Res PDF in Illustrator.
