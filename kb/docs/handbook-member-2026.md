# 2026 HLSR Member Handbook

**Quick answer:** This is the official handbook for HLSR members. It explains membership types and costs ($50 annual, $25 junior, $500 life), how to pay dues, what membership gets you, Corral Club rules, the legal and harassment policies, marketing and social media rules, and the emergency and safety plans. Members who also serve on a volunteer committee are subject to the Volunteer Handbook too.

## What this is

The Member Handbook lays out your role as an HLSR member. It sets the guidelines, rules, and procedures expected of members. It is organized into six sections: Show Information, Membership Information, Corral Club, Legal, Marketing, and Safety Plans. Any member who serves on a volunteer roster is also subject to the rules in the HLSR Volunteer Handbook.

HLSR is a membership organization with about 47,000 members worldwide. Its mission is to promote agriculture by hosting an annual, family-friendly experience that educates and entertains the public, supports Texas youth, showcases Western heritage, and provides year-round educational support within the community.

## Membership eligibility and types

Membership is open to any individual who is interested in supporting the HLSR mission.

Types of membership:

- **Annual (May 1 to April 30).** Available to any individual 21 years of age and older. Cost: $50. Expires yearly on April 30. Renew through the member's online account.
- **Junior Annual (May 1 to April 30).** Available to any individual under age 21. Cost: $25. Expires yearly on April 30. Renew through the member's online account.
- **Life.** A one-time $500 payment. No subsequent Show membership purchases are required for the rest of the member's life.

## Membership benefits

Annual membership benefits:

- Official Rodeo car decal
- Subscription to the quarterly "Bowlegged H" Magazine
- Opportunity to volunteer on a committee (optional)

Life benefits (all Annual Member benefits plus):

- Life Member pin
- Opportunity to purchase a Life Member plaque for $75
- Life Membership Card (grants admission to the Livestock Shows and Horse Shows, Commercial Exhibits, Carnival, and the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest). It does not include NRG Stadium or Rodeo Uncorked! events.

Not included in Annual or Life benefits: parking, Gold or Silver badges, and a guaranteed spot on a volunteer committee.

## Membership dues and payment

Members are encouraged to pay dues online using the Rodeo's online tools. Other options are at the Show membership windows or by mail sent directly to the Rodeo office. From September to February, membership kiosks are available at the top of the escalator in NRG Center, with computers for online payments.

Insufficient funds rules:

- A member who submits a check with insufficient funds for committee dues or annual memberships is deemed ineligible to serve on a committee if the returned amount is not paid in full within 30 days of notice from Accounting. If a gold badge or parking permits for the upcoming Rodeo have already been provided, those items will be confiscated.
- A member who submits a check with insufficient funds for other obligations is deemed ineligible to serve on a committee if the returned amount is not paid in full within 90 days of notice from Accounting.

## How to apply

Apply online at rodeohouston.com, or complete a Membership Application form from the Membership Department or a Membership Committee member. Failure to provide all required information may prevent the application from being processed.

A completed membership application, indemnity agreement, member handbook acknowledgement, and membership payment must be received by the HLSR Membership Department.

## Membership cards

A membership card, along with any other benefit items, is sent to new members by the Membership Department. Member cards are valid until the expiration date printed on the physical card. At expiration, the Membership Office sends a new card to the member's address on file. Cards are non-transferable and are for the exclusive use of the individual to whom they are issued.

## Suspension and expulsion

Any member, life or annual, may be suspended or expelled by the chairman of the board and the president when it is determined that the member is a detriment to the welfare of HLSR. Suspension or expulsion takes effect after the member has been notified of the action in writing by the Show.

## Miscellaneous membership notes

Memberships are non-transferable and non-refundable and are issued on an individual basis only. There are no provisions for company, corporate, or family memberships.

If an annual member has already paid their current annual membership and wants to convert to a life membership within the same May 1 to April 30 membership year, they can do so by purchasing a $450 life member conversion from their online account or the Membership Office. Payment is due by the end of that same Show year.

## Corral Club

**Responsible Alcohol Management.** It is the policy of HLSR and the Corral Club to always serve alcohol responsibly. Alcohol is not served to anyone under 21 or to any obviously intoxicated person. The Show and Corral Club follow all applicable laws and Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission regulations.

**Guest policy.** Guests must be accompanied by their Corral Club host and must leave when their host leaves.

**Management.** The Corral Club chairman, the respective club chairmen, and the Corral Club Committee members are responsible for the proper management of the Corral Clubs.

**Hours and locations.** All Corral Clubs are open throughout the run of the Show. A complete list of hours and locations is at rodeohouston.com. The Show reserves the right to limit access to or close Corral Clubs as needed to meet health and safety guidelines.

**Admittance.** No smoking is allowed in any clubs (Chute Club and The Cantina have outdoor smoking balconies). The NRG Center Committeeman's Club and NRG Arena's Stockman's Club admit minors daily until 8 p.m. if accompanied by a parent, legal guardian, or an adult spouse who has proper credentials for the area (for example a gold badge or Show credentials). The Stockman's Club in NRG Arena also admits Horse Show Exhibitors if capacity allows and sells drinks to them at $6 per drink. Any minor not in compliance is referred to the Security Office.

## Legal guidelines

**Equal opportunity.** The Show is committed to a work and service environment where all individuals are treated with respect and dignity, free of discrimination and harassment. In accordance with applicable law, the Show prohibits harassment or discrimination based on gender, race, color, creed, religion, national origin, ancestry, age, mental or physical disability, medical condition, sexual orientation, veteran or marital status, or other reasons prohibited by law. Harassment can apply to conduct at any time and place, not just during committee service.

**Sexual harassment.** Defined as unwanted sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature when submission is a condition of service, submission or rejection is a basis for service decisions, or the conduct unreasonably interferes with performance or creates a hostile environment. A partial list of offensive behavior includes unwanted advances or propositions, offering service benefits in exchange for sexual favors, making or threatening reprisals after a negative response, derogatory or sexually explicit comments, verbal abuse of a sexual nature, and physical conduct such as touching, assault, or blocking movement.

**Other types of harassment.** Harassment on the basis of race, color, national origin, ancestry, religion, disability, age, or any other protected basis is also prohibited. This includes verbal conduct (threats, epithets, slurs), visual conduct (derogatory posters, photos, cartoons, gestures), physical conduct (assault, unwanted touching, blocking movement), and retaliation for reporting harassment.

**Complaint procedure.** Any member who feels subjected to discrimination or harassment, or who has witnessed it, should file a written complaint with the general counsel or the Show's Human Resources Department as soon as possible. The Show will conduct a discreet, thorough, and objective investigation, and if harassment or discrimination is found, prompt corrective action is taken, up to removal from committee membership.

**Liability.** Any Show member found to have engaged in prohibited harassment or discrimination is subject to discipline, up to and including removal from membership.

**Acts or threats of violence.** Acts or threats of physical violence, including intimidation, harassment, and coercion, are not tolerated on Show property. A person who engages in a threat or violent act is removed from the premises as quickly as safety permits. Members, employees, and volunteers are prohibited from carrying weapons on the Show's premises (per NRG Park Policy) and in Show vehicles, regardless of location. "Weapon" includes firearms, whether licensed or not, and any potentially dangerous object not customarily used in the performance of duties.

**Drug use.** The manufacture, distribution, dispensing, possession, sale, purchase, or use of a controlled substance on Rodeo property is prohibited. Being under the influence of illegal drugs at NRG Park or on Rodeo property is prohibited. Unauthorized use of prescription drugs and illegal use of over-the-counter drugs is also prohibited. This includes marijuana or cannabis in all forms. Any member who violates this policy may be suspended or expelled. The Rodeo reports possession, distribution, or use of illegal drugs to law enforcement and cooperates fully in prosecution. Searches are conducted based on reasonable suspicion.

## Marketing and media

**Media relations.** The Rodeo's Communications Department maintains year-round media relationships. Send all media requests to Marisa Saenz at 713-819-4773 or msaenz@rodeohouston.com, or to the general media email media@rodeohouston.com. Only designated Show officials are approved to speak for the Show.

**Social media policy.** Members must use care on social media. They should follow the same standards of behavior online as in person. Anything written online can be traced to its author and may go viral. Key rules:

- You are responsible for anything you write or post online.
- Postings must not violate any handbook policy, including the policies against harassment and other misconduct.
- Do not post or link to content that is vulgar, obscene, threatening, pornographic, harassing, or defamatory, or that discriminates against a protected class.
- Testimonials and endorsements about the Rodeo should be avoided unless posted to the Rodeo's official platforms by the Marketing, Public Relations, and Presentations Division. The FTC regulates this (16 CFR Part 255.5). If a representative does post one, they must clearly disclose their volunteer or membership relationship. Suggested disclaimer: "The postings on this site are my own and do not represent the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo's positions, strategies or opinions."
- Do not post confidential or proprietary Rodeo information, trade secrets, or business plans. Do not post confidential personal information about volunteers, employees, sponsors, or vendors (social security or tax ID numbers, addresses, birth dates, health information, credit card numbers, driver's license numbers).
- Abide by copyright laws. Do not use Show, sponsor, or vendor trademarks or logos without written permission, except images shared from the Rodeo's official platforms (for example via @RODEOHOUSTON).
- Representatives are not required to connect with or "friend" other volunteers or employees on social media, and may not coerce others to do so.

Questions about the policy go to the Marketing, Public Relations, and Presentations Division.

## Safety plans

The objective is to protect life and property in an emergency during Showtime operations. Types of emergencies covered: severe weather, fire, bomb threat, and other accidents or disasters.

**Coordination of emergency actions.** The first individual(s) at the scene should immediately contact the Medical and Safety Committee (832.667.1300) or the Security Office (832.667.3636). The Rodeo then follows its Crisis Communications protocol.

**Evacuation.** The complex is divided into four sections: NRG Center, NRG Stadium, NRG Arena, and NRG Park grounds. The priority is the safety of all people. Animals are contained in the exhibit space until the emergency is evaluated, then evacuated at the direction of Security and Safety personnel. Each building (NRG Center, NRG Stadium, NRG Arena) has its own PA and warning systems, and announcements are made per the Crisis Communications Plan.

**Severe weather.** NRG Park staff monitor local weather through two command posts in NRG Stadium and NRG Center, which operate continuously during the Rodeo. The command post decides what safety measures are needed, which can include closing tented structures, the carnival, other outdoor venues, or a full grounds evacuation.

- Lightning and thunderstorms: stay inside buildings while the storm is in the area. Go to safe shelter immediately unless advised otherwise. When outdoors, seek shelter in NRG Stadium, NRG Center, or NRG Arena, whichever is closest. When indoors, stay until cleared by Security and Safety personnel.
- Windstorms: if sustained winds reach 25 mph, scheduled events may be canceled and a voluntary evacuation may be announced. If sustained winds reach 35 mph, all outdoor exhibits may close, outdoor events may be canceled, and a mandatory evacuation may be issued.
- Tornadoes: put as many walls between yourself and the tornado as possible. Interior bathrooms, hallways, and closets on the lowest floor are best. In NRG Center, go to first-floor bathrooms (lobby or exhibit areas), office and storage rooms as directed, a first-floor stairwell foyer, or the exhibit hall near a permanent structural wall. In NRG Arena, go to first-floor bathrooms, office and storage rooms as directed, a first-floor stairwell foyer, or the arena area near a permanent structural wall. Once sheltered, use arms to protect head and neck in a "drop and tuck" position and stay away from windows. If caught outside, lie flat in a ditch or depression, protect head and neck, use a jacket or backpack to protect the face, and avoid small buildings, isolated trees, tents, fences, and metal poles.

**Fire.** Report a fire emergency to the Medical and Safety Committee (832.667.1300). Be prepared to give your name and contact number, the fire location, the type or cause of the fire if known, and whether emergency medical help is needed. All fires, no matter the size, must be reported to the Medical and Safety Committee.

## FAQ

**How much does membership cost?**
Annual membership is $50 for anyone 21 or older. Junior Annual is $25 for anyone under 21. Life membership is a one-time $500 payment. An existing annual member can convert to Life within the same membership year for $450.

**When does my membership expire and how do I renew?**
The membership year runs May 1 to April 30, and annual and junior memberships expire on April 30. Renew through your online account, at the Show membership windows, by mail to the Rodeo office, or at a membership kiosk in NRG Center (available September to February at the top of the escalator).

**Does membership get me parking or a badge or a committee spot?**
No. Parking, Gold or Silver badges, and a guaranteed spot on a volunteer committee are not included in Annual or Life benefits. Volunteering on a committee is an optional benefit you have the opportunity to pursue.

**What do I do in an emergency during the Show?**
Contact the Medical and Safety Committee at 832.667.1300 or the Security Office at 832.667.3636. Show Security is also 832.667.3636. For a fire, call the Medical and Safety Committee at 832.667.1300 and report it no matter the size.

**Can I speak to the media or post about the Rodeo on social media?**
Only designated Show officials may speak for the Show. Send media requests to Marisa Saenz (713-819-4773, msaenz@rodeohouston.com) or media@rodeohouston.com. On social media, avoid testimonials or endorsements about the Rodeo unless they are posted by the official Marketing division, and if you do post one you must disclose that you are a volunteer or member.

**What happens if my dues check bounces?**
For committee dues or annual memberships, you become ineligible to serve on a committee if you do not pay the returned amount in full within 30 days of notice from Accounting, and any gold badge or parking permits already provided are confiscated. For other obligations, the window is 90 days.
