# Sales Tip #3: Approaching a Prospect

**Quick answer:** Once you have your stack of 20 to 30 qualified prospects, decide whether to reach each one by phone or in person (phone if you know them through business, in person if you only know them face to face), then open with a friendly conversation. Lead with the question "Have you ever considered being involved with the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo?" and use the three sample responses below to steer the talk toward advertising in the Souvenir Program. That sets up your presentation, which is covered in Tip #4.

## What this is

The third in the Rookie sales tip series. Tip #2 covered converting a suspect into a prospect by checking that they aren't already an advertiser and by building a prospect card. This tip assumes that's done and you now have a stack of 20 to 30 qualified names. It covers how to actually contact them and what to say, and it ends by handing off to the presentation in the next tip.

A reminder carries over from Tip #2. Before you approach anyone, review the protected advertisers list so you don't approach a name that's already taken.

A few minutes of preparation before each contact pays off.

## Step 1: Decide the best method to contact the prospect

Pick the contact method that fits your relationship with the prospect.

- If you know the prospect through business and are comfortable talking with them on the phone, a phone call is appropriate.
- If you're a customer who only knows the prospect from face to face encounters, plan on visiting with them in person.

## Step 2: Prepare for your conversation

A good sales presentation begins by engaging the prospect in a conversation through questions. Only ask questions whose answers support your cause. Here's a sample sales conversation starter you might use.

**Introduction:** "Mr. Prospect, do you have a few minutes that we might talk? I am very excited about my association with HLSR."

**Question:** "Have you ever considered being involved with the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo?" This is a good opening question because it gives you a starting point for your discussion.

## The three potential answers and how to respond

The opening question leads to one of three kinds of answers. Each has a suggested response. In all three cases, whatever the prospect says back, acknowledge it and then proceed to your presentation (covered in Tip #4).

### Potential answer 1: they're already involved

The prospect says something like "Yes, I am on the ____ committee and have been for years." They may talk about their experience at length and with enthusiasm. If so, let them talk and share their enthusiasm before you continue.

**Response:** "Great, as you know it is a wonderful charity with a great cause and has an enormous impact on the Houston economy. Have you ever considered advertising in the Souvenir Program?" Their answer here might be yes, no, or a request for more information such as "what do you mean?" or "what is it?" Acknowledge whatever they say, then move to your presentation.

### Potential answer 2: they support the show through a purchase

The prospect says something like "Yes, I buy a (pig, chicken, barbecue tent, etc.) each year from my (brother, friend, business associate, etc.)." Again, let them tell their story while you listen intently.

**Response:** "Great, did you know there are additional ways to support HLSR that may actually serve not only your philanthropic desires, but might also help your business?" Acknowledge their response, then move to your presentation.

### Potential answer 3: they haven't considered it

The prospect says something like "No, I haven't considered getting involved because ...." The reasons range from "I am too busy" to "I didn't know anything about it."

**Response:** "There are many ways to be involved ranging from the very active involvement of joining a committee to the more passive and potentially lucrative method of simply advertising in the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo Souvenir Program, which is the committee I represent. May I tell you more about my committee and the Souvenir Program?" Acknowledge their response, then move to your presentation.

## After the approach

That's it. You've engaged the prospect and prepared them for your presentation. The next tip (Tip #4) covers the presentation you might use, in a form that fits your style.

One last thing. Let the committee know what you're using that's working. These are time tested sales strategies, but they aren't the only ones. The meetings are everyone's chance to share successes so we can help every member badge.

## FAQ

**Should I call my prospect or visit in person?**
It depends on how you know them. If you know the prospect through business and you're comfortable on the phone, call. If you only know them from face to face encounters as a customer, plan to visit in person.

**What should I do before I approach anyone?**
Review the protected advertisers list first so you don't approach a name that's already taken. This carries over from Tip #2.

**What's a good opening question?**
"Have you ever considered being involved with the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo?" It gives you a starting point and leads into one of three responses you can prepare for.

**What if the prospect is already on a committee?**
Let them talk and share their enthusiasm, then say it's a wonderful charity with a great cause and a big impact on the Houston economy, and ask if they've ever considered advertising in the Souvenir Program.

**What if the prospect says they're not interested or too busy?**
Acknowledge it, then explain that there are many ways to be involved, from joining a committee to the more passive and potentially lucrative option of simply advertising in the Souvenir Program, and ask if you can tell them more about your committee.

**What happens after I get through the approach?**
You move into your presentation, which is covered in Tip #4. Whatever the prospect's response, acknowledge it first and then proceed.
