# Volunteer Handbook: Sponsorship program

**Quick answer:** The Show's Sponsorship Program sells marketing and advertising rights to companies for a minimum of $150K. Sponsors are not the same as donors, and the word "sponsor" can only be used for official Show sponsors. Many sponsors pay extra for category exclusivity, so volunteers have to honor those exclusive rights at committee events (for example serving Coca-Cola products, not displaying competitor trucks, and following donation-agreement rules for beer and soft drinks). Donors at the $10,000 level and above are protected and should not be solicited for more funds.

## What this is

The Sponsorship Program section of the 2026 Volunteer Handbook (pages 101 to 104). It tells volunteers what the program is, why sponsors and donors are different, which companies hold exclusive rights, and the rules committees must follow at their events to protect those rights. It closes with the protected donors and sponsors rule.

## What the Sponsorship Program is

The Show's Sponsorship Program exists to give companies a way to buy marketing, promotional, and advertising rights. Sponsors pay a minimum of $150,000 to buy the rights to associate with the Show through these methods:

- using the Show Marks
- displaying their logos
- promoting their brands
- on-site promotions (activation)
- in-store advertising
- social media and website
- promoting a RODEOHOUSTON ticket giveaway
- other creative means to make their brand stand out from their competitors

Sponsors focus on a variety of goals, such as creating awareness of their brands, creating goodwill within the community, increasing sales, establishing business-to-business relationships, expanding the company's market, and so on.

The term "sponsor" may be used only in relation to official Show sponsors. The term "sponsor" cannot be used to refer to donors.

## Ambush marketing

Committees work with staff coordinators to help protect the rights of exclusive Show sponsors. Volunteers should be aware of ambush marketing, which is when a company implies an association with the Show without paying for marketing rights.

Be mindful that what may appear as a benevolent donation to the Show may actually be a company's attempt to associate itself with the Show. An example would be when a company provides its mascot for a Show or committee event along with a food donation.

## Exclusivity

### Category

- Sponsors with category exclusivity pay additional fees to exclude their competitors from associating with the Show or participating in the Show and at year-round Show events.
- The Show is bound by legal contracts to protect the exclusivity of these sponsors and their designated products, which provide substantial revenue to the Show.

### Sponsors with exclusivity at Reliant Park and throughout the Houston Metropolitan / Area Go Texan communities and counties

- NRG / Reliant Energy: Heritage Partner, Naming Rights Partner, Electricity Sponsor
- Ford / Houston Area Ford Dealers: Heritage Partner, Truck/SUV/CUV Sponsor/Provider, Vehicle Provider
- Miller Lite (Molson Coors): Heritage Partner, Beer, Hard Seltzers and Malt Liquor Sponsor
- Coca-Cola: Heritage Partner, Non-alcoholic Beverage Sponsor (excluding coffee, tea and energy drinks)
- Mattress Firm: Heritage Partner, Mattress, Massage Chairs Sponsor
- Carnival Cruise Line: Official Partner, Cruise Line Sponsor

### Providers with product and/or sponsorship exclusivity at Reliant Park

- Cat Lift Trucks: Lift Truck Provider
- Darr Equipment Co.: Lift Truck Provider
- Mustang CAT: Heavy Construction Equipment Provider
- Shoppa's Farm Supply: Tractor Provider
- WM: Sustainability Provider

## Show and committee events in relation to exclusive Show sponsors

No competitor of an Official Show Sponsor with exclusive rights may be acknowledged as a Show patron or presenter, or underwriter of any committee event that is under the direct and sole control of the Show. Competing products of Show exclusive sponsors may not be displayed at any Show events, including committee activities at Reliant Park or off-site.

All Show-purchased products (purchased with any type of Show funds) must honor sponsor exclusivity.

### Ford

Ford competitors' Trucks/SUVs/CUVs may not be displayed or used in conjunction with any activity that is under the direct and sole control of the Show or any of its divisions.

- Donations of non-Ford trucks/SUVs/CUVs cannot be accepted by Show volunteers or staff for operational use or for giveaway, auction, or other activity at Reliant Park or off-site Show events.

### Coca-Cola

Coca-Cola products must be served at Show events held either at Reliant Park or in the Houston Metropolitan area. (This includes all soft drinks, sports drinks, juices, bottled flavored milk drinks, and bottled water.)

- Coca-Cola products must be purchased and served at off-site events unless the event is held at a facility not controlled by the Show (for example a restaurant, country club, or hotel).
- Coca-Cola may approve, on a case-by-case basis, a donation of a competitor's product outside Reliant Park, as long as there is no public acknowledgment by the Show or competitor. The product must be served in a generic container with no competitor's name or logo.
- A soft drink donation agreement (in the form section), whereby the donor agrees not to publicize or promote the donation, is required, and each instance of donation must have a valid donation agreement on file with the staff coordinator and the Sponsorships, Business and Corporate Development Department.

### Miller Lite (Molson Coors)

Miller Lite (Molson Coors) is the exclusive beer, hard seltzer, and malt beverage sponsor, and all sponsorship exclusivity, such as signage, must be honored.

- However, state law as administered by the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission forbids exclusivity of pourage at public events.
- Committee fundraising activities that are not held at NRG Park may accept product donations from Molson Coors competitors as long as there is no public acknowledgment by the Show or competitor. A beer and malt liquor donation agreement (in the form section), whereby the donor agrees not to publicize or promote the donation, is required, and each instance of donation must have a valid donation agreement on file with the staff coordinator and the Sponsorships, Business and Corporate Development Department. Such donations of product cannot result in a situation where more than 50 percent of the product served at the event is an exclusive category competitor.

### WM (Waste Management)

WM (Waste Management) competitors may not be sponsors or underwriters at event activities or fundraisers.

- Competitors may be used in the Houston Metro area (outside of NRG Park) if the event/fundraiser is held at a facility that has a service contract with the competitor, or if the competitor donates the services.
- If services are donated by a competitor, care must be taken to keep logo-marked containers out of public view.

Waste Management is given exclusivity at off-site events under the Show's control.

## Protected donors and sponsors

A current list of sponsors and donors can be found in the General Announcements section of the Online member account.

Donors are protected at the $10,000 level and above and are not to be solicited for additional funds.

This helps:

- to reduce frequent solicitations by multiple committee volunteers
- to protect committee and volunteer sales credit for donations
- to protect relationships already secured by the Show or another committee, and to ensure that recognition and benefits remain consistent across the programs

## FAQ

**What's the difference between a sponsor and a donor?**
A sponsor is a company that pays at least $150K to buy marketing, promotional, and advertising rights to associate with the Show. A donor gives money but does not buy those rights. The word "sponsor" can only be used for official Show sponsors, never for donors.

**What is ambush marketing and why should I watch for it?**
Ambush marketing is when a company implies an association with the Show without paying for marketing rights. Sometimes it looks like a generous donation but is really an attempt to associate with the Show, for example a company offering its mascot along with a food donation. Committees work with staff coordinators to protect the rights of exclusive sponsors.

**Which companies have exclusive rights I need to honor at my committee's events?**
At Reliant Park and across the Houston Metro / Area Go Texan area, the exclusive sponsors are NRG / Reliant Energy (electricity), Ford / Houston Area Ford Dealers (trucks/SUVs/CUVs), Miller Lite / Molson Coors (beer, hard seltzers, malt liquor), Coca-Cola (non-alcoholic beverages, excluding coffee, tea, and energy drinks), Mattress Firm (mattresses, massage chairs), and Carnival Cruise Line. Reliant Park providers with exclusivity include Cat Lift Trucks and Darr Equipment Co. (lift trucks), Mustang CAT (heavy construction equipment), Shoppa's Farm Supply (tractors), and WM (sustainability).

**Can my committee accept a donation of beer or soft drinks from a competing brand?**
Sometimes, with strict conditions. For soft drinks, Coca-Cola may approve a competitor's product donation outside Reliant Park on a case-by-case basis if there's no public acknowledgment and it's served in a generic container with no competitor name or logo, and a signed soft drink donation agreement must be on file. For beer, committee fundraisers not held at NRG Park may accept donations from Molson Coors competitors with no public acknowledgment, a signed beer and malt liquor donation agreement on file, and no more than 50 percent of the product served being an exclusive category competitor. Both agreements are in the form section and must be on file with the staff coordinator and the Sponsorships, Business and Corporate Development Department.

**What drinks have to be Coca-Cola products at our events?**
At Show events held at Reliant Park or in the Houston Metropolitan area, all soft drinks, sports drinks, juices, bottled flavored milk drinks, and bottled water must be Coca-Cola products. Off-site, they must be purchased and served as Coca-Cola unless the event is at a facility the Show doesn't control, such as a restaurant, country club, or hotel.

**Can we use a non-Ford truck for a giveaway or to move things at our event?**
No. Ford competitors' trucks, SUVs, and CUVs may not be displayed or used in any activity under the Show's direct and sole control. Show volunteers and staff cannot accept donations of non-Ford trucks/SUVs/CUVs for operational use or for a giveaway, auction, or other activity at Reliant Park or off-site Show events.

**Why am I told not to ask certain donors for more money?**
Donors are protected at the $10,000 level and above and are not to be solicited for additional funds. This reduces repeated solicitations by multiple volunteers, protects committee and volunteer sales credit, protects relationships already secured by the Show or another committee, and keeps recognition and benefits consistent. A current list of sponsors and donors is in the General Announcements section of the Online member account.
