# SPC traditions and recurring annual events

**Quick answer:** The SPC season runs on a calendar of recurring events. It opens with the Kick-Off Crawfish Boil and closes with Rodeo Prom, the year-end Awards Banquet. Along the way there's a Karaoke Contest, Rookie Orientation for new members, a book drive with the Trailblazer Committee, dress-up meeting themes, and several long-running raffles and awards.

## What this is

A reference on the committee's traditions and the events that come around every year. It's synthesized from The Heartbeat, the committee newsletter, across 59 issues from 2014 to 2024. Every fact below cites the issue it came from. Where a detail changed over the years, the most recent version is given and the change is noted. Nothing here is invented, and dated news from a single year is not restated as if it's current.

## The season opener: Kick-Off Crawfish Boil

The committee opens each year with an annual Kick-Off Crawfish Boil, a recurring season-opening tradition (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.1, Jun 2016). It doubles as a recruiting event geared toward attracting rookie members (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.1, Jul 2014). It's a combined kick-off and recruiting party where members can also pick up their badges, and members are invited to bring a guest or a potential new member (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.7, Apr 2021).

## The season closer: Rodeo Prom (Awards Banquet)

The committee's end-of-year Awards Banquet is its season-closing celebration, and it's nicknamed the "Rodeo Prom" (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019, and Vol. 1.6, Nov 2014). It's where the year's final ad-sales numbers and committee awards are shared (The Heartbeat Vol. 9.3, Jan 2023).

The banquet's timing has been described differently across the years. Early issues placed it in late January (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.6, Nov 2014) or simply in January (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.5, Oct 2016). A later issue puts it in February (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.7, Nov 2017). Treat the exact date as something to confirm against the current season's calendar rather than fixed.

Committee dues help cover the year's functions, including food and drinks for meetings and the Rodeo Prom celebration (The Heartbeat Vol. 7.3, Aug 2020).

## Karaoke Contest

The committee runs an annual Karaoke Contest. It began with its inaugural event in 2014, organized by Cynthia Vinson (The Heartbeat Vol. 2.6, Oct 2015).

## Rookie Orientation

New members, called rookies, attend a Rookie Orientation as part of joining the committee (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.1, Jun 2016). The recruiting process invites prospective members to Rookie Orientation meetings to get their questions answered before committing (The Heartbeat Vol. 10.2, Jul 2023).

## Rodeo ROPES book drive

The committee partners with the Trailblazer Committee's Rodeo ROPES program on an annual book drive, hosting the collection over committee meetings (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.4, Sep 2019). Rodeo ROPES (Reading Opens the Path to Education Success) is a literacy program run by the Trailblazer Committee that collects gently used children's books for low-income, elementary-aged children around Houston and stocks free lending libraries for Livestock Exhibitors during the Show (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.5, Sep 2018, and Vol. 5.8, Nov 2018).

The drive accepts gently used Pre-K through 5th-grade books: storybooks, chapter books, learn-to-read books, children's non-fiction, board and toddler books, and Spanish or dual-language books. It does not accept damaged, religious, workbook, coloring, sticker, outdated non-fiction, teacher or parenting, audiobook, or inappropriate-content titles (The Heartbeat Vol. 5.6, Oct 2018).

## Dress-up meeting themes

The committee uses a season-long calendar of general meetings, each with its own dress-up theme (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.3, Aug 2017).

## The big final-meeting turn-in

The committee's final general meeting of the season always produces the largest sales turn-in of the year (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.7, Nov 2017).

## Recurring raffles and incentive drawings

Members who keep their red raffle ticket from every meeting and hold the complete set are entered into a special prize raffle at Rodeo Prom (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.4, Sep 2014, and Vol. 1.3, Sep 2014).

There's also an early-sales incentive drawing for a donated School Art Auction champion print. Members need not be present to win, but they can only win once per year (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.4, Sep 2019).

## Rookie of the Year

The committee names a Rookie of the Year each year. Past honorees include Rebecca Bertrand (2012), Terry Richardson (2013), Beth Garney (2014), Rachel Atherton (2015), Greg Turman (2016), and Lori Hill (2017) (The Heartbeat Vol. 4.6, Oct 2017).

## FAQ

**What event kicks off the SPC season?**
The annual Kick-Off Crawfish Boil. It opens the year and doubles as a recruiting party where members can pick up badges and bring a guest or potential new member (The Heartbeat Vol. 3.1, Jun 2016, and Vol. 7.7, Apr 2021).

**What is Rodeo Prom?**
It's the committee's nickname for the year-end Awards Banquet, the season-closing celebration where the final ad-sales numbers and committee awards are shared (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.5, Nov 2019, and Vol. 9.3, Jan 2023).

**When is Rodeo Prom held?**
The newsletter has described it as late January in early years and as February in a later year, so confirm the date against the current season's calendar (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.6, Nov 2014, and Vol. 4.7, Nov 2017).

**What's the deal with the red raffle tickets?**
Keep your red raffle ticket from every meeting. Members who hold the complete set are entered into a special prize raffle at Rodeo Prom (The Heartbeat Vol. 1.4, Sep 2014).

**Does the committee do anything for charity besides scholarships?**
Yes. It partners with the Trailblazer Committee on the annual Rodeo ROPES book drive, collecting gently used Pre-K through 5th-grade children's books over committee meetings (The Heartbeat Vol. 6.4, Sep 2019, and Vol. 5.5, Sep 2018).
