# Membership, Dues, and Badging

**Quick answer:** To be an HLSR member costs $50 a year for Annual (ages 21 and up), $25 for Junior Annual (under 21), or a one-time $500 for Life. The membership year runs May 1 to April 30. To serve on the Souvenir Program Committee you become a Rodeo member ($50), then pay committee dues ($70). To earn your gold badge you pay your dues, have your legal name verified, sign the indemnity agreement and member handbook acknowledgement, and hit your sales minimum, which is $1,000 for a rookie (first-year member) and $2,000 for a veteran. A gold badge admits you and one guest to the NRG Stadium main concourse for performances plus the rest of the grounds. A silver badge admits you and one guest to everything except the stadium performances. Neither includes parking.

## What this is

This is the membership rulebook for a Souvenir Program Committee (SPC) member. It pulls together how you become and stay an HLSR member, how you pay dues, what your membership gets you, the two steps to join the committee, what it takes to earn your gold badge each year, what your gold and silver badges get you into, and how the committee's roster and hierarchy are managed. It draws from the 2026 Volunteer Handbook, the 2026 Member Handbook, and the 2024 Rookie Orientation deck. Where a value comes from a specific year's document, that is noted, but the membership types, dues, and badging rules are consistent across the sources.

Note on venue names: the Show's home is NRG Park (NRG Center, NRG Stadium, NRG Arena). Older documents call it Reliant Park, Reliant Center, Reliant Stadium, and Reliant Arena. Reliant is the same place as NRG. This doc uses the current NRG names throughout.

## Who can be a member

Membership is open to any individual interested in supporting the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo mission.

There are ways to lose eligibility on a bad payment:

- A member who submits a check with insufficient funds for committee dues or annual memberships is deemed ineligible to serve on a committee if the returned amount is not paid in full within 30 days of notice from Accounting. If a gold badge and/or parking permits for the upcoming Show have already been provided, those items are confiscated.
- A member who submits a check with insufficient funds for other obligations is deemed ineligible to serve on a committee if the returned amount is not paid in full within 90 days of notice from Accounting.

## Membership types and dues

There are three kinds of membership. The membership year runs May 1 to April 30.

- **Annual (May 1 to April 30).** Available to any individual 21 years of age and older. Cost: $50. Expires yearly on April 30. Renew through the member's online account.
- **Junior Annual (May 1 to April 30).** Available to any individual under the age of 21. Cost: $25. Expires yearly on April 30. Renew through the member's online account.
- **Life.** Available to any individual for a one-time $500 payment. No subsequent Show membership purchases are required for the rest of the member's life.

If an annual member has already paid the current annual membership and wants to convert to a life membership within the same May 1 to April 30 year, they can do so by purchasing a $450 life member conversion from their online account or the Membership Office. Payment is due by the end of that same Show year.

Memberships are non-transferable, non-refundable, and issued on an individual basis only. There are no provisions for company, corporate, or family memberships.

## How to pay dues

Members are encouraged to pay dues online using the Rodeo's online tools. Other options are at the Show membership windows or by mail sent directly to the Rodeo office. From September to February, membership kiosks are available at the top of the escalator in NRG Center, with computers for online payments.

## Membership benefits

Annual membership benefits:

- Official Rodeo car decal
- Subscription to the quarterly "Bowlegged H" Magazine
- Opportunity to volunteer on a committee (optional)

Life benefits (Annual Member benefits plus):

- Life Member pin
- Opportunity to purchase a Life Member plaque for $75
- Life Membership Card. Grants admission to the Livestock Shows and Horse Shows, Commercial Exhibits, Carnival, and the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest. Not included: NRG Stadium or Rodeo Uncorked! events.

Not included in Annual or Life benefits: parking, gold or silver badges, and a guaranteed spot on a volunteer committee. Volunteering on a committee is the optional benefit you pursue on top of membership.

## Membership cards

A membership card and any other benefit items are sent to new members by the Membership Department. Member cards are valid until the expiration date printed on the physical card. At expiration, the Membership Office sends a new card to the member's address on file. Cards are non-transferable and are for the exclusive use of the individual to whom they are issued.

## Joining the Souvenir Program Committee (the two steps)

Committee membership is a two-step process on top of Rodeo membership.

### Step 1: Become a member of HLSR

- Log onto www.RODEOHOUSTON.com and create your profile.
- Select Souvenir Program from the Committee drop-down.
- Under Sales Person, select the name of the Membership Committee member who is signing you up.
- Under Desired Committee, select SOUVENIR PROGRAM.
- Pay your Rodeo membership dues as soon as possible ($50).

### Step 2: Get accepted onto the committee

- Wait for the Rodeo welcome email.
- Pay your committee dues ($70) via www.RODEOHOUSTON.com.
- Pay by the committee's dues deadline (recent years have used October 1; confirm the current deadline with leadership).

Once both steps are done, you are official. Committee dues are non-refundable.

The eligibility rules for serving on any committee, including the SPC, are:

- Be a member of, and in good standing with, the Show.
- Be at least 21 years of age (the Jr. Rodeo Committee is the one exception).
- Pass a routine criminal background check from a third-party provider.
- Have no delinquent account with the Show as of the designated payment deadline of the appointment year.
- Have membership established, with payment made, prior to actual appointment. An appointment letter is not sent to non-members.
- Complete the Indemnity Agreement and member handbook acknowledgement on an annual basis.
- Complete all required training.

Committee dues must be paid prior to the first scheduled committee meeting or before beginning work on the committee, whichever comes first. A member may serve on multiple committees but may earn only one badge and one year of committee service credit per Show. You can verify service credits on the membership status page of your online account.

When you first sign up, account creation on the SPC Dashboard checks that you are on the committee roster before it lets you finish. If you are a brand-new member and cannot create a login yet, your roster data may not be updated yet. For the exact steps on that first login, see the dashboard how-to (`member-dashboard-tasks`).

## Earning your gold badge

A committeeman receives only one gold badge for committee participation, regardless of how many committees they serve on. To badge, you have to do two things: clear the prerequisites, and hit your sales minimum.

**Prerequisites before a badge is released.** Before a badge can be released:

- Show dues must be paid.
- Committee dues must be paid (committee dues payment may be mandatory before badge pickup, so check with leadership).
- A current indemnity agreement must be on file.
- The member handbook must be acknowledged.
- Legal name verification must have taken place.
- Any special committee requirements must be fulfilled, and the chairman or staff coordinator must authorize the badge.

**Sales minimum.** You earn your badge by hitting a minimum in sales:

- Rookies (anyone in their first year on the committee): $1,000 minimum.
- Veterans (anyone past their first year): $2,000 minimum.

You can work toward your minimum with both ads and donations. It is cumulative.

**Badge pickup.** Committee badges are distributed from the Membership Office on the second floor of NRG Center, or at pre-approved committee meetings. Valid photo identification must be presented at pickup, and you must sign a receipt for the badge and parking and acknowledge the benefits. A volunteer may pick up another volunteer's badge only with a completed pickup authorization form (an online account entry or the official form from the Membership Office). The designated person must provide photo ID, which is photocopied and placed on file for that Show. Pick up badges and parking passes before the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest begins, because a ticket or credential is required to access the Membership Office during that event and the Show.

**Your badge is your badge.** Committee badges are non-transferable and for the exclusive use of the individual they are issued to. Loaning, giving, or selling badges is strictly prohibited and is grounds for forfeiture of the badge and/or parking permit, and may result in removal from committee service. Committee badges are not issued to or worn by individuals under 21 (the one exception is Gold Jr. Rodeo Committee badges, for members ages 17 to 20).

**Lost or stolen badges and parking permits.** These may be replaced by submitting a request through a committee designee, who submits an official replacement request through Chairman Tools. It must be approved by the current chairman, officer-in-charge, staff coordinator, management coordinator, membership office, and Show president. The Show is not obligated to replace lost or stolen items. If a replacement is granted, a fee of $50 is charged per item, picked up at the Membership Office.

## Gold vs silver badge access

Neither badge provides parking, admission to restricted areas, or seating in handicap areas even if unoccupied. Neither qualifies the holder for special discounts from commercial exhibit vendors. The Show may modify badge access to meet health and safety guidelines.

**Gold badge.** Admits the badge holder and one guest to three things:

- The NRG Stadium main concourse for each rodeo/concert performance (March 2 to March 21, 2027). Seating is not guaranteed. Gold badge holders and their guest must leave a seat when the ticket holder arrives, and refusal is grounds for forfeiture and possible removal.
- The World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest (Feb. 26 to Feb. 28, 2027).
- The livestock show, carnival, horse show, and commercial exhibits areas.

The gold badge excludes Rodeo Uncorked! events and any other invitation-only or ticket-only events, and does not provide parking. Gold committeeman badges are not issued to or worn by individuals under 21 (gold guest badges have no minimum age, and gold JRC badges go only to Jr. Rodeo Committee members ages 17 to 20).

**Silver badge.** Admits the badge holder and one guest to the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest, the livestock show, carnival, horse show, and commercial exhibits areas. There is no minimum age requirement. The silver badge excludes admittance into NRG Stadium for rodeo/concert performances, Rodeo Uncorked! events, and any other invitation-only or ticket-only events, and does not provide parking.

## Parking and volunteer tags

Always keep your parking pass visible, and when on a committee assignment keep the working volunteer day tag visible. Parking lot directions are online at rodeohouston.com.

Volunteer parking tags (M and LC tags) are provided for Show events and committee meetings. M and LC tags are not valid for other events at NRG Park, including the Nutcracker Market. You do not get free admission to other events at NRG Park. When attending those, be prepared to purchase both parking and an admission ticket.

## Roster and hierarchy management

The chairman and the designees the chairman selects are given access to online features. HLSR's Privacy Policy prohibits using volunteer personal contact information from committee rosters for any outside solicitations.

### Assigning designees (chairman access only)

1. Log into your online account.
2. Select the Committee Permissions tab.
3. To add a member, select their role from the drop-down box, enter their member number, then click the add button.
4. Click the save button.

The designee has access to all responsibilities of the chairman except the permission table. To remove a member, click the delete button next to that person's name.

### Submitting a roster change

Roster changes are submitted through the Show's online system. The chairman and designees have access.

- Click Menu, and under the Chairman Tools section of the side menu, click Roster Request.
- In the Request Type box, select Addition or Title Change, Removal, or Removal-Leadership Recommendation.
- If Addition is selected, a second box offers a drop-down of the committee's volunteer request list. New members must be selected from this list.
- Click the Add button and a selection box appears for subcommittee and title assignments.
- Informative red text error messages may appear if the change cannot be processed. Click Save to continue later, or Submit when all adjustments are recorded.
- If a title change or removal is selected, a second box appears to enter the member number of the current member.
- The Reason field is mandatory for all removals.
- All removals must be on a separate roster request. Removals cannot be on the same request as additions or title changes.
- When possible, submit as many names as possible on a single request (maximum of 50), which is faster than requests with one or two names.

All roster changes, including additions, must be approved by the staff coordinator. Removals must note why the person left. Appointment and removal notices are automatically emailed to the email on file.

### Hierarchy change request

Hierarchy changes are used when the structure (titles) or size of a committee is to be altered. Submit through the online system:

- Select the Hierarchy Request tab.
- Review current maximums in the left-hand column. Enter the new maximums in the right-hand column.
- Enter a brief reason in the comment field.
- Hit the submit button.

The chairman should consult the staff coordinator, management coordinator, and officer-in-charge before submitting. The approval path is officer-in-charge, then management coordinator, then chief volunteer officer, then president. Each person in the chain receives an email with a link to the request form.

### Committee size

Each committee has a maximum number of members. Any deviation above the maximum must be approved by the president. Lifetime committeemen and lifetime vice presidents do not count toward the maximum.

### Committee hierarchy (titles)

Standard committee hierarchy, top to bottom:

- Officer-in-Charge
- Chairman
- Vice Chairman
- Captain
- Assistant Captain
- Committee Member

Additional positions include division chairman (restricted to 300-plus member committees), division vice chairman (restricted to 500-plus member committees), coordinator, past committee chairman, and lifetime committeeman.

## FAQ

**How much does HLSR membership cost, and what are the types?**
Three kinds. Annual is $50 (ages 21 and up). Junior Annual is $25 (under 21). Both run May 1 to April 30 and renew through your online account. Life is a one-time $500 payment, good for the rest of your life. If you already paid an annual membership this year and want to convert to Life within the same May 1 to April 30 year, you can buy a $450 conversion from your online account or the Membership Office.

**How do I join the Souvenir Program Committee?**
Two steps. First, create your profile at www.RODEOHOUSTON.com, select Souvenir Program from the committee drop-down, pick your Membership Committee sales person, choose SOUVENIR PROGRAM as your desired committee, and pay the $50 Rodeo membership dues. Second, wait for the Rodeo welcome email and pay your $70 committee dues. Pay by the committee's dues deadline (recent years have used October 1; confirm the current date with leadership).

**What do I have to do to serve on the committee?**
Be a member in good standing, be at least 21, pass a third-party criminal background check, have no delinquent account with the Show, have your membership paid before appointment, and complete the Indemnity Agreement, the member handbook acknowledgement, and all required training each year. Committee dues must be paid before your first committee meeting or before you start work, whichever comes first.

**How much do I have to sell to earn my badge?**
Rookies (first-year members) need $1,000 minimum and veterans need $2,000 minimum. You can mix ads and donations, and it is cumulative. On top of the sales minimum, before a badge is released you need your Show dues paid, committee dues paid, a current indemnity agreement on file, the member handbook acknowledged, and legal name verification done.

**What do my gold and silver badges actually get me into?**
A gold badge admits you and one guest to the NRG Stadium main concourse for each rodeo/concert performance (March 2 to March 21, 2027, no guaranteed seating), the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest (Feb. 26 to 28, 2027), and the livestock show, carnival, horse show, and commercial exhibits. A silver badge admits you and one guest to everything except the NRG Stadium performances. Neither badge includes parking, Rodeo Uncorked! events, or any invitation-only or ticket-only event, and neither gets you commercial exhibit vendor discounts.

**Where do I pick up my badge, and what has to be done first?**
At the Membership Office on the second floor of NRG Center, or at a pre-approved committee meeting. Before a badge is released you need Show dues paid, a current indemnity agreement on file, the member handbook acknowledged, and legal name verification done. Committee dues may also be required first (check with leadership). Bring valid photo ID and sign for the badge and parking.

**My badge or parking permit was lost or stolen. What now?**
Contact your committee's designee, who submits an official replacement request through Chairman Tools. It must be approved by the chairman, officer-in-charge, staff coordinator, management coordinator, membership office, and Show president. The Show is not obligated to replace it, but if a replacement is granted there is a $50 fee per item, picked up at the Membership Office.

**Does membership by itself get me parking or a badge or a committee spot?**
No. Parking, gold or silver badges, and a guaranteed spot on a volunteer committee are not included in Annual or Life benefits. Volunteering on a committee is an optional benefit you have the opportunity to pursue.

**When are the Show offices closed?**
On the standard holidays. For 2026 the closures are New Year's (Jan. 1 to 2), Good Friday (Apr. 3), Rodeo Days (May 4 to 8), Memorial Day (May 25), Juneteenth (Jun. 19), Independence Day (July 3), Labor Day (Sept. 7), Thanksgiving (Nov. 26 to 27), Christmas (Dec. 23 to 25), and New Year's Eve (Dec. 31).
