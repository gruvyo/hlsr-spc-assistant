# Volunteer Handbook: Donor program

**Quick answer:** The Show's Donor Program thanks donors who give to scholarships, Show events, and the Educational Fund. Donations are philanthropic and carry no marketing rights. Badges start at a $2,000 donation. Silver badges scale one per $2,000 step up to four at $8,000 to $9,999, and gold badges start at $10,000 with one more per $10,000 increment up to twelve. To get Show benefits a donor must use a Show approved form, donate directly to the Show, and pay in full before benefits are distributed.

## What this is

This is the donor program section of the 2026 SPC Volunteer Handbook. It sets out the Show-wide rules every committee follows for donations, donor recognition, and the gold and silver badges. It is the Show's policy framework. For the specific SPC benefit packages by donation level (and their fair market values), see the separate Donor Benefits reference.

## Donor program basics

The Donor Program is designed to thank donors for their generosity. It includes companies, individuals, and foundations that donate for scholarships and Show events, as well as those who donate to the Educational Fund.

Key rules:

- Benefits are designed to be comparable across all programs, regardless of the donor category.
- Donations are philanthropic contributions. They result in donor benefits but do not provide any marketing rights. Marketing rights include, but are not limited to, the use of any Show Marks, co-branding, conducting register-to-win contests, distributing branded merchandise or handouts, or any other endeavor that can be construed as marketing, advertising, or promotion.
- Benefit schedules are established for each donor category and are available through your staff coordinator.
- Specific silver and gold badge allotments are set for various donation levels pertaining to Show programs, events, and/or exhibitor prizes, awards, and premiums (see the gold and silver badge policy below).
- All donations must be paid in full prior to benefit distribution.
- The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo is a 501(c)(3) charitable organization. The amount of the donation that may be deductible for federal tax purposes is the excess value donated by the donor over the value of goods or services received. Consult with your tax professional for more information.
- Payments for donations from foundations or charities are reviewed individually, and all benefit acceptances are determined by individual foundation policies. Review your foundation policy before accepting any donor benefits.

## Donor categories and opportunities

### Scholarship and Educational Fund donors

- Scholarship Donors contribute $10,000 or more, 100 percent of which goes directly to the Educational Fund to support the scholarship program.
- Educational Fund donors contribute less than $10,000 directly to the Educational Fund.

### Show Donors

- These donors give donations to approved Show programs or Show events (other than the Educational Fund).
- 100 percent of the donations are directed to specific Show programs or other designated funds to offset the spending of the program or event.

### In-Kind donations

- In-kind donations are goods or services provided (no cash is given).
- Any in-kind donation must be approved in advance by the Show and must qualify as goods or services that the Show would normally purchase in a given fiscal year under operating or capital budgeted expenditures.
- In-kind donations must provide appropriate documentation to support the value of the donation claimed on the In-Kind Donor Form. Such documentation includes, but is not limited to, invoices, inventory papers, price lists, and register-produced receipts.
- In-kind donations to committee events, such as silent auctions, goodie bags, and door prizes, are not eligible for benefits.

## Donor recognition

Committees are encouraged to recognize donors who contribute to eligible educational programs or events. Donors may be recognized on donor boards and event programs. Guidelines:

- "Donor" is an acceptable acknowledgment title on printed and electronic materials.
- No company logos, including fonts or corporate colors, may be used, UNLESS the company is an official Show sponsor.
- As a general rule, donor boards are permitted to acknowledge names (not logos) but may not be placed in view of the general public at Show events. The staff coordinator will communicate with the manager, donor relations, and fulfillment to determine signage requirements and locations.

### Design

- Lettering on donor boards must be no larger than six inches and must be in approved Show colors.
- Company logos are prohibited UNLESS the company is an official Show sponsor. Refer to the most recent official Show sponsor List, or contact the Sponsorships and Development Department.

### Placement

- Donor boards must be displayed where only contestants, committee members, and Show officials may view them. For example, a donor board could be displayed in the area of the Horspitality Office, inside a Show superintendent's office, or inside a meeting room during an auction breakfast.
- Donor boards may not be displayed to the general public at events such as the Downtown Rodeo Parade, Rodeo Roundup, and so on. The only exception is the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest, where names (no logos) of team donors may be displayed outside teams' tents.
- Within Reliant Park, donor boards cannot be positioned on or near areas that are subject to news media cameras (for example, on or near the auction sales arena).
- Donor boards may be displayed to the attending public at "off-site" activities (such as Go Texan fundraising events).

### Presentation items, awards, and trophies

- Donors of presentation items, awards, or trophies (such as Agricultural Mechanics Contest award donors) may be acknowledged in Show catalogs and on tags or placards (not to exceed 2 inches. No company logos will be allowed UNLESS the company is an Official Show Sponsor) affixed to the donated item.
- Items may be displayed to the general public at Reliant Park, provided that the items and associated recognition placards are displayed in association with, and only for the duration of, the actual event.
- Donated equipment and vehicles utilized by the Show may display commercial signage (signage that normally would be on the vehicle year-round) that displays the phone number, website, and/or address of the donor company. Such signage should "appear" to be permanently affixed to the donated equipment (paint, decal, magnetic, or vinyl). In addition, "Show-provided" donor placards (not to exceed 2 inches. No company logos will be allowed UNLESS the company is an Official Show Sponsor) may be used.

## Committee solicitation guidelines

### Prizes, awards, and premiums

- Money solicited from corporations, businesses, or individuals to be used for event donations and/or exhibitor prizes, awards, and premiums must be used solely for these purposes.
- Funds that are raised for exhibitor prizes and awards must be deposited in the awards account (4000 account).
- These funds may not be used to buy awards, prizes, or "tokens of appreciation" for contributors, Show committee members, or judges.

Other accounts where funds may be solicited:

- 5000 accounts: Funds solicited for use outside the committee's purpose or goals.
- 6000 accounts: Committee funds derived from dues or solicited from committee members for purposes other than awards. Contributions can come only from committee members and are not tax-deductible.

### Food and hospitality

Committees may have food and other items donated for their hospitality rooms, receptions, and parties. Keep in mind there may be category exclusivity agreements with Show sponsors.

- These donations are treated as committee donations only.
- These donations are not eligible to receive benefits.
- These funds, whether cash or in-kind, must be deposited or recorded in the committee operating accounts (6000 accounts).

## Gold and silver badges

Badges are issued for donations of at least $2,000 for approved Show events and/or exhibitor prizes, awards, and premiums.

All donations must be monetary, except for those obtained by the Equipment Acquisition Committee or for donated items that the Show would otherwise purchase from the current Show year budget.

Any "in-kind" item, such as donated equipment, services, or other goods or services donations, must be approved by the Show before applicable benefits can be determined. All in-kind donations require verification from the donor that proves the value of the donation. Donations will not be eligible for benefits if the donor cannot provide documentation to substantiate the claim.

### Gold badge

Gold badges are distributed to:

- Committee volunteers.
- Scholarship and Educational Fund donors.
- Show Donors and official Show sponsors (for donations of $10,000 or more to the Educational Fund or an approved Show program).
- Auction Donors. The donation total is calculated based on cumulative auction donations at any or all six auctions, including auction-day purchases, pre-sales, and cash contributions. Auction badges are based on prior-year spend.
- In-Kind Donors (for Show approved goods or services valued at $10,000 or more).

Gold badge distribution guidelines:

- A donor is eligible to receive one gold badge beginning at the $10,000 donation level. An additional gold badge may be received for each $10,000 increment, with a limit of 12 gold badges. For example, a donation of $35,000 would receive three gold badges.
- A gold badge allows entry for two into the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest, the Reliant Park grounds, and the Reliant Stadium main concourse for most rodeo and concert performances, unless stated otherwise. No seat is guaranteed, and badge holders must leave seats when ticket holders arrive.
- A gold badge allows entrance to the Committeeman's Club.
- Gold badges do not allow entry into Rodeo Uncorked! events, concert-only events, or other invitation-only or ticketed events.
- A gold badge does not guarantee or provide parking.
- A gold badge does not qualify the holder to receive special discounts from commercial exhibit vendors during the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo.
- A gold badge allows free access to the Show's Rodeo Express parking lots, plus a free ride for the badge holder and one guest. This does not include METRO Park & Ride lots, METRO buses, or the METRORail.

### Silver badge

Silver badges are distributed to:

- Scholarship and Educational Fund donors.
- Show Donors and other donors contributing to approved Show programs.
- Champion Lot Donors (excluding buying groups) in the six auctions, who also receive silver champion lot donor badges. These donors receive benefits in the following Show year.
- In-Kind Donors (for Show approved goods and services valued between $2,000 and $9,999.99).

Silver badge distribution guidelines:

- One silver badge is given for a donation from $2,000 to $3,999.
- Two silver badges are given for donations between $4,000 and $5,999.
- Three silver badges are given for a $6,000 to $7,999 donation.
- Four silver badges are given for a $8,000 to $9,999 donation.
- A silver badge allows entry for two into the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest and the Reliant Park grounds for all days of the Show. It does not allow entry into Reliant Stadium for RODEOHOUSTON or concerts, Rodeo Uncorked! events, or other invitation-only or ticketed events.
- A silver badge does not guarantee or provide parking.
- A silver badge does not qualify the holder to receive special discounts from commercial exhibit vendors during the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo.

## How to make a donation count for benefits

To receive Show benefits, donors must use a Show approved form and donate directly to the Show. Contact your staff coordinator for the appropriate forms. Donations to fundraising events are not eligible for Show benefits, and donors accept the benefits included at the fundraising event.

## FAQ

**What's the minimum donation to earn a badge?**
$2,000. A donation of $2,000 to $3,999 earns one silver badge. Badges are issued for donations of at least $2,000 for approved Show events and/or exhibitor prizes, awards, and premiums.

**How many silver badges does my donation get?**
One silver badge for $2,000 to $3,999, two for $4,000 to $5,999, three for $6,000 to $7,999, and four for $8,000 to $9,999.

**When do I get a gold badge, and how many?**
Gold badges start at the $10,000 level. You get one gold badge at $10,000, plus one more for each additional $10,000 increment, up to a maximum of 12. So a $35,000 donation earns three gold badges.

**What's the difference between a gold and a silver badge for getting into events?**
Both let two people into the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest and the Reliant Park grounds. A gold badge also gets you into the Reliant Stadium main concourse for most rodeo and concert performances (no guaranteed seat) and into the Committeeman's Club. A silver badge does not allow entry into Reliant Stadium for RODEOHOUSTON or concerts. Neither badge allows entry into Rodeo Uncorked! events or other invitation-only or ticketed events, and neither provides parking.

**Is my donation tax-deductible?**
The Show is a 501(c)(3) charitable organization. The amount that may be deductible for federal tax purposes is the excess value you donate over the value of the goods or services you receive. Consult your tax professional.

**Can a donor's company logo go on a donor board?**
No, unless the company is an official Show sponsor. Donor boards may acknowledge names, not logos. Lettering must be no larger than six inches and in approved Show colors. Boards generally may not be placed in public view at Show events. The one public exception is the World's Championship Bar-B-Que Contest, where team donor names (no logos) may be displayed outside the tents.

**Are in-kind donations eligible for benefits?**
Only if the Show approves them in advance and the donor documents the value (invoices, inventory papers, price lists, register receipts). In-kind goods or services valued from $2,000 to $9,999.99 earn silver badges, and those valued at $10,000 or more earn gold badges. In-kind donations to committee events like silent auctions, goodie bags, and door prizes are not eligible for benefits.

**What account does fundraising money go into?**
Money for exhibitor prizes and awards goes in the awards account (4000 account). The 5000 accounts are for funds used outside the committee's purpose. The 6000 accounts are committee funds from dues or member solicitations for non-award purposes, which can come only from committee members and are not tax-deductible. Donated food and hospitality items go in the 6000 operating accounts and are not eligible for benefits.
