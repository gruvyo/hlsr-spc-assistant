# Sales Tip #2: Converting a Suspect to a Prospect

**Quick answer:** Turn a suspect (a name on your list) into a qualified prospect in three steps. Confirm they aren't already a Souvenir Program advertiser or on the Protected List, gather their company details, then check last year's program to see if their competitors advertised.

## What this is

The second in the Rookie sales tip series for SPC members selling advertising. It assumes you already built a suspect list of 20 to 30 names from Tip #1.

## The three steps

**Step 1: Make sure the suspect isn't already an advertiser.** Check the name against the index in last year's Souvenir Program and against the Protected List. If they're already there, they can't be your prospect, so cross them off. You can pick up hard copies of the Protected List and last year's program at Rookie Orientation and at the next General Committee meeting.

**Step 2: Gather data to qualify the prospect.** For each remaining suspect, collect their name, title, company name, company address, phone, fax, website, and nature of business. Visiting the company website helps you understand the business and how established it is. A qualified prospect is a decision maker at a company that has been around long enough to have a website and become successful. A 3x5 card or a spreadsheet row per prospect helps when you start making calls.

**Step 3: Check the competition.** Review last year's program to see whether the prospect's competitors advertised, or whether the prospect would have exclusive space in their category. Both are good selling points. Others advertising is social proof. Being first and only in a category is a strong motivator. Note the specifics on your card for the call.

## Key points

- A suspect already on the Protected List or in last year's program can't become a prospect.
- Hard copies of the Protected List and last year's program are available at Rookie Orientation and General Committee.
- Keep one note card or spreadsheet row per prospect to use on the call.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between a suspect and a prospect?**
A suspect is any name on your list. A prospect is a qualified decision maker you've confirmed isn't already an advertiser and have gathered company details on.

**Where do I get the Protected List and last year's program?**
Pick up hard copies at the Rookie Orientation session and at the next General Committee meeting.

**What information should I collect about a prospect?**
Name, title, company name, address, phone, fax, website, and nature of business.
