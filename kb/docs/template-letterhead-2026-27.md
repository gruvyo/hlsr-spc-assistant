# Volunteer Letterhead template (2026-27)

**Quick answer:** This is the official HLSR Volunteer Letterhead. It's a Word template file (.dotx) with the branded header and footer already in place, so you write your letter on it. Save it to your own location first, then type your letter. Do not save over the original or move it.

## What this is

A ready-made letterhead for SPC volunteers who need to send a letter on official Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo stationery, for example a letter to a prospect, a thank-you to a donor, or a follow-up to a sponsor. The branding is already built in. You just add your own words.

The file is a Word template (.dotx), not a regular document. A template is meant to be reused. When you open it you make a fresh copy to work in, and the master stays untouched so the next person gets a clean one. That's why the instructions tell you to save your copy somewhere else and leave the original alone.

## What the letterhead looks like

The page comes pre-branded so anything you type sits inside the official look. The whole design is one full-page background picture (it lives in the header but covers the top banner and the bottom line), so it stays put behind whatever you type.

- **Header (top of the page).** A wide orange, leather-textured banner with stitching along the edges. Across it, the word "Committee" and the word "Volunteer" in a navy script font, separated by a small white longhorn emblem, sitting above "HOUSTON LIVESTOCK SHOW AND RODEO" in bold white capitals. A stitched seam line runs along the bottom of the banner.
- **Footer (bottom of the page).** The Show's four pillars in navy capitals on white, separated by small orange stars: "AGRICULTURE * EDUCATION * ENTERTAINMENT * WESTERN HERITAGE".
- **Body (the middle).** Blank space for your letter. The template ships with placeholder text you replace:

  > To Whom it May Concern,
  >
  > This is a template document. Please save this to another location on your Sharepoint for further use. Do not save over or move this document.
  >
  > Thank you,
  > The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo

That placeholder text is just instructions to you. Delete it and write your own letter (your greeting, your body, your sign-off) in its place.

## How to use it

1. Open the Volunteer Letterhead from the Dashboard (Links widget, Forms, handbooks & maps section).
2. Save a copy to your own location first. The note inside the file says to save it to another location on your SharePoint for further use.
3. Do not save over the original and do not move it. Leave the master template where it is so it stays available for everyone.
4. In your copy, replace the placeholder text with your actual letter. Keep the branded header and footer.
5. Print or send your letter as usual.

## FAQ

**Where do I find the official letterhead?**
On the SPC Dashboard. Open the Links widget and look in the Forms, handbooks & maps section for "Volunteer Letterhead." It downloads as a Word template (.dotx).

**Why does it say "do not save over or move this document"?**
Because it's a shared master template. If you save your letter over it, or move it, the next volunteer loses the clean copy. Always save your own copy to a different location first, then write your letter in that copy.

**What's a .dotx file and why isn't it a normal Word doc?**
A .dotx is a Word template. It's designed to be reused. When you start from it, Word makes a new document from the template instead of editing the template itself, which keeps the original clean for the next person.

**What do I do with the "To Whom it May Concern" text already in it?**
That's placeholder text with the save instructions. Delete it and type your own letter in its place. Keep the header and footer.

**Can I use this for a donor or sponsor letter?**
Yes. It's the general-purpose official letterhead. Use it for letters to prospects, donors, and sponsors when you want HLSR branding.
