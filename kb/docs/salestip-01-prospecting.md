# Sales Tip #1: Prospecting

**Quick answer:** Every sale starts with prospecting. Build a list of 20 to 30 names by asking yourself who you'd call for everyday services and who you know with business or HLSR ties, without trying to guess whether they'll buy. Those names are your suspects, and Tip #2 turns them into prospects.

## What this is

The first in the Rookie sales tip series and the kickoff of the Badge Run, the push to grow your sales to badge and earn an award level. It's written for veterans and rookies alike.

## The right way to prospect

A common question is "Where do I start?" The answer is prospecting, but there's a right and a wrong way.

The wrong way is to ask "Who do I know who will buy an ad?" That's unanswerable. You can't know who will buy until you ask, and the question kills your creativity.

The right way is to ask yourself prompting questions and write down every name that comes to mind, without qualifying anyone yet.

## The prompting questions

Who would I call if I:

- Had a plumbing or electrical problem
- Needed a fence, pool maintenance, or landscaping
- Needed a haircut or styling
- Needed specialty groceries
- Wanted to go out to dinner
- Wanted to buy a car or truck, or needed one fixed
- Needed suits tailored, or jeans washed, starched, and pressed
- Wanted a glass or bottle of wine, or a quick beer
- Wanted to help a friend get a job
- Needed to buy a suit, dress, or other apparel
- Needed office supplies
- Wanted my car washed
- Needed supplies to hunt, fish, or golf
- Needed tack
- Wanted to add on to my home
- Needed new furniture (couch, bed, dining room, and so on)

Who do I know that:

- Owns a company
- Attends the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo at least once a year
- Used to be on an HLSR committee
- Is a family member who's expressed interest in supporting HLSR
- Is a friend who's expressed interest in supporting HLSR

## The result

Review the questions a couple of times and just write down the names. You should end up with 20 to 30 names. In sales jargon these people are called suspects. Tip #2 covers how to convert suspects into prospects.

## FAQ

**Where do I start with sales?**
With prospecting. Build your list of names first, before trying to sell anything.

**What's a suspect?**
A name on your starting list. You haven't qualified them yet. Tip #2 explains how to turn a suspect into a prospect.

**How many names should I aim for?**
About 20 to 30.

**What's the wrong way to prospect?**
Asking "Who do I know who will buy an ad?" You can't answer that until you ask them, and it limits the names you come up with.
