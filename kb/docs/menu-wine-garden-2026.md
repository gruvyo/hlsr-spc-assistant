# Champion Wine Garden menu (2026)

**Quick answer:** This is the full 2026 Champion Wine Garden menu, branded Rodeo Uncorked! and presented by Frost. Wines are grouped by category (Bubbles, Sweet Wines, Whites & Sake, Rosé, Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Reds, Malbec, Pinot Noir, Cabernet Sauvignon, and a Reserve List). Each wine shows an item number, glass and bottle prices (written glass/bottle, like 6/24 meaning $6 a glass and $24 a bottle), and any competition awards it won. There's also a non-alcoholic option and food accompaniments (cheese plate $34, charcuterie plate $40, GoVino glass $4).

## What this is

The wine list served at the Champion Wine Garden at the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo. The full title on the back is Rodeo Uncorked!, the Show's wine event, and the menu is presented by Frost. The wines come from the RODEOHOUSTON International Wine Competition, so each listing carries award icons showing what that wine won.

Prices are shown as glass/bottle. For example 6/24 means $6 per glass and $24 per bottle. Bottle-only Reserve List wines show a single price. Accompaniment prices are printed on the menu with a dollar sign, for example $4 for a GoVino glass.

## How to read the menu

- **Item number.** Every wine has a three-digit number (100 through 950) used to order it.
- **Glass/bottle price.** Two numbers separated by a slash, glass first then bottle. A single number on the Reserve List means bottle only.
- **Awards.** Icons to the right of each wine show what it won at the competition. The award key is below.

## Award key

The legend at the bottom of the menu defines the award icons. It opens with the Top Award Winner marker, then the medal and champion icons:

- **Top Award Winner** (black star icon, used with the banner-style callouts below)
- **CC** Class Champion
- **RCC** Reserve Class Champion
- **TCC** Texas Class Champion
- **RT / CC** Reserve Texas Class Champion
- **REG / CC** Regional Class Champion
- **RRCC** Reserve Regional Class Champion
- **DG** Double Gold Medal
- **G** Gold Medal
- **S** Silver Medal
- **Texas Wine** (icon marking a Texas wine)
- **FR** Feature Region Wine

Some wines also carry a banner-style top-award callout. Those appear on the menu as Top Texas Winery, Top White Wine, Top Region Wine Company, Reserve Grand Champion Best of Show, Top Value Wine, Top All-Around Winery, Top Sparkling Wine, Top Region Wine, Top Red Wine, and Grand Champion Best of Show.

## FAQ

**How are the prices shown on the menu?**
As glass/bottle, glass price first. So 6/24 means $6 a glass and $24 a bottle. Reserve List wines (items 900 to 930) are bottle only and show a single price.

**What do the icons next to each wine mean?**
They're competition awards from the RODEOHOUSTON International Wine Competition. The key: CC is Class Champion, RCC is Reserve Class Champion, TCC is Texas Class Champion, RT/CC is Reserve Texas Class Champion, REG/CC is Regional Class Champion, RRCC is Reserve Regional Class Champion, DG is Double Gold Medal, G is Gold Medal, S is Silver Medal, FR is Feature Region Wine, and a separate icon marks a Texas wine.

**Which wine is the Grand Champion Best of Show?**
Item 930, Chimney Rock Cabernet Sauvignon, Tomahawk Vineyard, Stags Leap District, 2022. It's on the Reserve List at $150 a bottle. The Reserve Grand Champion Best of Show is item 280, Sequoia Grove Chardonnay, Napa Valley, 2023, at $12 a glass or $54 a bottle.

**What food can I get at the Wine Garden?**
A Cheese Plate for $34 (item 715) and a Charcuterie Plate for $40 (item 720). There's also a GoVino Glass for $4 (item 735).

**Are there non-alcoholic options?**
Yes. A dealcoholized wine (item 950, Giesen Dealcoholized Sauvignon Blanc, New Zealand, $8 a glass or $30 a bottle), plus bottled Dasani water ($4), Topo Chico Lime ($6), and Minute Maid Orange Juice ($4).

**Is the menu sponsored?**
Yes. The Champion Wine Garden is branded Rodeo Uncorked! and is presented by Frost.

**Is there an easier way to browse the wines?**
Download the RODEOHOUSTON Mobile App for an interactive version of the menu.
